## Business Overview

Airbnb (NASDAQ: ABNB) connects ~5M hosts with ~150M guest accounts in 220+ countries. Revenue is a take-rate (~14–15%) on Gross Booking Value. The model is capital-light by construction: no inventory, no real estate, no hotel staff. **FY2025 scale**: $12.2B revenue (+12%), ~$83B GBV, $4.6B FCF (38% margin), $2.5B GAAP net income, 121.9M nights & seats booked in Q4 alone (+10%), ADR $168 (+6%) ([Q4 2025 Letter](https://s26.q4cdn.com/656283129/files/doc_financials/2025/q4/Airbnb_Q4-2025-Shareholder-Letter.pdf)).

**Strategic refresh (May 2025)**: Brian Chesky's "Airbnb 2.0" relaunched **Experiences** at scale and launched **Services** (photographers, chefs, fitness, beauty), framed internally as **"Project Hawaii."** Both are early-stage; revenue contribution is small in 2025 but represents long-dated optionality. Q4 2025 also surfaced **Reserve Now, Pay Later** as a meaningful pricing innovation.

**Per FY2024 10-K**: 61% of revenue is international; ~8,200 employees; ~13,000 third-party support workers; 20+ local currencies; Q3 is the seasonal peak ([10-K FY2024](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1559720/000155972025000010/abnb-20241231.htm)).

## Competitive Moat — Wide

Airbnb captured the alternative-accommodations category and became the verb. Per [Skift](https://skift.com/2025/03/14/short-term-rentals-airbnbs-dominance-and-bookings-gains-in-1-chart/), Airbnb's market share in the $183B short-term-rental category moved from 28% (2019) to 44% (2024). The Big Three (Airbnb / Booking.com / Vrbo) collectively hold ~71%.

| Competitor | Approx. STR Share (2024) | Read |
|---|---|---|
| **Airbnb (ABNB)** | ~44% (+16pts vs. 2019) | Category-verb brand; ~90% direct traffic; only scaled global pure-play |
| **Booking.com (BKNG)** | ~18% (+4pts vs. 2019) | Real and rising threat; $7B/yr marketing engine; alt-accom now 36% of bookings |
| **Vrbo (EXPE)** | ~9% (–2pts vs. 2019) | **Losing share**; family/whole-home niche |
| **Trip.com (TCOM)** | small ex-Asia | Dominant in China; APAC competitor |
| **Hotel chains** | substitute, not direct | Substitute for shorter / business trips |

**How Airbnb's moat differs (with evidence)**:
1. **Brand-verb status drives direct traffic** (~90% direct vs. BKNG's heavy performance-marketing). ABNB sales-and-marketing ~17% of revenue vs. BKNG ~30%+ — a ~13pt structural pricing-power gap.
2. **Pure two-sided focus on alt-accom**, not hotels-plus-alt — Vrbo's –2pt share loss confirms scale economics favor a single dominant pure-play.
3. **+16pt share gain 2019→2024** while category TAM expanded — the rare case of share gain *within* a structurally growing pie.
4. **Tail risk honest**: BKNG is the well-capitalized peer currently gaining; if alt-accom matures to a duopoly, BKNG is the credible #2.
