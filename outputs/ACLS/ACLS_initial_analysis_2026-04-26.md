## Business Overview

Axcelis (NASDAQ: ACLS) designs, manufactures, and services **ion-implantation systems** — capital equipment that injects dopant atoms (boron, phosphorus, arsenic) into silicon wafers to alter electrical properties. Ion implantation is a **non-substitutable** step in front-end semi fabrication.

- **Product platform**: Purion family — high-current, medium-current, and high-energy implant systems
- **End markets**: Power semiconductors (SiC), memory (DRAM, HBM), general mature-node logic
- **Revenue mix**: Systems ~70% (cyclical, large-ticket capex); CS&I (Customer Service & Innovation) ~30% (recurring, cycle-stabilizer)
- **Scale (FY2025)**: $839M revenue (–17.6% YoY off the $1,130.6M FY2023 record); $4.4B mkt cap; 30.7M shares; net cash positive

**Strategic transformation under way**: On **October 1, 2025**, ACLS and **Veeco Instruments (VECO)** announced an all-stock merger ([Axcelis IR](https://investor.axcelis.com/news-releases/news-release-details/axcelis-technologies-and-veeco-instruments-combine-creating)) — 0.3575 ACLS/VECO, combined EV ~$4.4B, ACLS holders ~58% post-close. Stockholders of both approved in **February 2026** ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/axcelis-stockholders-approve-merger-with-veeco-302680838.html)). Pro-forma FY2024: **$1.7B revenue, ~44% non-GAAP GM, $387M adj EBITDA** — combined-co becomes the **fourth-largest US WFE supplier**. Russell Low (current ACLS CEO) leads the combined company.

## Competitive Moat — Narrow-but-Real

The implant TAM (~$2.5–3B) is structurally a duopoly: **ACLS + AMAT** account for the substantial majority. Within that duopoly, ACLS has carved out specialty leadership in SiC and high-energy implant. The Veeco merger expands the combined-co competitive set into broader WFE.

| Peer | Niche | Approx FY25 Rev | Read on Threat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ACLS** | Implant specialist | $839M | 70–80% SiC share; high-energy implant edge |
| **AMAT** | Broad WFE; implant one line | ~$28B | Direct implant competitor; could subsidize pricing — has not displaced ACLS in SiC despite years of attempts |
| **LRCX / KLAC / ASML / TEL** | Etch / metrology / litho / coater | $11–31B | Different process steps; complementary, not direct |
| **Sumitomo / ULVAC** | Implant remainders | small | Marginal share; no sustained challenge |
| **VECO** (soon-combined) | MOCVD / laser anneal / IBD | ~$700M | Pre-merger partial overlap; complementary post-close |

**How ACLS's moat differs (with evidence)**:
1. **vs AMAT**: ACLS has *protected* SiC implant share for over a decade — 70–80% SiC share is the evidence. Differentiator is **focus + customer qualification depth**: SiC implant requires high-energy capability that AMAT's mainline tool was not optimized for. AMAT has tried to enter SiC implant directly and has not yet shifted share materially.
2. **CS&I cycle stabilizer working as designed**: CS&I share rose meaningfully in FY2025 because systems revenue dropped while CS&I grew — record full-year CS&I revenue at trough is the validating data point.
3. **Tail risk**: AMAT remains the most credible long-term threat. If AMAT decides to materially invest in winning SiC implant share, ACLS pricing power could compress. Q1 2026 commentary on competitive dynamics is worth tracking.
