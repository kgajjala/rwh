## Business Overview

Adobe (NASDAQ: ADBE) operates two reportable segments:
- **Digital Media** — Creative Cloud (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere, Lightroom, InDesign), Document Cloud (Acrobat, Sign, AI Assistant), AI-first products (Firefly, Express, GenStudio)
- **Digital Experience** — Adobe Experience Cloud enterprise marketing stack (Analytics, Real-Time CDP, Journey Optimizer, Marketo, Workfront, Commerce)

**Scale (FY2025, fiscal year ended Nov 28, 2025)**:
- $23.77B revenue (+11% YoY); $25.20B total ARR; $19.20B Digital Media ARR
- ~46% non-GAAP operating margin; $10.03B operating cash flow
- 850M+ MAU across Adobe properties; 2B+ Acrobat installs; ~99% subscription
- ([FY2025 release](https://www.adobe.com/cc-shared/assets/investor-relations/pdfs/adbe-10k-fy25-final.pdf) | [Q1 FY2026 release](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260312749997/en/Adobe-Delivers-Record-Q1-Results))

**The bear narrative**: AI-natives (Figma, Canva, Midjourney, Runway, Sora) erode the creative-pro moat; per-seat subscription compresses as generative AI commoditizes parts of the workflow.

**The bull narrative**: Adobe's installed base + enterprise distribution + Firefly/Express AI roadmap monetize AI faster than peers; the ~42% drawdown prices catastrophe rather than an 11–12% top-line, mid-40s margin business — and management's April 2026 $25B buyback authorization is a binary signal that the board agrees the stock is mispriced.

## Competitive Moat — Wide → Narrowing

| Competitor | Surface | Scale | Threat Read |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Figma** (public, July 2025 IPO) | UI/UX design | $1.1B ARR (+38%), **40.65% design share, 13M MAU** | Owns collaborative design outright; XD is dead. IPO'd at $45B → lost 81% of peak by Jan 2026 — *AI commoditization is hitting Figma harder than Adobe so far*. |
| **Canva** (private) | SMB / non-pro creative | $3.5B revenue 2025, **260M MAU**, $42B valuation | Compresses Express + Photoshop entry pricing. 95% of Fortune 500 use Canva. Direct revenue overlap modest; brand/seat overlap material. |
| **Midjourney / OpenAI / Runway** | Generative AI image / video | Midjourney ~$300M+ ARR; Sora bundled in ChatGPT; Runway $3B val | Threaten Premiere video roadmap most acutely. *Lack* enterprise sales motion + brand-safe training (Firefly's wedge). |
| **DocuSign** | E-signature | ~$3B revenue | Adobe Sign gaining; Acrobat bundling is the wedge. |
| **Salesforce / Oracle / SAP / HubSpot** | Marketing Cloud | CRM ~$40B; HUBS ~$3B | Adobe Experience Cloud is genuinely #2/#3 in MarTech; growth held 9–10% despite this set. |

**How Adobe's moat differs (with evidence)**:
1. **Workflow lock-in across both creative AND enterprise marketing** — Figma has design but not document/marketing; Canva has SMB but not enterprise; AI-natives have models but not workflow + distribution
2. **Firefly's commercially-safe training data** (Adobe-licensed Stock content) — defensible vs. peer copyright concerns; the only enterprise-defensible generative model for commercial use
3. **Distribution scale** — 850M MAU + ELA pipeline funnels AI monetization at marginal cost
4. **Capital model** — ~46% NGAAP op margin, near-zero capex intensity, $10B+ annual op CF funds R&D + buybacks indefinitely

**Honest tail-risk read**: A well-capitalized peer that could escalate is **OpenAI / Microsoft Designer + Copilot stack** — same enterprise distribution as Adobe (via M365), AI-native, and would not need to win on creative-pro alone. Single most credible structural threat in 3–5 years.

## Management Signal

⚠️ **CEO transition announced March 12, 2026**. Shantanu Narayen — CEO since 2007, architect of the 2012–2014 boxed-software-to-subscription transition (most successful business-model transition in software history) — announced he will step down once a successor is named. Stays as Chair. Frank Calderoni (Lead Independent Director) chairs the special committee. **No timeline disclosed**.

**Track record under Narayen**: Revenue ~$3B (2007) → $23.77B (FY2025), ~7.5× compound. Market cap ~$20B → peak >$240B (2021), now ~$99B. Subscription transition canonical playbook. One miss: failed 2023 Figma acquisition ($20B walkaway, $1B break-up fee).

**Multi-year strategic-framework arc — succession + AI pivot**: FY2024 → FY2025 letter transition shifted from "Adobe Magic" (brand-led) to *"deliver offerings that advance customer creativity, marketing and productivity in the age of AI"* (technology-led). Combined with the March 2026 succession announcement, the implicit signal is that Narayen is intentionally engineering the AI-era leadership handoff. Wildcard: an external hire with AI-native pedigree would re-rate the multiple positively; an internal Digital Experience / finance promotion would extend the overhang.

**Capital allocation**:
- 💰 **April 21, 2026 — $25B share repurchase authorized through April 30, 2030**. Replaces prior authorization. **Largest buyback in Adobe's history; ~25% of market cap; executed at the lowest forward multiple in 10+ years.**
- FY2025 buyback execution: ~$9.5B+ deployed
- No dividend — pure-buyback return-of-capital model
