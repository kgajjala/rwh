## The Berry Global Merger — The Central Fact

The transformative event that defines Amcor's investment case is the April 30, 2025 completion of the Berry Global all-stock combination:

- **Structure**: Fixed ratio of 7.25 Amcor shares per Berry share; Amcor shareholders ≈63%, Berry shareholders ≈37% of combined entity
- **Scale**: Revenue base more than doubled (legacy Amcor ≈$13.6B FY2024 → combined ≈$22B run-rate); 77,000 employees, 400+ facilities, 40+ countries
- **Segments**: Reorganized into two segments — Global Flexible Packaging Solutions (Amcor Flexibles + Berry Flexibles) and Global Rigid Packaging Solutions (Amcor Rigid + Berry Consumer Packaging)
- **Synergies**: $650M pre-tax run-rate by FY2028; $260M target FY2026 (raised to $270M at Q3); $280M one-time working-capital cash benefits by FY2028
- **FCF path**: Management guides FCF exceeding $3B by FY2028 at full synergy run-rate *[Management guidance]*
- **Leverage**: Net debt stepped up to ≈$13.3B (FY2025); 3.8x adjusted EBITDA (Q3 FY2026); target 2.5–3.0x long-term via FCF generation + divestitures

**Why this matters**: The market typically discounts post-M&A integration for 12–36 months. At Q3 FY2026 (≈12 months post-close), synergy delivery is tracking ahead of target — the most critical early signal that the integration thesis is intact. The FCF cut is the one negative signal, but management explicitly frames it as a deliberate supply-chain inventory build, not an earnings shortfall.

## Business Model in One Page

Amcor is a **margin-on-conversion manufacturer**: it buys resins, films, aluminum foil, and paperboard; converts them into proprietary packaging structures (flexible pouches, rigid containers, specialty healthcare formats) at company-owned plants; and sells under multi-year supply agreements with raw-material cost pass-through clauses.

**Revenue drivers**: Volume × conversion price. Volume is tied to consumer staples demand (food, healthcare, beauty, household) — relatively defensive, 3–5% cyclical variance vs. GDP. Conversion price is the premium Amcor charges for design, engineering, barrier properties, recyclability, and shelf-life optimization.

**Profit drivers**: Fixed-cost absorption (volume is king), purchasing scale (top-5 global resin buyer), and mix uplift toward high-value healthcare/specialty formats.

**Capital structure**: Asset-intensive (400+ owned/leased manufacturing plants); capex ≈$850–900M/yr FY2026E; FCF is after-capex.

**End-market mix** (approximate): Food ≈45%; healthcare/pharmaceutical ≈15%; home & personal care / beauty ≈15%; beverage ≈10%; specialty/industrial ≈15%.

## Key Risks Summary

1. **Berry integration execution failure**: Synergy run-rate below $500M vs. $650M target → collapses FCF thesis. *Criterion d: specific large discretionary investment with uncertain outcome.*
2. **Leverage / covenant risk**: 3.8x net debt/EBITDA well above long-term 2.5–3.0x target; covenant risk if EBITDA misses while FCF is consumed by capex + integration costs.
3. **Dividend sustainability**: $1.2B annual dividend vs. $1.5–1.6B FCF (FY2026E) = 75–80% payout ratio; sustainable with FCF ramp but fragile if ramp delays.
4. **EU PPWR compliance**: PFAS food-packaging restrictions Aug 2026; PCR content mandates by 2030 — ongoing capex and reformulation required.
5. **North America Beverage divestiture risk**: If sold below 4.5x EBIT, portfolio marks down and deleveraging is slower.
6. **Short interest build**: 7.85% of float (Apr 15, 2026) with 3-consecutive-period MoM increase — elevated but not extreme.

## Structural Wrinkles to Flag

- **10-K, not 20-F**: Despite Irish incorporation, Amcor has filed Form 10-K (domestic issuer) since FY2019. No foreign-private-issuer complexity.
- **1-for-5 reverse split (Jan 15, 2026)**: All per-share data presented on post-split basis. Pre-split historical prices and EPS must be divided by 5 and multiplied by 5, respectively.
- **Fiscal year-end change**: Amcor is moving from June 30 fiscal year to December 31 calendar year, effective 2027. Stub period: July 1 – December 31, 2026. Q4 FY2026 (Aug 2026) will include stub-period guidance.
- **Pre/post-Berry comparability**: FY2025 financials include only 2 months of Berry (May–June 2025). First full-year combined reporting is FY2026.
