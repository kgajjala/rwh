## Business Overview

Amazon (NASDAQ: AMZN) is a $716.9B FY2025 revenue platform with three reportable segments: **North America retail** ($435B / $29.6B op income), **International retail** ($152B / $4.7B op income — first time profitable at scale), and **AWS** (~$130B / $45.6B op income). Op income mix differs sharply from revenue mix — AWS contributes ~57% of total op income on ~18% of revenue; advertising at ~10% of revenue likely contributes ~25% of op income.

**Business-model evolution arc**: 1P retail (1995–2005) → 3P marketplace + Prime (2005–2015) → AWS dominance (2015–2025) → AI infrastructure / vertical AI / Kuiper / autonomous (2025–2035, current bet).

## Competitive Moat — Wide and Reinforcing

Amazon competes across **four distinct competitor sets simultaneously** (retail, cloud, advertising, logistics). The cross-pillar reinforcement is the differentiator.

**Cloud (IaaS / PaaS)**:
| Peer | Share | Threat Vector |
|---|---|---|
| **AWS** | ~30% (down from 33% peak) | Service breadth, custom silicon, ecosystem |
| **Microsoft Azure** | ~25% (rising) | Copilot-pull AI demand; OpenAI partnership |
| **Google Cloud** | ~12% (rising) | Gemini AI + TPU silicon |
| **Oracle Cloud** | ~3% | Niche; OpenAI / TikTok deal beneficiary |

**Retail**:
| Peer | Scale | Threat |
|---|---|---|
| **Walmart** | ~$650B revenue, $100B+ U.S. e-commerce | Strongest direct NA threat; omnichannel + grocery |
| **Costco** | ~$260B revenue, membership warehouse | Different model; not a direct e-commerce threat |
| **Shopify** | $11.6B platform rev, $378B GMV | Indirect — counter-positions AMZN's marketplace |
| **Temu / Shein** | $50B+ U.S. GMV | Long-tail price aggregator threat |
| **MercadoLibre** | ~$20B rev, LatAm leader | International category leader; AMZN trailing in LatAm |

**Advertising**: Google ($300B+) > Meta ($170B+) > **Amazon Ads** ($60–70B, growing 20%+, first-party purchase data + closed-loop attribution unique to AMZN) > TikTok ($30B+ U.S.).

**Logistics**: **Amazon Logistics** (internal cost center at hyperscale; same-day/next-day breadth × speed unmatched) reduced reliance on FedEx (~$90B) / UPS (~$95B) over a decade.

**How Amazon's moat differs**:
1. **Cross-pillar reinforcement** — Prime data → advertising; logistics scale → retail margin; AWS profits → retail capex; Trainium silicon → lower Bedrock pricing → AI workload pull
2. **Custom silicon (Trainium2/4, Inferentia2)** — 30–40% better price-performance vs. GPU-only stacks ([FY24 letter](https://www.aboutamazon.com/news/company-news/amazon-ceo-andy-jassy-2024-letter-to-shareholders))
3. **Cost-to-serve flywheel** — first per-unit cost-to-serve reduction since 2018 (FY23) is the operating signature of the +$32B 3-year NA swing
4. **No single peer competes across more than two pillars**; Microsoft (Azure + advertising) comes closest but lacks retail/marketplace/logistics

**Honest tail-risk read**: Microsoft is well-capitalized, currently winning AI workloads on Copilot pull, and could escalate further if OpenAI continues to cluster on Azure. **AWS share trend (33% → 30%) is the cleanest real-time vulnerability indicator. Q1 FY26 AWS growth rate vs. Azure growth rate is the single most important data point on April 29.**
