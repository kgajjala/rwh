## Business Overview

Booking Holdings (NASDAQ: BKNG) is the world's largest online travel agency by gross bookings, operating six global travel platforms: **Booking.com** (accommodations, global market leader and ~80%+ of revenue), **Priceline** (U.S.-centric discount travel), **Agoda** (Asia-Pacific accommodations), **Kayak** (metasearch), **OpenTable** (restaurant reservations), and **Rentalcars.com** (car rentals). The core business connects 3M+ accommodation properties across 220+ countries with hundreds of millions of travelers, and is increasingly extending into airlines, car rentals, attractions, and restaurants through the **Connected Trip** strategy.

Revenue is generated via two models:
- **Agency** (commission paid after the stay completes): historically the entire business
- **Merchant** (BKNG collects payment from the traveler upfront, then remits to the supplier net of commission): rapidly becoming dominant — **70% of gross bookings in 2025 vs. ~25% in 2021**

FY2025 revenue was **$26.92B (+13% YoY)** with **$9.09B in FCF** at a **33.7% FCF margin** — among the highest-quality cash-conversion profiles in the S&P 500. ~90% of revenue is non-U.S. (Europe-heavy mix via Booking.com/Netherlands).

> **Does AI disintermediate Booking (travelers bypass the OTA via AI agents) — or does AI amplify Booking's value (fulfillment complexity, supplier relationships, and payment rails make Booking *more* necessary in an AI-assisted booking world)?**

Management has committed **$700M of incremental 2026 spend** into GenAI, Connected Trip, Asia, and fintech to engineer the latter outcome. The stock at –22.8% from 52-week high implies the market is pricing meaningful AI-impairment risk.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding Insight**: Travel is the world's largest discretionary-spend category, and matching 3M+ accommodation properties across 220+ countries with hundreds of millions of travelers is an impossible coordination problem without an intermediary. Booking.com (founded 1996 in Amsterdam; acquired by Priceline for $133M in 2005 in one of the all-time-great mega-cap acquisitions) became the dominant accommodation marketplace by solving the coordination problem online first and at the greatest scale.

**Strategic Vision**: Become the **Connected Trip** — the single platform where a traveler books and manages every element of a trip (accommodation, flight, car, attraction, restaurant). This reduces dependence on any single vertical, builds recurring relationships beyond a single hotel transaction, and — most importantly — creates fulfillment moat that an AI travel agent cannot replicate.

**Pivotal question** (restated): Does AI disintermediate Booking, or amplify it? Management's $700M 2026 reinvestment is the bet that AI is amplification, not impairment.

**Vulnerabilities**:
- ⚠️ **AI assistants (ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity)** becoming the discovery layer above OTAs — the existential debate in the multiple
- ⚠️ **Google Hotels** using search dominance to surface direct booking (moderated by EU/US antitrust scrutiny)
- ⚠️ **Direct hotel loyalty apps** (Marriott Bonvoy, Hilton Honors) — chains reward direct booking with points
- ⚠️ **EUR/USD reported growth volatility** — not a moat issue but an optics one

**How BKNG's moat differs and what evidence supports it**:

1. **EU + Asia geographic dominance vs. EXPE's U.S. concentration** — Booking.com's 25-year EU SEO moat is genuinely uncopiable; Agoda's Asia position gives BKNG the only credible OTA growth engine ex-China. **Evidence**: ~90% non-U.S. revenue mix; FY2024 reported growth outpaced EXPE by 400+bps despite EXPE's larger U.S. exposure benefit from a strong dollar.
2. **Marketing-efficiency advantage** — Booking.com direct-traffic share has grown 5+ years running, lowering effective CAC versus EXPE/ABNB which still pay heavy Google CPC. **Evidence**: marketing as % of revenue trending *down* across FY2022→FY2024 in 10-K MD&A even as gross bookings accelerated.
3. **Capital-return cadence vs. ABNB** — BKNG has bought back **$30B** of stock 2021–25 and initiated a dividend in FY2024; ABNB has only $4B of buybacks and no dividend. **Evidence**: 22% pre-split share-count reduction over 5 years.
4. **Connected Trip + merchant float vs. all peers** — Connected Trip transactions growing +30%+ YoY at low-double-digit % of mix; merchant model at 70% generates fintech-grade payment float. **Evidence**: Q4 2025 disclosure (68M airline tickets, +37%; attractions +80%; alt-accom >30% of room-night growth).

**Honest tail-risk read**: The competitive position vs. peers is *strengthening*, not weakening — BKNG is the share-gainer. The genuine tail risk is not from OTA peers but from the **discovery layer** above the OTAs (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT-Operator, Perplexity-style agents). If those layers monetize as direct-booking referrers to hotels rather than as OTA-routing referrers, BKNG's marketing-CAC moat erodes — this is the entirety of the AI bear case.

4. **Interest rates**: Merchant-model float (advance bookings) generates rate-sensitive interest income. 100bps rate compression ≈ ~$80–100M interest-income headwind *[Estimate]*. Higher-for-longer = silent EBITDA tailwind.
