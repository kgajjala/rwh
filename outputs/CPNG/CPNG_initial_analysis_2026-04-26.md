## Executive Summary

Coupang owns the dominant e-commerce + logistics platform in Korea (Wide moat — ~14M WOW members, ~25–30% Korean retail e-commerce share, irreplicable Rocket Delivery footprint), trading at ~0.85× FY26E EV/Revenue and ~14–16× FY26E EV/EBITDA — a meaningful discount to global peers. **But the Q4 2025 print materially weakened the near-term thesis vs. the prior v2.5 baseline:**

1. A **33M-account data incident** (former-employee illegal access) triggered a **~$1.2B customer-voucher compensation program**, collapsing Q4 2025 EBITDA to **$267M (–37% YoY)**.
2. **Korea Product Commerce constant-currency growth bottomed at +4% in January 2026** — the weakest print since IPO. Management said February showed sequential recovery, but Q1 2026 guide is just **+5–10% CC**.
3. **Developing Offerings 2026 EBITDA loss guide is $950M–$1B** — the largest deliberate annual burn in CPNG's public-company history without a defined ROI window.

The moat is intact: Q4 gross margin still expanded +85 bps YoY despite the breach charge, Taiwan posted triple-digit revenue growth with 75% next-day delivery on its own network in December 2025, Farfetch had its first positive YoY revenue growth quarter since the January 2024 acquisition close, and the company executed **5.9M shares of buyback in Q4 alone** ($243M of $1B authorization deployed) at sub-$22 — Bom Kim's most explicit intrinsic-value signal in 4 years as a public-company CEO.

**The thesis now hinges on whether January 2026 was the trough.** The May 5, 2026 Q1 print is the validating event. PW EV $26 / +26% over 3 yr is positive but not asymmetric enough to compel pre-print initiation against the binary outcome.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Was January 2026 the trough? If yes, Korea Product Commerce growth recovers from +4% to +10–12% by Q4 2026, gross margin re-expands, $1B buyback compounds the share-count tailwind, and the multiple re-rates from ~14× toward 18×. If no, the breach overhang is structural, the $950M–$1B Developing Offerings burn extends beyond 2027 without unit-economics inflection, and the depressed multiple is justified.

## Three Material Bull-Case Anchors

1. **Korea gross margin still expanded +85 bps in Q4 2025 despite the breach charge.** The structural margin engine (3P marketplace mix + advertising + payments) is operating. If voucher impact is a 1–2 quarter event, the margin trajectory resumes intact.
2. **Active $1B buyback at sub-$22 with $243M deployed in Q4 alone.** First sustained capital-return program in CPNG's history. The pace step-up through the breach window is the cleanest Bom-Kim intrinsic-value signal — IPO'd March 2021, never repurchased meaningfully until 2025.
3. **Net cash $5B+ funds the entire $950M–$1B 2026 Developing Offerings burn without external financing.** Capital structure is risk-free; the burn is a discretionary investment, not a survival expense. Taiwan + Farfetch optionality is funded for 2–3 years even at high-end loss guide.

## Three Material Bear-Case Anchors

1. **+4% Korea Product Commerce constant-currency growth in January 2026 is the weakest print since IPO.** Management's "February improving" disclosure is verbal forward guidance, not in any filing. Q1 print on May 5 is a binary validation event.
2. **$950M–$1B 2026 Developing Offerings burn is materially larger than consensus modeled.** Without Taiwan profitability or Farfetch break-even by end-2027, the framing changes from "patient builder" to "value destroyer."
3. **Korean demographic headwind compounds long-term.** Lowest fertility in the world (~0.78), population peaks ~2030. The breach is a multi-quarter overhang on top of a structural TAM cap.
