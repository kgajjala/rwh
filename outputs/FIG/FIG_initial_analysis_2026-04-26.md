## Business Overview

Figma (NYSE: **FIG**) is the leading collaborative design and prototyping platform for digital products, used by **~95% of Fortune 500 design teams**. Founded in 2012 by Dylan Field and Evan Wallace, the company built a browser-native, real-time multiplayer design tool by combining WebGL with operational-transform collaboration primitives — an architecture Adobe and Sketch could not match without ground-up rewrites.

**What Figma sells (FY2025)**:
- **Figma Design** (per-editor SaaS) — the core product, used by product designers, PMs, and engineers
- **FigJam** — collaborative whiteboarding (vs. Miro / Mural)
- **Dev Mode** — developer-handoff workflow (specs, code snippets, design tokens)
- **Figma Slides, Figma Sites, Figma Buzz, Figma Draw** — newer products launched 2024–2025 that round out a "design platform" rather than a "design tool"
- **Figma Make** (launched 2025, GA July) — AI-assisted code/design generation; **>50% of $100K+ ARR customers use weekly** by Q4 2025; ~60% of Make files created by non-designers ([Q4 2025 release](https://investor.figma.com/news-events/news/news-details/2026/Figma-Announces-Fourth-Quarter-and-Fiscal-Year-2025-Financial-Results/default.aspx))

**FY2025 scale**:
- **$1.056B revenue** (+41% YoY) — first $1B-revenue year; Q4 alone was **$303.8M (+40%)**
- **136% NDR** for $10K+ ARR cohort in Q4 (highest in 10 quarters)
- **67 customers >$1M ARR** (+68% YoY); 1,250+ customers >$100K ARR
- **88% gross margin** (FY); **14% non-GAAP operating margin** (Q4); **13% adjusted FCF margin** (Q4)
- **$1.7B cash and marketables**

**The Adobe inheritance**: A $20B Adobe acquisition agreement was abandoned 2023-12-18 under UK CMA + EU + DOJ pressure. Adobe paid Figma a **$1B termination fee** — which funded the AI build-out and the path to IPO. That outcome is now part of why Figma exists as an independent public company.

**The post-IPO drawdown**: Figma went public 2025-07-31 at $33/share, surged to a **$142.92 first-day peak** (~$55B market cap, ~52× P/S), and has since fallen to **$17.47** as of 2026-04-24 — **–88% from peak, –47% from IPO price**. Three forces compounded: (1) staggered lock-up expirations with **54.1% of shares under extended lock-ups** unwinding through August 2026, (2) AI-disruption competitive overhang (Cursor, Vercel v0, Lovable, ChatGPT Canvas, Google Stitch), (3) sentiment whiplash off an unsustainable peak. Q4 2025 fundamentals — NDR 136%, +41% revenue, 67 $1M+ customers (+68% YoY) — do not match the price action. That gap is the setup.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist?

**Founding insight (Dylan Field & Evan Wallace, 2012)**: Browser-native real-time multi-user editing of vector design files was technically possible (WebGL + operational transforms) but no incumbent (Adobe, Sketch) had the architecture to deliver it. By rebuilding the design tool around **collaboration as the primary primitive** — not file syncing or one-user-at-a-time editing — Figma made design a team sport and captured the design-team workflow.

**The collaboration thesis won decisively** for traditional design workflows. The harder forward question (Section 5) is whether design itself remains a discrete workflow as AI-driven code generation collapses the design-to-code pipeline. Figma's response is to integrate AI into the platform (Make, AI credits, Weavee acquisition) and position itself as the **collaboration surface** for AI-native creation — so far, the empirical evidence (Make weekly adoption among $100K+ customers crossing 50%) is that this is working.

## Section 5 — Competitive Moat

**Rating: Narrow but Widening.** Strong product-led moat within the traditional design-team workflow (browser-native multiplayer, dev-handoff stickiness); the *category itself* is being reshaped by AI-native code generation, and Figma's moat-extension bet is to be the **collaboration surface** that connects humans + AI agents in product creation.

### Moat sources

| Source | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Browser-native real-time multiplayer | **Strong** | Multi-year architectural lead; Adobe XD discontinued; Sketch never matched it. The original moat. |
| Enterprise penetration / network effects | **Strong** | ~95% of Fortune 500 design teams; once team files live in Figma + Dev Mode is in the engineering workflow, switching cost is high. |
| Dev Mode workflow lock-in | **Moderate-Strong** | Developer-handoff specs, code-snippets, design tokens — extends moat from designers into engineering org. |
| Brand / "design verb" | **Moderate** | "Figma" is the default tool reference among modern product teams; harder to dislodge than feature parity suggests. |
| Multi-product platform (8 products) | **Moderate (widening)** | 70%+ customers on 3+ products; cross-sell is real, but newer products are less proven than the core. |
| AI-native repositioning (Make + UCP-like surfaces) | **Moderate (developing)** | Make adoption >50% of $100K+ customers weekly by Q4 — empirical evidence the platform is *capturing* AI value. |

### Moat vulnerabilities

- **AI-native code-generation tools** (Cursor, Vercel v0, Lovable, ChatGPT Canvas, Claude Code, Google Stitch) let developers + PMs **skip the design step entirely**. This is the existential thesis-break risk — *if* engineering teams stop needing dedicated design files, the collaboration moat is on the wrong substrate.
- **Adobe is back**: Adobe Express + Firefly + Photoshop incorporate generative design surfaces; despite the abandoned $20B deal, Adobe remains a well-capitalized direct competitor.
- **Lock-up overhang** is mechanical, not fundamental — but creates persistent supply pressure through Aug 2026.

### Competitive Landscape

Direct competition spans three layers — traditional design tools, AI-native creation tools, and adjacent collaboration tools.

| Competitor | Type | Approx. Position | Threat Vector |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adobe** (XD discontinued; Express + Firefly + Photoshop) | Public, $250B+ mkt cap | Legacy design giant; XD failed to match Figma; Firefly/Express now hosting generative design | Re-entry via AI generative tools + creative cloud bundle pricing |
| **Canva** | Private, ~$32B last valuation (2024) | **Largest direct private competitor**; ~190M MAU; non-designer/marketing focus; Affinity acquisition (2024) brings pro-design closer | Non-designer / SMB creation TAM; building toward enterprise design |
| **Microsoft Designer + Loop** | Bundled in M365 | Free in Microsoft 365; integrates Copilot | Enterprise bundling pressure on per-seat pricing |
| **Sketch** | Private, niche | Legacy macOS-native design tool; market share continues to erode | Niche only — not a serious threat |
| **Penpot** | Open-source, free | Open-source Figma alternative; institutional/EU adoption | Long-tail price/lock-in pressure; not a direct enterprise threat |
| **Cursor / v0 / Lovable / Stitch (Google)** | AI-native | Code-first creation; design surface is generated on demand | **Existential** — bypasses the dedicated design-file workflow entirely if the bear case plays out |
| **Miro / Mural** | Whiteboard-collab | Direct FigJam competitors | FigJam is the response; mostly contained |

**How Figma's moat differs**: Figma's defensibility is the **combined** collaboration substrate (browser-native real-time multi-user) + **the dev-handoff workflow** + **Auto Layout / Variants tooling depth** (years of accumulated design-system primitives competitors can't easily replicate) + **the cross-functional adoption pattern** (designers + PMs + engineers all in the same file). No single competitor combines all four. Adobe has the brand and creative-cloud bundle but not the multiplayer collaboration substrate; Canva has scale but not enterprise design-system depth or dev-handoff; AI-native tools have the new-creation paradigm but no incumbent collaboration graph.

**Empirical evidence the moat is holding**: $1M+ ARR cohort +68% YoY (large enterprise customers are *deepening* commitment, not displacing); international revenue +45% (TAM is still expanding faster than category disruption); 70%+ multi-product adoption (cross-sell motion is functioning); Make >50% of $100K+ weekly (Figma is *capturing* AI value within its surface, not being routed around).

**Honest tail-risk read**: Canva is the best-capitalized adjacent threat for SMB/marketing TAM, and Google Stitch (AI-native, free, integrated with Workspace) is the most concerning for the developer-handoff segment because it is bundled and well-resourced. If 2026–2027 AI-tool adoption shows enterprise design teams routing around Figma rather than through Make, the bear case becomes the base case.

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Design-tool TAM**: Traditional collaborative-design tool market is ~$5–8B and growing low-double-digit. Figma is the dominant share-holder. AI-creation-tool TAM expansion (Make, code-generation surfaces) potentially expands category into a much larger productivity/creation TAM — but exposes Figma to substantially larger competitors (OpenAI, Microsoft, Google).

**SaaS multiple compression**: Multiples have broadly compressed since 2021. Even high-quality SaaS trades far below 2021 peak. Figma's ~6× FY26E EV/Revenue is below MNDY (~6–8×) and TEAM (~10×) despite faster growth — that gap is the asymmetry.

**AI-credit monetization regime change**: March 2026 Figma began monetizing AI credits explicitly. This is a **regime shift in unit economics** — Figma now has both a per-seat lever (traditional) and a per-AI-action lever (consumption). This could materially expand revenue per customer if Make adoption deepens.

**Lock-up dynamics (FIG-specific)**: 54.1% of shares under extended lock-ups expiring in tranches tied to Q1/Q3 2026 earnings, with **final expiration August 2026**. Until then every print is also a supply event. Post-August 2026, supply pressure structurally lifts.
