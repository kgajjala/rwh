## Why This Setup Is Asymmetric

1. **Diversification is real, not narrative**: 2021 transaction revenue was 77% of total; Q1 2026 it's 58%, with NII (34%) + Gold + Rothera providing structural ballast that did not exist in 2022 (when revenue fell 25% in a retail engagement contraction).

2. **Multiple is below historical fintech-platform norms**: ~21× FY26E adj EPS vs. peer set 20–25× and HOOD's own historical 30×+. The drawdown bakes in (a) crypto cyclicality (already evidenced Q1) and (b) PFOF tail (live but unfinalized).

3. **Insider sentiment is mixed but explicable**: Tenev's $26M Apr 6 sale was a 10b5-1 plan adopted Sept 2025; the pattern (50 sells / 0 buys over 5 yrs) is a real governance flag but is also consistent with a founder with a deep legacy stake. *Material caveat — never opportunistically bought even at 2022 sub-$10 lows.*

## Key Risks (top 3)

1. ⚠️ **Crypto revenue cyclicality** — Q1 2026 –47% YoY proved the exposure. Crypto remains 12.5% of revenue. *Partially priced in* at $84.71.
2. ⚠️ **PFOF SEC rulemaking** — Dec 2022 proposals (Reg Best Ex, retail auction) remain live; a binding rule would compress 30–50% of transaction-revenue unit economics. **Not priced in.**
3. ⚠️ **Retail engagement contraction** — 2022 saw revenue –25%. NII + Gold provide a partial floor that didn't exist then, but through-cycle adj EBITDA durability is unproven.
