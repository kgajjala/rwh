## Business Overview

Intuit (NASDAQ: INTU) is a vertical SaaS platform serving consumers, small and mid-market businesses, and accountants through four product lines:

- **TurboTax**: Consumer and assisted tax preparation; ~70% of U.S. DIY e-filed returns. The "TurboTax Live" assisted tier (human expert + AI) is the primary ARPU expansion vector.
- **QuickBooks (GBSG)**: De-facto SMB accounting standard (~34% market share); cloud engine is QuickBooks Online (QBO) with ~7.5M paying subscribers. QBO Advanced and the new Intuit Enterprise Suite (IES) target the $10M–$500M mid-market.
- **Credit Karma**: Free credit scores and financial-product marketplace; 100M+ U.S. members. Acquired 2020 for $8.1B. Re-accelerating +23% in Q2 FY26 after two years of credit-cycle suppression.
- **Mailchimp**: Email and marketing automation; acquired 2021 for $12B. The underperformer — not expected to return to double-digit growth until beyond FY2026 per management.

FY2025 revenue: **$18.83B** (+15.6% YoY). TTM revenue (Jan 2026): **$20.1B**. FY2026 guide (reaffirmed Q2): **$20.997B–$21.186B** (+12–13%).

The company has repositioned as an **"AI-driven expert platform"**: multiyear Anthropic partnership formalized; all four apps in OpenAI's directory; proprietary **Intuit Assist** agentic workflows across QBO and TurboTax with 3M+ users and 85%+ repeat engagement. The data isolation structure (customer data contractually walled from external LLMs) is management's primary moat claim against horizontal AI disruption.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**U.S. Tax Preparation TAM**: ~$15B annually; INTU captures a disproportionate share of revenue (estimated 40–50% of industry revenue) vs. share of returns (~25–30%), because TurboTax commands premium ARPU and grows its assisted tier. Total U.S. DIY e-filed returns: ~80M/year; TurboTax serves ~55–60M of those. IRS Direct File is the structural counter-trend, but its scope is constitutionally and politically bounded to the simplest return types.

**SMB Accounting/Payroll TAM**: $50B+ in the U.S.; QuickBooks dominates the sub-$10M revenue segment but is sub-5% penetrated in the $10M–$500M mid-market that IES is targeting. Mid-market is the primary long-run valuation lever.

**AI Macro Disruption**: The horizontal LLM narrative has compressed INTU from 35× to 17× non-GAAP P/E. The Q2 FY26 bull counter: SMB finance specifically requires accuracy, liability accountability, and integration with regulated government systems — all areas where general LLMs cannot currently substitute for Intuit's specialized, data-verified workflow. The bear scenario requires LLMs to become auditable and trusted for tax-outcome accuracy within a 3–5 year window.

**Credit Cycle**: Credit Karma's revenue is highly correlated with origination volumes. The FY2022–FY2024 tightening suppressed segment performance; the FY2025–FY2026 recovery is the primary segment swing factor. A recession would materially compress Credit Karma, but Intuit's core (GBSG + Consumer) has recession-resistant fundamentals (tax filing is legally mandatory).

**Regulatory Environment**: IRS Direct File expansion is a policy decision, not judicial, subject to Treasury Department priorities. A shift in Treasury posture could slow or reverse the rollout. Current trajectory (all 50 states, expanding scope) is the base case headwind. FTC advertising overhang resolved. *[stockanalysis.com](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/intu/)*

## Section 10 — BAIT Framework

### B — Behavioral: **Strong**

The –51% drawdown from $813 to $396 is the deepest peak-to-trough move in INTU's history for a non-recessionary period. The narrative (AI horizontals will commoditize tax + bookkeeping) is clean and fear-inducing. But Q2 FY26 data directly contradicts it: +17% growth while AI partnerships were being announced. The market is treating Anthropic/OpenAI partnerships as threat evidence rather than moat-building. Classic behavioral mispricing — narrative-led, not data-led.

### A — Analytical: **Strong**

At 17× non-GAAP P/E with 12–13% top-line growth, $6.1B FCF, and 80%+ gross margins, INTU is priced as a value stock despite wide-moat characteristics. IES at ~40% growth is underweighted in consensus models. Credit Karma at +23% is a $400M+ revenue swing factor above FY2024 troughs. AI-as-moat-amplifier (data wall + human-expert pairing) creates compounding upside not in base-case consensus.

### I — Informational: **Moderate**

Primary-source disclosures underappreciated by consensus: the contractual data isolation structure (Anthropic/OpenAI), the IES accountant-channel percentage (1 in 3 new deals), the FTC appeal dismissal, and the Credit Karma vertical granularity (broad-based recovery across three verticals).

### T — Technical: **Weak**

Stock sub-200-day MA, sub-50-day MA. $342 low has held in April 2026 — possible base-building. No mechanical catalyst (no index change, buyback wasn't publicly quantified until post-quarter). Beta 1.21 amplifies broad tech moves. May 21 Q3 earnings is the first event to potentially reverse the downtrend.

### BAIT Verdict

🟢 **Triple overlap (B + A Strong, I Moderate; T Weak)**. Conviction: **Moderate-High**. One of the cleanest narrative-vs-fundamentals dislocations in the SaaS complex: 51% drawdown, narrative-led, results contradict the narrative, FCF yield 5.6% on a wide-moat business, and analyst consensus targeting +77%.
