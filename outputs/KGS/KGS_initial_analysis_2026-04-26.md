## Business Overview

Kodiak Gas Services owns and operates large-horsepower compression units deployed at customer well sites, gathering systems, and midstream facilities to move natural gas across the Permian Basin and U.S. production regions. The business model is fee-based: take-or-pay contracts with investment-grade midstream and E&P customers, economics resembling a utility more than a cyclical oilfield services company.

**Two operating segments**:
- **Contract Services** (~90% of revenue): company-owned and customer-owned compression and gas treating infrastructure under fixed-revenue, multi-year contracts
- **Other Services** (~10%): station construction, compressor maintenance/overhaul, freight, crane services

**Three transformative events completed 2024–2026**:

| Event | Date | Size | Impact |
|-------|------|------|--------|
| CSI Compressco acquisition | April 1, 2024 | ~$870M | +580K HP; KGS became undisputed #1 U.S. operator; fully integrated by YE2024 |
| Mexico operations exit | Sept 30, 2025 | $34.3M proceeds | Took $33.3M loss; purified U.S. focus; 97.7% utilization is pure-U.S. |
| DPS acquisition → Kodiak Power Solutions | April 1, 2026 | ~$675M | +395 MW distributed power gen; new revenue vertical targeting AI data centers |

**EQT AB exit**: KGS's PE sponsor (formerly ~43.1% of shares) fully exited the register by December 2, 2025, removing all secondary-offering overhang.

## Section 3 — Competitive Moat & Landscape

**Rating: Narrow** — scale + large-HP specialization + asset specificity + take-or-pay contract structure in a tight 3-player U.S. oligopoly.

### Moat Sources

| Source | Strength | Evidence |

| Large-HP specialization | Strong | 970 avg HP/unit (+4.7% YoY); deepest super-large fleet (>1,500 HP) for Permian gas-lift; commands premium pricing |

### Competitive Landscape

**U.S. contract compression — the 3-player market**:

| Operator | Revenue-Gen HP | Fleet Utilization | FY2025 Revenue | FY2025 Adj EBITDA | EV/EBITDA (NTM) | Dividend Yield | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|------------------|-----------------|--------------|----|
| **KGS** | **4.35M (#1)** | **97.7%** | **$1.31B** | **$715M** | **~11×** | **3.0%** | Scale + large-HP depth; Kodiak Power Solutions new vertical |
| Archrock (AROC) | ~3.3M (#2) | ~96% | $1.49B | ~$700M [Estimate] | ~9–10× | 2.4% | Faster deleveraging (2.7×); also entering distributed power; [AROC data](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/aroc/) |

**Critical observation**: Both AROC and USAC entered distributed power generation in 2025–2026. This means Kodiak Power Solutions' AI-data-center thesis is industry-wide — KGS has the largest footprint (395 MW) but not a unique claim on the opportunity.

### How KGS's Moat Differs

1. **Utilization edge → pricing power**: 97.7% vs. AROC ~96% vs. USAC ~94%; near-zero idle HP means customers cannot threaten to switch and KGS contracts renew at premium rates. Competitors with 4–6% idle capacity face pricing pressure; KGS does not.
2. **Large-HP specialization depth**: 970 avg HP/unit vs. AROC estimated ~800–850 HP/unit; larger units have longer lead times, higher barriers, and deeper customer embedding.
3. **Cleaner shareholder register**: EQT exit (Dec 2025) eliminated the secondary-offering overhang that AROC and USAC never had. KGS now trades without a PE sponsor hand-to-mouth.
4. **Portfolio quality over volume**: Mexico exit and deliberate large-HP focus signal a willingness to sacrifice lower-margin assets for quality — consistent with the "utility-like" compounding thesis.

### Vulnerabilities

- AROC ahead on de-leveraging (2.7× vs KGS ~3.8× post-DPS); more balance sheet flexibility if cycle turns
- Distributed power is now a 3-way competition; KPS first-mover window is 12–18 months, not 3–5 years
- Customer vertical integration risk (Williams/ET/Targa self-building) at extreme pricing
- New PE-backed entrants constrained mainly by Caterpillar/Ariel OEM lead times

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**TAM**: U.S. installed contract compression HP ~17–18M; KGS's 4.35M = ~24% market share.

**Structural demand drivers**:
1. **Permian associated-gas growth**: Crude production → associated gas → compression demand; gas-to-oil ratio rises as wells age → structurally bullish per barrel
2. **LNG export build-out**: U.S. LNG expanding ~14 Bcf/d → ~25 Bcf/d by 2028; multi-year compression demand anchor from Gulf Coast projects
3. **AI data center power demand**: Behind-the-meter natural gas generation = fastest-deployable large-scale power source; AI compute load growing 40%+ annually per hyperscaler capex announcements
4. **Pipeline-to-power shift**: Natural gas as backup/baseload for intermittent renewables; persistent compression demand regardless of renewables build-out pace

**Regulatory environment**:
- **Methane mitigation rules**: EPA push for lower-emission compressors → tailwind for KGS's modern large-HP fleet
- **LNG export policy**: Current administration is pro-LNG; Jan 2024 pause (Biden) illustrates bipartisan policy volatility
- **Trade / tariff environment**: New in FY2025 10-K 1A — equipment cost risk
- **OBBBA full expensing (July 4, 2025)**: Meaningfully reduced FY2025 effective tax rate; structural FCF tailwind for capital-intensive companies

**Cyclicality**: The 2014–2016 oil collapse pushed compression utilization to ~80%. Today's 97.7% leaves near-zero slack — the next cycle's downside starting point is worse (no idle-to-active HP transition buffer). However, take-or-pay contracts mitigate near-term realization risk; only ~9% of HP is on month-to-month (per 10-K).
