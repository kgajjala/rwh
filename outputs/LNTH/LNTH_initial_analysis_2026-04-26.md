## Executive Summary

Lantheus Holdings (NASDAQ: LNTH) is the U.S. market leader in PSMA-targeted PET imaging for prostate cancer via PYLARIFY — the first radiodiagnostic to reach blockbuster status ($1.06B in FY2024). The company is navigating a deliberate strategic transition: divesting its legacy SPECT business (completed January 1, 2026), acquiring Life Molecular Imaging (Neuraceq amyloid PET, Alzheimer's) and Evergreen Theragnostics (NET diagnostic + RLT manufacturing pipeline), and positioning as a fully integrated PET radiopharmaceutical platform.

The near-term setup is asymmetric and event-driven:
- **Revenue trough**: FY2026 guidance of $1.4–1.45B (–6 to –9% YoY) as PYLARIFY pricing erodes from competitor entry (Posluma, Illuccix/Gozellix)
- **Three FDA catalysts in 2026**: PYLARIFY TruVu ✅ (approved March 6), LNTH-2501 (PDUFA June 29), MK-6240 (PDUFA August 13)
- **CEO vacancy**: Brian Markison retired December 31, 2025; Mary Anne Heino (former CEO) serves as interim; permanent CEO search ongoing
- **Securities litigation**: Class action filed alleging failure to disclose PYLARIFY pricing/competitive risks (class period Feb–Aug 2025)

**Verdict**: 🟡 **Watch** for non-holders. 🟡 **Hold** for current holders. The PW EV of ~$94 at $82.91 implies modest +13% 3-year asymmetry — not compelling at spot but attractive in the $65–78 zone where bear-case risk/reward improves meaningfully.

## Business Overview & Moat

### What the Company Does

Lantheus is a radiopharmaceutical company with two commercial legs and a pipeline:

| Product | Revenue | % Total | Role |
|---------|---------|---------|------|
| PYLARIFY (PSMA PET) | $989.1M | 64% | Prostate cancer staging/monitoring |

| Neuraceq (amyloid PET) | ~$85M | 6% | Alzheimer's disease staging |
| SPECT (divested Jan 1, 2026) | ~$155M | 10% | Legacy SPECT diagnostics |

**Pipeline (2026–2028)**:
- LNTH-2501: Ga-68 NET PET diagnostic (PDUFA June 29, 2026)
- MK-6240: F-18 tau PET for Alzheimer's monitoring (PDUFA August 13, 2026)
- PYLARIFY TruVu: larger-batch PSMA PET (approved March 2026; commercial Q4 2026)
- LNTH-2503: Lu-177 RLT Phase 1 (CCK2R target, small cell lung cancer)

### Moat: Narrow

The moat is real but not durable at PYLARIFY's prior pricing. First-mover scale in PSMA PET (established in >3,000 U.S. sites), regional cyclotron + radiopharmacy distribution network, and physician workflow stickiness provide meaningful advantages. However, the entry of Posluma (Blue Earth/Bracco), Illuccix/Gozellix (Telix), and Curium U.S. (pending) has structurally broken PYLARIFY's pricing power.

The strategic thesis is a pivot from single-product PSMA dominance to a multi-indication PET platform (PSMA + NET + tau + amyloid) that no single competitor currently replicates.

## Competitive Position

**The core competitive dynamic**: Posluma (Blue Earth/Bracco) holds FDA-approved PSMA PET with a transitional pass-through (TPT) reimbursement advantage through September 30, 2026. In markets where hospital systems receive TPT-level reimbursement for Posluma but not for PYLARIFY (whose TPT has expired), there is a direct financial incentive to switch. This mechanism — not just price competition — is driving PYLARIFY's market share loss and price erosion. When Posluma's TPT expires September 30, 2026, this advantage disappears.

**Telix (Illuccix + Gozellix)** generated US$161M in PSMA franchise revenue in Q4 2025 alone with volumes growing and profitability established — a meaningful and growing competitive presence. Telix's two-agent PSMA platform (Illuccix + the newly launched Gozellix) is the most operationally sophisticated PSMA PET competitor.

**Novartis (Locametz + Pluvicto)** owns the most complete theranostic pair in PSMA: the diagnostic and the therapy are both approved and marketed together. This creates institutional loyalty in major oncology centers running Pluvicto programs.

**Lantheus's differentiated position**: No other U.S. radiopharmaceutical company simultaneously holds PSMA PET (PYLARIFY), NET PET (LNTH-2501 pending), tau PET (MK-6240 pending), and amyloid PET (Neuraceq) diagnostics alongside an RLT manufacturing CDMO. The multi-indication PET platform is the structural moat claim if the pipeline delivers.
