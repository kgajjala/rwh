## 1. What MP Materials Does

MP Materials (NYSE: MP) owns and operates **Mountain Pass** in San Bernardino County, California — the only fully-integrated rare-earth mining and processing site in North America, and one of the world's richest NdPr orebodies. The company produces **NdPr (neodymium-praseodymium) oxide** — the critical input for permanent magnets used in EV motors, wind turbines, defense systems (F-35 jets, missiles, autonomous drones), and consumer electronics.

**The founding insight** (James Litinsky, 2017 acquisition out of Molycorp bankruptcy): Mountain Pass had been mothballed during the 2010s rare-earth price crash. Litinsky's thesis was that (1) the orebody is world-class and irreplaceable, (2) China's structural control of rare-earth supply is a national-security vulnerability the U.S. would eventually have to solve, and (3) restarting at the bottom of the cycle could be massively asymmetric. The company went public via SPAC in November 2020.

**Today**: MP is the only U.S. company with a fully operational rare-earth stack — **Stage I** (mining + concentrate), **Stage II** (oxide separation, operational), **Stage III** (sintered NdFeB magnet manufacturing at Independence, TX, commercially launched December 2025). The **DoD partnership** (July 2025) transformed the equity story from "commodity producer" to "national-security strategic asset."

**FY2025 scale**:
- $224.4M revenue (+10% YoY)
- 2,599 MT NdPr oxide produced (+101% YoY)
- Q4 2025: $39.2M Adj. EBITDA (first positive quarter), $51M PPA income, $21.9M Magnetics revenue
- $1.8B cash on balance sheet (post-DoD $400M preferred investment)

## 3. The DoD Partnership — The Transformational Catalyst

On July 9, 2025, MP and the U.S. Department of Defense announced a landmark public-private partnership:

- **$400M convertible preferred equity investment** by DoD
- **$150M loan** (One Big Beautiful Bill Act funding)
- **10-year NdPr offtake** with a **$110/kg price floor** (the "PPA mechanism")
- **DoD becomes largest shareholder** (~15% on as-converted basis, post-warrant exercise)

**PPA economics**: When NdPr spot trades below $110/kg, DoD pays MP the difference on its offtake volume. In Q4 2025 — with NdPr spot below $110 — this generated **$51M of PPA income in a single quarter**. Annualized, trough-NdPr PPA income could reach $150–200M/yr. This effectively eliminated the "commodity collapse" bear case that previously dominated the risk set.

## 5. Competitive Moat: Wide

Mountain Pass is the only scaled U.S. rare-earth resource with a 70+ year reserve life. The DoD partnership creates a regulatory + capital moat that no competitor can replicate. MP is the only U.S. company with a complete mine-to-magnet stack at commercial scale.

**Peer landscape**:

| Company | Geography | Model | Near-Term Threat to MP |
|---------|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| **[Lynas Rare Earths (LYC.AX)](https://www.lynas.com/)** | Australia + Malaysia + TX (processing) | Mine → oxide (no magnets) | Closest ex-China peer; ~10–15% global ex-China NdPr; no U.S. domicile premium; no magnet manufacturing |
| **[Energy Fuels (UUUU)](https://www.energyfuels.com/)** | U.S. (UT) | Uranium + early-stage REE | Minimal near-term competition |
| **China Northern Rare Earth** | China | State-controlled dominant producer | Sets reference spot price; threat via export quota manipulation |
| **Shenghe Resources** | China | Former Mountain Pass offtake customer | Now a competitive reference (relationship terminated July 2025) |

**The moat differentiator**: U.S. domicile + DoD backstop + mine-to-magnet integration is irreplicable by Lynas or any other ex-China producer. No peer has shipped commercial sintered NdFeB magnets at scale in the U.S. — MP did this in December 2025. The 10X campus, if executed, extends this moat into the 2030s.

## 7. Industry Macro Context

**Global supply structure**: China controls ~85–90% of NdPr mining and ~95% of separation and magnet manufacturing. Bloomberg Intelligence projects China's share declining from 90% (2024) to 69% by 2030 as Western supply chains develop — but this is a decade-long transition. *[Source: [Bloomberg Intelligence press](https://www.bloomberg.com/company/press/world-scrambles-for-rare-earths-to-erode-chinas-dominance-from-90-to-69-market-share-and-gain-pricing-power-according-to-bloomberg-intelligence/)]*

**NdPr supply-demand**: Small deficit of ~2,000 MT in 2025, growing to ~6,000 MT deficit in 2026, before returning to tight balance 2028–2030. The deficit window aligns with MP's thesis horizon. *[Source: [Rare Earth Market Outlook March 2026](https://rare-earth-mining.com/rare-earth-market-pricing-analysis-march-2026/)]*

**Policy tailwinds**:
- **IRA domestic-content EV credits**: Pull-through demand advantage for MP magnets in U.S.-built EVs
- **DPA Title III**: Direct DoD procurement authority for strategic materials
- **One Big Beautiful Bill Act** (July 4, 2025): $150M MP loan; broader strategic-minerals funding
- **China rare-earth export controls**: Each PRC restriction announcement re-rates non-Chinese supply premium
