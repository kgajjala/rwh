## Business Overview

NIKE, Inc. (NYSE: NKE) — founded 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports by Phil Knight and Bill Bowerman — is the dominant global athletic footwear and apparel brand with FY2025 revenues of **$46.3B** across two channels:

- **Wholesale**: $25.9B (~58% of NIKE Brand); re-emphasized under Hill; Q3 FY2026 +5%
- **Nike Direct**: $18.8B (~42%); –13% FY2025 from DTC over-pivot correction; digital –21%

Four geographic segments: North America ($19.6B, 42%), EMEA (~$12.1B, 26%), Greater China (~$6.7B, 14%), APLA (~$6.3B, 14%), plus Converse ($1.7B).

## Competitive Moat & Landscape

**Rating: Wide** — brand + athlete IP + scale moat intact; DTC misstep was strategic, not structural.

| Competitor | Share | Primary Threat to NKE |
|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| Adidas | ~12–14% global | Lifestyle competition; less sport-specific depth |
| **On Holding** | ~2–3% (running, growing) | **Winning $200+ performance running — biggest long-term threat** |
| Deckers (Hoka) | ~1–2% (running) | Ultra-running + comfort crossover |
| New Balance | ~4–5% | Credibility in lifestyle + performance simultaneously |
| **Anta Sports** | ~15–16% China | **Nike's primary China structural threat; exceeds Nike in China** |
| Li Ning | ~7–8% China | National pride + improving quality |

Nike's structural advantage: Jordan Brand alone generates ~$7.3B revenue — larger than any single competitor's US athletic total. No competitor has comparable breadth of elite athlete/cultural IP.
