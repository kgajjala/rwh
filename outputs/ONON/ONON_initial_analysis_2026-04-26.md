## Executive Summary

On Holding AG is a Swiss premium athletic brand that has compounded from CHF 725M in revenue at its September 2021 IPO to CHF 3.0B in FY2025 — a 4× revenue scale in four years while expanding gross margins from 59% to 63%. The stock sits –41% from its $61.29 peak, offering an entry at ~16× forward EBITDA vs. a direct comparable (Hoka parent Deckers) trading at ~16× on materially slower growth and lower gross margins.

The setup is a classic macro-driven behavioral mispricing story: four negative narratives compressing simultaneously (FX headwind, tariff risk, flat EBITDA margin guide, CEO transition) on a brand whose underlying metrics — 35.6% constant-currency FY2025 growth, 63.9% Q4 gross margin, +85% APAC constant-currency in Q4, apparel acquired 10% of new customers — are demonstrably better than at the $61 peak.

**Central thesis**: ONON is a premium consumer compounder mispriced by reversible macro headwinds. The 3-year probability-weighted EV is ~$53 (+46% from $36.25), with a ~2.7:1 bull/bear risk/reward and a strong buy consensus (18/21 analysts Buy-equivalent, median target $60).

**Primary risk**: U.S. tariff escalation on Asia-sourced footwear (probability 50%, partially priced) and a leadership transition (co-founders Allemann + Coppetti replacing CEO Breu effective March 2026, with simultaneous CFO change in May 2026) that creates near-term execution uncertainty.

## Business Overview

On Holding AG (NYSE: ONON) was founded in Zurich in 2010 by former Ironman triathlete Olivier Bernhard and co-founders David Allemann (brand/design) and Caspar Coppetti (commercial). The company's founding insight — garden-hose cushioning prototypes evolved into the proprietary CloudTec architecture — created a premium running shoe that combined Swiss aesthetic identity with genuine performance credibility.

The brand has scaled around three pillars: (1) **no permanent discounting** (preserving 63%+ gross margins), (2) **elite athlete ecosystem** (co-investor Roger Federer; Grand Slam champion Iga Świątek; ATP top-10 Ben Shelton), and (3) **DTC channel discipline** (41.8% DTC mix vs. <30% industry norm). From running shoes, On has expanded into tennis footwear, trail/hiking, lifestyle, and now apparel — apparel growing +46% constant currency in Q4 2025 and becoming a customer acquisition channel (from 6% to 10% of new customers acquired through apparel in FY2025).

**FY2025 snapshot**: CHF 3.01B net sales (+30% reported, +35.6% constant currency); 62.8% gross margin (record; 5-year expansion from 56.0%); adj. EBITDA margin ~18.8%; DTC 41.8%; APAC surpassed CHF 1.0B; cash CHF 1.02B (highest ever); 67 owned stores globally.

## Competitive Position

**Moat rating: Narrow** — brand premium, pricing discipline, and DTC ownership, without structural switching costs.

The most important moat evidence is the 5-year gross margin trajectory: 56.0% (FY2022) → 62.8% (FY2025), expanding *while* scaling rapidly. This is rare. Hoka (direct competitor, Deckers parent) runs ~55% blended gross margins despite premium positioning. Nike runs ~44%. The ONON margin premium reflects genuine brand pricing power — the no-permanent-discounting policy that co-CEO Allemann captured in: *"We are not following trends; we are co-creating culture."*

**Most direct competitor**: Hoka (Deckers). Both compete at $150–200 price points in premium running. ONON's edge: higher DTC mix (41.8% vs. Hoka's lower penetration), more distinctive visual identity (CloudTec pods vs. maximalist foam stack), and stronger lifestyle crossover. ONON grew +35.6% c-c FY2025 vs. Hoka's mid-teens — the premium consumer is choosing both but engaging more with On's aspirational positioning.

## Management & Leadership Transition

**The March 26, 2026 leadership change is the key new material event** not present in the prior v2.5 wiki page. Co-founders David Allemann (brand/design) and Caspar Coppetti (commercial) transitioned to Co-CEO roles, replacing Marc Aurel Breu who served as CEO since 2022 (previously CFO). Simultaneously, CFO Martin Hoffmann is departing; incoming CFO Frank Sluis joins in May 2026.

**Bull read on the transition**: Allemann + Coppetti are the brand architects. Bringing them back to the top role at a time when On is scaling into apparel, APAC, and lifestyle — precisely the growth vectors requiring brand integrity over operational efficiency — is thesis-consistent. Breu built the margin discipline; the founders can build the brand the next step.

**Bear read**: Two leadership changes simultaneously at a CHF 3B scale-up, just as the company is navigating tariff headwinds, APAC buildout, and CFO onboarding. Execution noise is virtually guaranteed for 1–2 quarters. The stock sold off on the announcement and has not recovered.

**Key quote from the final Hoffmann-era call (Q4 2025)**:
> *"We expect net sales to grow at least 23% at constant currency and a full-year gross margin of at least 63% — above our 2025 result — despite tariff impact."*
— Martin Hoffmann, Q4 2025 Earnings Call, [March 3, 2026](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/03/03/on-holding-onon-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

## Key Risks

1. **⚠️ U.S. tariff escalation** (High impact, 50% prob, partially priced): Vietnam + China sourcing exposed. 2026 guidance built on 20% tariff assumption. Each additional 10pts = ~50bps gross margin drag. Upside scenario: any de-escalation → direct beat vs. guide.

2. **⚠️ Leadership transition** (High impact, 35% prob, partially priced): Co-CEO structure untested at CHF 3B+ scale; simultaneous CFO change. Execution noise likely 1–2 quarters; strategic continuity risk if founders pivot away from Breu's financial discipline.

3. **⚠️ Swiss franc strength** (Medium impact, 60% prob, partially priced): ~800bps reported-revenue drag. The c-c/reported gap (35.6% vs. 30% FY2025) persistently understates underlying demand.

4. **⚠️ Athletic footwear category fade** (High impact, 15% prob, not priced): Long-cycle risk — premium running penetration elevated in U.S. at ~6 years into current cycle. Historical brand cycles suggest 8–12 year leadership windows.

5. **⚠️ Apparel under-execution** (Medium impact, 30% prob, not priced): Apparel is the primary new growth vector. A stall at 5–7% of revenue through 2027 would remove the bull-case multiple support.
