## Business & Competitive Overview

P&G operates five segments spanning the full household necessity spectrum:

| Segment | Q3 FY2026 Net Sales | Q3 Organic | Key Brands |
|---------|--------------------|------------|-----------|
| Fabric & Home Care | $7.4B | +3% | Tide, Downy, Febreze |
| Baby/Feminine/Family Care | $5.1B | +3% | Pampers, Charmin, Bounty |
| Beauty | $3.9B | **+7%** | Head & Shoulders, Olay, SK-II |
| Health Care | $3.1B | +2% | Oral-B, Vicks, Crest |
| Grooming | $1.6B | +1% | Gillette, Braun |

**Moat**: Wide but cyclically stressed. Brand equity (#1 positions in Fabric Care, Diapers, Razors) + distribution scale (180+ countries, entrenched retailer relationships) + R&D ($1.9B/year) constitute durable advantages. However, pricing-power fragility was confirmed by four straight quarters of volume losses — the Wide moat does not imply unlimited price elasticity.

**Competitive peer context**:
- Colgate-Palmolive and Unilever trade at 17–18× forward P/E vs. PG's ~21× — the premium requires execution proof.
- Private label is the most structurally relevant threat: 20–30% category share in laundry and paper; quality parity narrowing in detergents.

## Management Assessment

**Shailesh Jejurikar** (CEO since Jan 1, 2026): 36-year P&G veteran; former COO and Fabric & Home Care CEO. Continuity pick. Q2 FY2026 debut was a miss; Q3 shows improvement. Q3 commentary: *"We're increasing investments to accelerate momentum with consumers despite the challenging geopolitical and economic environment, while still maintaining our guidance ranges."*

**Jon Moeller** (Executive Chairman): Oversaw the FY2025 restructuring. Notably sold ~$28M in PG shares over the past 6 months — the largest insider-sell cluster in recent history. RSU vesting explains some; the magnitude warrants attention.

**FY2025 letter theme** (Moeller): *"To meet these opportunities and challenges, we need to widen our margin of advantage."* — the strategic mandate Jejurikar now owns. Capital allocation was disciplined: $9.9B dividends + $6.5B buybacks = $16B+ returned in FY2025.
