## The Business

RH operates 75 immersive Design Galleries (69 North America, 6 Europe) selling luxury home furnishings under the RH brand ecosystem (Interiors, Modern, Outdoor, Beach House, Ski House, Baby & Child, Teen) plus the premium Waterworks sub-brand. 25 galleries integrate restaurants/wine bars. Membership program ($175/yr) drives 95%+ of revenue from committed, full-price buyers.

**Scale** (FY2025, ended Jan 31, 2026):
- Revenue: **$3.44B** (+8.1% YoY)
- Adj EBITDA: **$596.5M** (17.3% margin)
- FCF: **$252M**
- Net financial debt: **~$2.35B**
- Annual interest expense: **$225M**

## The Thesis in One Sentence

RH is a deeply cyclical luxury home retailer at the trough of a multi-year housing freeze, with a completed inventory destocking, confirmed market share gains vs. all peers (+15% 2-year revenue vs. Pottery Barn –6%), and a $20–25B global brand target — where a housing recovery + tariff resolution + international ramp could deliver 3× equity returns, but leverage and litigation amplify downside if recovery delays to 2028+.

## Pivotal Question

Is the FY2026 margin compression (guided 14–16% adj EBITDA, down from 17.3% actual) a temporary pre-opening-cost blip that reverses as international galleries ramp and housing turns — or is it the beginning of a structural margin deterioration that forces dilutive refinancing before the recovery arrives?

## What Changed vs. Prior Analysis (v2.5 → v2.9)

Five material updates from primary-source synthesis:

1. **Long-term target upgrade**: Management's global brand target is **$20–25B** (not the $5.4–5.8B North America figure cited in the old wiki). The North America target is $5–6B from full Design Gallery deployment. The global number is management's own projection from the Q4 press release and 10-K forward-looking statements.

2. **FY2025 actuals better than feared**: Adj EBITDA margin of **17.3%** for FY2025 (the old wiki cited ~14%, confusing FY2026 guidance for FY2025 actuals). FCF of $252M vs. –$214M in FY2024 — cash generation has returned.

3. **Inventory destocking complete**: Merchandise inventories declined from $1.02B to $818.6M (–$200.9M, –20% YoY). This removes the markdown overhang and sets up clean demand signals from the RH Estates launch.

4. **Short interest is exceptional**: 35.62% of float (vs. "moderately elevated" in prior analysis). This is a major T (Technical) BAIT signal — any positive catalyst creates mechanical short-covering amplification.

5. **Shareholder lawsuits**: Multiple class-action lawsuits filed April 2026 investigating potential fraud claims related to buyback timing. Not in the prior analysis; now a material risk.

## Key Risks

1. **Housing freeze extends to 2028**: Demand driver requires home sales turnover recovery; currently at multi-decade lows.
2. **Tariff uncertainty**: IEEPA tariffs struck down Feb 20, 2026; new tariffs announced under alternative authority; 40% of assortment in resourcing motion; $30M Q4 backorder drag.
3. **International execution**: Germany impairments ($19M in FY2024) established real risk; 6 new international galleries planned for 2026 require flawless local execution.
4. **Leverage**: $2.35B net financial debt, $225M annual interest; equity was barely positive ($61M) as of Jan 31, 2026.
5. **Friedman concentration**: No succession plan; brand identity inseparable from one individual.
6. **Shareholder litigation**: Multiple class-actions filed April 2026; potential for significant legal costs or settlement.
