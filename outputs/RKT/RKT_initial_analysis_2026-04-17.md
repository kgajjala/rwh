## Business Overview

Rocket Companies (NYSE: RKT) is the largest U.S. mortgage originator (~25% non-bank origination market share), the largest U.S. mortgage servicer (~$2.7T servicing portfolio post the October 2025 Mr. Cooper acquisition), and — following the July 2025 Redfin acquisition — operates the first fully integrated end-to-end U.S. homeownership platform spanning home search (Redfin, 100M+ MAU), mortgage origination (Rocket Mortgage), and servicing (Mr. Cooper, Rocket).

**Core revenue streams**:
- **Mortgage origination** — Gain on sale (GOS) income from originating and selling loans; volume-driven; cyclically rate-sensitive.
- **Mortgage servicing** — Recurring fee income from $2.7T MSR portfolio; counter-cyclical (benefits when rates rise and refi falls).
- **MSR fair value changes** — Non-cash mark-to-market on the $19.4B MSR asset; excluded from Adjusted Revenue (the basis for management's reporting and consensus models).
- **Redfin** — Real estate media + brokerage commissions + mortgage attach (40%+ of Redfin transactions now route through Rocket Mortgage).

**Operating brands**: Rocket Mortgage, Rocket Money, Rocket Homes, Redfin, Bay Equity, Mr. Cooper.

**Corporate-structure note**: The Up-C structure was collapsed June 30, 2025 — RKT is now a clean C-corp. Public float grew from ~7% to ~35%. Year-over-year EPS comparisons require pro-forma adjustment; use Adjusted Revenue and Adjusted EBITDA for all period comparisons.

## Moat Assessment

**Narrow-to-Emerging-Wide** — durable structural advantages exist but the bear case is non-trivial.

**Sources**:
- **Recapture rate 3× industry**: Rocket retains ~3× more borrowers when they refinance or move than the industry average — a data-driven, servicing-fed structural advantage that compounds with portfolio scale.
- **AI operating leverage**: ~half the headcount for the same origination volume vs. 2020; 800K AI mortgage conversations/month; 2.5× conversion lift on digital pre-approvals. Cost structure competitors cannot replicate without years of investment.
- **Integrated platform flywheel**: Redfin (search) → Rocket Mortgage (originate) → Mr. Cooper (service) → Rocket (recapture). Redfin mortgage attach went from 27% pre-acquisition to **40%** in 4 months — faster than analyst expectation.
- **Servicing scale**: $2.7T MSR portfolio generates recurring servicing fees regardless of origination cycle — the natural hedge against rate increases that compress GOS volume.
- **Brand**: Rocket Mortgage is the #1-awareness non-bank mortgage brand.

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Mortgage origination is commodity at the transaction level; Rocket competes on efficiency and recapture, not pricing power.
- Redfin brand is weaker than Zillow / Realtor.com for organic home search.
- $10.6B of goodwill ($1.75B Redfin + ~$8.8B Mr. Cooper) is the bet; impairment risk if either integration disappoints.
- $10.4B of senior notes; ~$7.7B net debt — leveraged compared to a counter-cyclical comp.
- Dan Gilbert's super-voting control concentrates governance authority.
- RESPA class-action filed January 2026 alleges illegal referral-kickback structures across the integrated platform — if certified, threatens the ecosystem model.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight**: The American homeownership process — search, offer, finance, close, refinance — is absurdly fragmented. Realtors, mortgage brokers, banks, servicers, title companies, and appraisers operate in disconnected silos with no shared data and significant consumer friction. Rocket's thesis is that a single technology platform with shared data and AI can do all of this better, faster, and cheaper, capturing a fee at every step of the homeownership lifecycle.

**Today**: Post-Redfin and post-Mr. Cooper, Rocket controls the only end-to-end stack: home search → mortgage origination → mortgage servicing → recapture. The AI cost-structure advantage means Rocket can win on price *and* on margin if volume returns to normalized levels.

**Pivotal question**: At ~$48B EV the market is pricing RKT somewhere between "large cyclical with good operating leverage" and "early platform with structural margin advantages." Is the platform thesis far enough along — the 40% Redfin attach, the 3× recapture, the 50%-of-2020-headcount AI advantage, the $500M+ Mr. Cooper synergy run-rate "ahead of the original 2027 target" per CFO — to justify $48B against ~$2.5–3.5B run-rate EBITDA, or does the bear case (rate re-inflation + integration failure + goodwill impairment) win?

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**The housing lock-in effect**: 85–90% of outstanding mortgages have rates below 5%. With 30-year rates at 6.5–7.0%, almost nobody wants to sell. This lock-in has suppressed both purchase volume *and* refi volume simultaneously — creating the trough Rocket is navigating.

**The unlock scenario**: Any sustained move of 30-year rates toward 5.75–6.25% would simultaneously (a) unlock purchase volume as sellers decide to move, (b) trigger a refi wave from underwater 7%+ borrowers, (c) lift Rocket origination volume at high GOS margins. Even 50–75bps of rate relief = industry-wide $300–500B+ volume increase.

**Mortgage market sizing**:
- 2023 trough: ~$1.3T
- 2024 recovery: ~$1.6–1.8T
- Normal market: ~$2.0–2.5T
- Refi boom potential: $3.0–4.0T (2020 analog)

**Market share dynamics**: Rocket gained share during the trough as competitors exited. Non-bank lenders now control ~65%+ of origination vs. banks. Rocket at 25% of non-bank ≈ ~16% total market share. The structural shift toward non-bank is Rocket's tailwind.

## Section 12 — BAIT Framework

### B — Behavioral: **Strong**
The –35.9% drawdown from the January 16 peak — accumulating across the post-Q4-beat period and into a *new* 52-wk low at $11.08 — is a textbook behavioral overreaction. The Q4 2025 print was a clear beat ($1.7B vs. $1.56B guide; $592M EBITDA), Krishna pre-announced strength on CNBC, and Q1 2026 guide implied a step-change ($2.6–2.8B annualizing to $10–11B). The selloff is attributable to macro anxiety (tariff-driven re-inflation fear, rate uncertainty, Redfin CEO departure) — not company-specific operating deterioration. Sentiment is now pricing the bear case despite operating data confirming the bull setup.

### A — Analytical: **Moderate-Strong**
Multiple analytical edges:
- The Up-C collapse makes historical EPS comparisons numerically misleading — most analysts haven't cleanly articulated pro-forma earnings.
- MSR fair-value changes distort GAAP revenue by billions; most media summaries cite GAAP, missing the adjusted picture.
- $592M Q4 EBITDA × 4 = $2.37B run-rate; vs. EV ~$48B = ~20× — sounds expensive until a $4–5B 2027 EBITDA scenario is modeled, which compresses to 10–12×.
- Q1 2026 guide of $2.6–2.8B has the credibility of 4 consecutive quarters of beats.
- CFO's "synergy ahead of schedule" comment is not in consensus.

*Limits the signal*: the bear case has real fundamental merit (rate sensitivity, integration risk, $10.6B goodwill, RESPA tail risk) — analytical work doesn't unambiguously resolve "clear mispricing." It resolves to "asymmetric risk/reward with real downside."

### I — Informational: **Moderate**
Primary disclosures underappreciated by media:
1. Krishna's "4-year volume high" Squawk Box pre-announcement (most coverage missed)
2. Quantified AI operating leverage (800K chats/month, 2.5× conversion, 50% of 2020 headcount)
3. CFO Brown's explicit "ahead of original 2027 synergy target" statement
4. Redfin attach 27% → 40% in 4 months — faster than any analyst projection

### T — Technical: **Weak-Moderate**
- No stock split, no index inclusion catalyst, no buyback program (leverage prevents)
- Dan Gilbert control structure limits buyback optionality
- However: Q1 2026 earnings April 30 is a clear ~13-day hard catalyst
- Stock approaching the 52-wk low for the second time = tradeable technical level
- Up-C collapse increased public float from ~7% to ~35% — institutional ownership should grow as RKT enters the proper C-corp universe; a slow-burn technical tailwind

### BAIT Verdict
**Double overlap (B + A) — Moderate Conviction**. RKT shows meaningful behavioral overselling post-beat plus genuine analytical complexity that obscures earnings power. The signal is less clean than a triple overlap because (1) the bear case has real fundamental merit, (2) technical tailwinds are absent, (3) the informational edge is narrower than the behavioral edge. Behavioral fear is disproportionate (–35.9% on a beat-and-raise quarter), but the platform thesis requires more execution proof to graduate to triple-BAIT status. Reasonable risk/reward at $15.60; conviction should rise materially on a Q1 2026 beat.
