## Business Overview

Shopify (NASDAQ/TSX: SHOP), founded 2006 by Tobias Lütke, provides **integrated internet infrastructure for retail businesses** — storefronts, POS, payments (Shopify Payments / Shop Pay), shipping, capital/lending, B2B wholesale, ads, and increasingly the connective tissue between merchants and AI agents via UCP. Owns no inventory, runs no warehouses, carries no retail risk — pure platform economics. Independent (not Amazon-owned), open-standard-oriented, merchant-aligned.

**Scale (FY2025)**:
- **$11.56B revenue** (+30% YoY); **$378B GMV** (+29%); $124B in Q4 alone (+31%)
- Merchants in **175+ countries**; **875M+ unique shoppers** transacted via Shop Pay; 42M MAU in Q4
- **11+ consecutive quarters of 25%+ revenue growth (ex-logistics)** — among the fastest-growing large-cap technology companies globally

**Revenue mix (FY2025)**: Merchant Solutions ~73% (payments at 68% GMV penetration, Shop Pay +62%, capital, shipping, Shop Campaigns advertising — revenue doubled in 2025); Subscription Solutions ~27% (SaaS plans, **Plus enterprise upgrades — 34% of MRR**, enterprise wins).

**Geographic mix (FY2025)**: US ~63% (30% US e-commerce platform share), EMEA ~21% (+49% Europe GMV), APAC ~10% (~$699M revenue, 18.5% projected growth — clearest international expansion lever), Canada ~5%, LatAm ~1%. International revenue grew **+36% in FY2025 (vs. +33% FY2024 — accelerating)**.

**Recent enterprise wins (2024–2025)**: GM, L'Oréal, Estée Lauder, Sonos, Keurig Dr. Pepper, Amer Sports (Wilson, Salomon), Spanx, Glossier, Vuori — Plus is now ~34% of total Plus MRR, fundamentally repositioning SHOP from "SMB SaaS at scale" to "enterprise commerce platform with SMB tail" by 2027.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Does AI disrupt Shopify (LLM tools let merchants self-build, or AI agents bypass checkout entirely) — or does AI amplify Shopify (multi-channel, multi-currency, multi-agent commerce complexity makes Shopify's infrastructure MORE necessary, and UCP/Catalog become the connective tissue between merchants and every AI surface)?

The market priced the *disruption* scenario through Q4 2025/Q1 2026 (drove the stock from $182 to $88). Primary data — 11–15× growth in AI-attributed orders since Jan 2025, UCP co-developed with Google, **OpenAI's Instant Checkout retreat** (only ~30 merchants successfully onboarded; revised model now routes users to Shopify storefronts) — supports the *amplification* scenario. The thesis is "AND" not "OR": AI surfaces discover, Shopify handles fulfillment.

**How Shopify's moat differs (with evidence)**:
1. **Full-stack integration**: store + payments (68% GMV penetration) + POS + shipping + lending + ads + AI storefronts — no peer has all layers at scale
2. **Network effects via Shop Pay**: 875M+ unique shoppers; Shop App native GMV +94% YoY; consumer-side lock-in via saved checkout
3. **Data / AI advantage**: $378B GMV dataset feeds Catalog specialized LLMs and Sidekick AI assistant
4. **Agentic commerce first-mover**: UCP co-developed with Google; **20+ endorsers** across payments (Visa, Mastercard, Stripe, Adyen, AmEx) + retail (Target, Walmart, Etsy, Wayfair)
5. **OpenAI Instant Checkout retreat as moat validation**: OpenAI abandoned native AI checkout after only ~30 Shopify merchants were successfully onboarded; revised model routes to Shopify merchant storefronts. Cleanest single primary-source piece of evidence that fulfillment complexity flows *through* Shopify, not around it

**Vulnerabilities**: 84× P/E is the moat's tax — any deceleration below 20% growth compresses violently; Lütke's dual-class control (40% voting via Founder Share) is feature-now, governance-risk-later; cross-border SMB exposure to tariff regimes.

Shopify is **Pattern B** (quarterly letters substituting for annual). Across the 2015 IPO Letter + Q4 FY2020–FY2025 letters + April 2025 *"AI is non-optional"* internal memo, three structural shifts:
