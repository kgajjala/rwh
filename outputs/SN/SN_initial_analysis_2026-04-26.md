## Business Overview

SharkNinja is a global designer and marketer of differentiated consumer products under the Shark and Ninja brands. The model is "asset-lite manufacturing" — SN designs and markets; contract manufacturers (formerly ~70% China; now ~100% US-volume non-China-capable) build. Sales channels: ~65% US (Amazon, big-box, DTC), ~35% international and growing 20%+ year-over-year.

**FY2024 verified**: net sales **$5,528.6M (+30.0%)**; adj EBITDA **$951.1M (17.2% margin)**; gross margin +320bps reported / +220bps adj.

**Q4 2025 results** (Feb 11, 2026): net sales **$2.10B (+17.6%)**; adj EBITDA **$395M** (18.8% margin, +250bps); adj EPS **$1.93** vs $1.40 PY; cash **$777M (+100% YoY)**.

**FY2026 guide**: net sales +10–11%; adj EBITDA $1.27–1.28B (+12–13%); adj EPS $5.90–$6.00 (+12–14%); capex $190–210M. Guide assumes current tariff levels persist (20% China / 20% Vietnam / 19% Indonesia/Thailand/Malaysia/Cambodia).

## Why SN Now — Three Reasons

1. **Supply-chain pivot already executed (and not priced in)**. Sell-side models still tag SN as "tariff-exposed." Barrocas' Q4 2025 disclosure that ~100% of US volume can be made outside China structurally re-frames the central thesis risk. The 52-week low of $76 (May 2025) was the tariff-scare bottom; the operational reality that delivered Q4 2025 (+17.6% rev, +36% EBITDA, +250bps margin) post-dates and contradicts that fear.
2. **Inaugural $750M buyback layered on $777M cash**. First-ever capital-return program for SN as a public company. Roughly 4.6% of $16.3B market cap. Funded internally; not levered. New floor under the stock.
3. **Innovation cadence is the moat**. 25+ launches/year, two new categories planned in 2026, four category-creation wins in three years (CREAMi 2022 → Slushi 2024 → TrioGlow 2025 → Fireside 360 2025). No peer (NWL, HELE, IRBT) matches the cadence; only Dyson is comparable on innovation but operates at the premium tier.
