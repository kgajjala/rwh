## Business Overview

UnitedHealth Group (NYSE: UNH) is the largest U.S. health insurance and healthcare services company by revenue ($447.6B in FY2025), built on a **vertical-integration thesis** that is structurally rare among public managed-care companies. Two operating reporting segments:

- **UnitedHealthcare** (~$344.9B FY2025 revenue) — the insurance / managed-care arm covering employer-sponsored commercial, individual, Medicare Advantage (MA), Medicaid, and global plans, with **49.8M medical members** at year-end 2025.
- **Optum** (~$270.6B FY2025 revenue, partially intersegment) — three sub-businesses:
  - **Optum Health** — value-based care clinics, ~90K employed/affiliated physicians, surgical centers, infusion clinics.
  - **Optum Insight** — data analytics, consulting, Change Healthcare (claims clearinghouse and revenue-cycle management).
  - **Optum Rx** — #2 U.S. PBM by script volume; primarily intersegment, serves UHC members but also third-party payers.

Geographic mix is overwhelmingly U.S. (~99% of revenue); UnitedHealthcare International (Brazil, Latin America) is small (<1%). The differentiating thesis vs. peers (ELV, HUM, CI, CVS, CNC) is the closed-loop tri-leg structure: a UHC member can be insured by UHC, see an Optum-employed physician, fill prescriptions through OptumRx, and have their data flowing through Optum Insight — enabling cost capture and outcome measurement at every step of the dollar.

In 2025 this thesis hit its hardest test on record: MLR jumped to 89.1% (vs. ~85.5% historical), CEO Andrew Witty resigned (May 13, 2025), prior CEO **Stephen Hemsley returned**, FY2025 guidance was suspended, Optum Health adjusted earnings collapsed from $7.9B to $2.3B, and the company faces an ongoing DOJ antitrust investigation into the integrated structure. Q1 2026 (reported Apr 21, 2026) was the first quantitative confirmation that the recovery is on the planned glide-path: MCR 83.9%, Adj EPS $7.23 vs. $6.59 consensus (+9.7% beat), FY26 Adj EPS guide raised to >$18.25.

### Business Model Mechanics

**Verdict: Wide and durable, structurally differentiated by tri-leg vertical integration; currently constrained in expression, not in source.**

### Sources of Moat

| Source | Strength | Evidence |

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Mkt Cap / Scale | Read on Moat / Threat |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Elevance (ELV)** | #2 commercial insurer; ~47M med members | Two-leg (insurer + Carelon services); narrower than UNH; cleaner MLR profile but less margin upside in normalization. |
| **CVS Health (CVS)** | Aetna + Caremark PBM + retail | Closest tri-leg analog (insurer + PBM + retail clinics) but Caremark is mid-tier PBM and Aetna integration is uneven; trades at deep discount. |

| **Molina (MOH)** | Medicaid-pure-play | Smaller, similar Medicaid risk profile to CNC. |

**PBM peer-set** (OptumRx is #2): CVS Caremark (#1 by lives), Express Scripts (#3, owned by CI). The PBM industry is **consolidated to ~3 firms covering >80% of insured lives**; OptumRx benefits from regulatory barriers (PBM legislation has stalled) plus first-party UHC member base.

**Care-delivery peer-set**: Hospital systems (HCA, THC, CYH) compete for procedure volume; primary-care plays (Walgreens-VillageMD, Amazon-One Medical) compete for the value-based-care wedge but at vastly smaller scale than Optum Health's ~90K-physician footprint.

**Health-tech / analytics peers**: Epic (private, EHR market leader), Oracle Cerner (#2 EHR), McKesson (distribution + technology). Optum Insight is differentiated by claims-clearinghouse scale (Change Healthcare) — *no peer has equivalent cross-payer claims data flow.*

### How UNH's Moat Differs — Evidence

1. **Tri-leg integration is structural, not bolt-on**: Competitors that attempted vertical integration (CVS/Aetna, CI/Express Scripts) executed the moves later and at smaller scale than Optum's organic build. Evidence: Optum Health margin profile *prior to* the FY2025 dislocation (>$7.9B FY24 adj earnings) had no peer comparable.
2. **Cross-payer Optum revenue**: Optum Insight + OptumRx earn fees from competing insurers — meaning rivals' revenue partially funds UNH. No peer has this dynamic at scale.
3. **CMS concentration risk is shared across the industry, not a UNH-specific weakness** — the FY25 MLR shock hit all MA-exposed insurers; UNH's diversification (commercial bright spot in 2025) cushioned the consolidated impact better than HUM (MA-pure) or CNC (Medicaid-pure).

### Tail-Risk Read on Competitive Position

- **DOJ vertical-integration overhang** could *forcibly* unwind the tri-leg moat — this is a tail risk but its severity is enormous.
- **GLP-1 utilization shock** is industry-wide; UNH's PBM+Optum Health vertical may actually *capture* part of the cost passthrough, partially offsetting MLR pressure (under-modeled).
- No peer is currently positioned to take share *at margin* — HUM is loss-making, CNC is rate-pressured, ELV is steady but smaller. UNH's main competitive risk is regulatory, not commercial.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Medical Cost Ratio / utilization** is the dominant variable. FY25's 89.1% MLR vs. historical 82–85% represents ~400bps of excess losses. Drivers cited in 10-K MD&A and Q1 2026 call:

- Post-COVID behavioral health surge
- MA demographic age-up and acuity mix shift
- CMS-mandated prior-authorization changes increasing approval rates
- Medicaid redetermination — higher-acuity members remained, lower-acuity churned
- GLP-1 utilization step-up

**Sensitivity**: 100bps MLR improvement = ~$4–5B EBIT.

**Medicare Advantage CMS rate-setting** — the critical annual variable.
- FY2025 MA rates: approximately flat/unfavorable
- FY2026 MA rates (announced March 2025): modestly improved
