## Headline Read

Zillow Group is the dominant U.S. residential-real-estate consumer marketplace, executing a credible super-app pivot — **Q4 2025 revenue +18%, FY25 +16%, Adj EBITDA margin 24%, FCF $420M (+36%), first GAAP-profitable year ($23M)** — while the market prices a Rocket-Redfin disintermediation narrative that is *partly contradicted* by the **$100M Zillow-Redfin rental-syndication deal** (Feb 2025) which converted Redfin from rentals-competitor to rentals-partner, lifting Zillow's listing share from 54% → **63%** in 2025.

The stock is **–52% from its 52-wk high of $93.88**, trading at ~14× FY26E EBITDA and ~3.9% FY25 FCF yield with a $1.3B cash position + new $500M revolver. Bull / Base / Bear FY28 = $95 / $68 / $30 at 30 / 50 / 20% probability → **PW EV $68.50 (+51% / ~15% CAGR)**. R/R ~**3.2:1** (Bull/Bear midpoints). Analyst median target ~$81–84 (n=19) implies +78–85% upside on 12-month basis.

The dominant active overhang is the **FTC suit (filed Sep-2025)** challenging the $100M Redfin rental-syndication deal — motion-to-dismiss pending; full unwind would clip Rentals share gains.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Does Zillow's super-app monetization (Mortgages + Rentals + Showcase) durably compound through a multi-year housing-cycle trough — and is the Rocket-Redfin combination + the FTC challenge correctly priced into the **52% drawdown**?

The pivot: ZG just printed its **first profitable year** with **24% EBITDA margin, +36% FCF growth, and 39% Rentals + 37% Mortgages growth** while housing turnover sits at multi-decade lows (~4M existing-home sales). Operating leverage when turnover normalizes (5M+) is meaningful.

1. **Rocket-Redfin reframe**: The earlier framing treated Rocket-Redfin as pure competitive disintermediation. Discovery of the **$100M Zillow-Redfin rental-syndication deal** (Feb 2025) reframes this as *partial partnership + partial competition*. Rentals listing share rose 54% → 63% on this deal. FTC sued in Sep-2025; motion-to-dismiss pending.

2. **First GAAP-profitable year**: FY2025 GAAP NI **+$23M** (vs. –$112M FY24); Adj EBITDA margin to 24% (+200bps); FCF $420M (+36%). Materially strengthens the analytical case (BAIT-A from Strong → Stronger).

3. **CSGP/Homes.com is the more material competitive read** going forward. The $1B+ marketing escalation by CoStar is peer-differentiated, well-funded, and **not yet priced in**. Replaces Rocket-Redfin as the dominant medium-term portal-share threat in the §6 risk table.

4. **Showcase still <2% of new listings nationwide** — premium-tier ARPU lever has multi-year runway.

5. **Capital allocation is active**: $232M Q4 25 buyback + $500M new revolver. Positions ZG to be opportunistic into further weakness.
