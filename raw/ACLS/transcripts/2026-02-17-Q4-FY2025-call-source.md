## CEO Russell Low — key framing

- "Record CS&I quarter — strength of growing installed base"
- SiC: "demand currently muted as the industry prioritizes technology transitions, specifically the conversion from 150mm to 200mm wafer sizes" — implies SiC capex resumption tied to 200mm fab buildouts (multi-year, but PUSHED out vs prior expectation)
- Memory: "DRAM and HBM applications improved sequentially, driven by accelerating infrastructure requirements of artificial intelligence" — primary FY26 growth driver
- General Mature: China customers "continue to digest the robust investments they've made in mature node capacity" — mature node digestion phase ongoing
- Veeco merger: progress noted; "focused on completing pending merger"

## Q3 2025 call (reference, 2025-11-04)
- China 46% of sales (down from 55% prior Q)
- Korea 10%, US 14%
- Q3 non-GAAP gross margin 41.8% (below 43% outlook — mix)
- "high-energy ion implantation increasingly critical for next-generation silicon carbide devices"
