# Robinhood Markets (HOOD) — Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript Summary

**Date**: April 28, 2026 (after market close)
**Source**: [Motley Fool Q1 2026 transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/04/28/robinhood-hood-q1-2026-earnings-transcript/)
**Participants**: Vlad Tenev (CEO), Shiv Verma (CFO)

## Prepared Remarks — Key Themes

### Crypto Revenue & Cyclicality
- Crypto represented ~18% of FY2025 revenue, demonstrating diversification vs. prior peaks
- Q1 2026 crypto revenue –47% YoY ($134M) driven by weaker retail volumes industry-wide
- Tenev: *"crypto as technology infrastructure is going to be big"* — emphasized tokenization as the structural long-term opportunity beyond speculative trading
- April crypto volumes tracking "about similar to what it was in Q1" — no sequential recovery into Q2 as of call date
- Management did not provide specific crypto revenue targets or recovery timeline

### Rothera Exchange / Event Contracts
- Rothera launching Q2 2026 (vertically integrated joint venture with Susquehanna)
- JV structure gives Robinhood "end-to-end control of customer experience, including product selection and pricing"
- April event-contract volumes: ≈$3B traded, second-highest month ever
- Tenev: Prediction markets remain early-stage; DCM consolidation expected in "next couple of years" but "hard to predict exact timing"
- Management acknowledged state-level regulatory friction ("jurisdictional dispute will play out in coming years") without conceding structural risk

### Net Interest Income (NII) Path
- Interest-earning assets "continue to grow" and "more than offset lower short-term interest rates"
- $6B shift from off-balance sweep accounts to on-balance free credit balances (FCB) at lower take rates created headwind; Verma: sweep balances (~$24B) and FCB (~$6B) expected to "stay roughly at this rate" going forward
- Margin book grew 93% YoY to a record — management cited this as incremental NII upside
- Secular growth in interest-earning assets (net deposits $17.7B Q1) is the primary NII driver, partially offsetting rate sensitivity

### Gold Attach Rate & ARPU Trajectory
- 4.3M Gold subscribers (+36% YoY); 16% of funded base; 40% attach rate for new Q1 customers
- 800K+ Robinhood Gold credit cards with $15B annualized purchase volume
- 125K funded banking customers; 40% direct deposit rate among them
- On pace to surpass 1M Gold cards and $100M ARR on credit card this year
- Securities lending: 25% customer opt-in, 50% of assets — management sees "a long way to go" on penetration
- Verma: "really healthy adoption, but we also have a long way to go"

### Bitstamp / International
- Institutional segment gaining market share; Bitstamp lending desk = "low-hanging fruit" for incremental revenue
- Canada crypto launch mid-year via WonderFi partnership
- Singapore: in-principle regulatory approval for comprehensive brokerage
- ~1M funded international customers
- Strategy: organic expansion where feasible; acquisitions (Indonesia cited as example) where needed
- EU tokenized stocks rollout in flight; tokenization positioned as long-cycle growth vector

### Capital Allocation
- $250M buyback executed Q1 (3.1M shares ~$81 avg); $300M+ YTD as of call
- Refreshed $1.5B buyback authorization; Verma: reflects "great confidence and opportunities ahead"
- No specific M&A targets announced; WonderFi, Bitstamp, TradePMR cited as integration templates
- Dividend: not discussed; no indication

### FY2026 Guidance
- Adj opex + SBC raised to **$2.7B–$2.825B** (from $2.6B–$2.725B prior guide)
- Incremental ~$100M driven by Trump Accounts operating expenses (~$50M in Q2)
- Trump Accounts: cost-plus structure; Tenev: "revenues will surpass costs"
- April net deposits ≈$5B month-to-date (tracking well above Q1 pace)
- No revenue or EPS guidance provided

## Investment-Relevant Synthesis

1. **Rothera/event contracts not yet proven durable**: April volumes strong ($3B second-highest month) but Tenev hedged on consolidation timing. The exchange goes live Q2 2026 — Q2 print will be the first real read on durability beyond election-cycle tailwinds.

2. **NII structural growth intact but rate-sensitivity exists**: Margin book +93% YoY is an underappreciated NII driver. The sweep→FCB shift is a one-time headwind already absorbed in Q1; forward NII growth is driven by deposit growth (+22% annualized).

3. **Crypto gives no near-term hope**: April volumes "about similar to Q1" — management effectively signaled no sequential recovery in Q2 crypto revenue. This was the primary driver of the post-earnings sell-off.

4. **Gold compounding is real**: 800K credit cards, $15B annualized spend, path to $100M ARR this year — these are new disclosure details not fully in the press release. Gold is becoming a multi-product annuity, not just a subscription.

5. **Opex guide raise (+$100M) was pre-announced (Trump Accounts) but still weighed on near-term EPS estimates**: Sell-side 2026 EPS estimates likely to be cut ≈10–26% (KBW: –26%; consensus converging lower).
