# P&G Q3 FY2026 Earnings Press Release
# Source: https://us.pg.com/newsroom/news-releases/PG-Announces-Fiscal-Year-2026-Third-Quarter-Results/
# Date: April 24, 2026 (pre-market)
# Also at: https://www.stocktitan.net/news/PG/p-g-announces-fiscal-year-2026-third-quarter-5cks48a43klj.html

## Key Results

Net Sales: $21.2B (+7% YoY vs. $19.8B prior year)
Organic Sales: +3% (Volume: +2%, Pricing: +1%)
GAAP Diluted EPS: $1.63 (+6% vs. $1.54 prior year) — BEAT consensus $1.56
Core EPS: $1.59 (+3% vs. $1.54 prior year) — narrowly missed consensus ~$1.60

## Segment Organic Sales Growth
- Beauty: +7% (volume +5%)
- Grooming: +1% (volume -2%)
- Health Care: +2% (volume -2%)
- Fabric & Home Care: +3%
- Baby, Feminine & Family Care: +3%

## Segment Net Sales
- Beauty: $3.9B (+11%)
- Grooming: $1.6B (+7%)
- Health Care: $3.1B (+7%)
- Fabric & Home Care: $7.4B (+7%)
- Baby, Feminine & Family Care: $5.1B (+6%)
- Total: $21.2B (+7%)

## FY2026 Guidance (Maintained, "toward lower end")
- All-in sales growth: 1-5%
- Organic sales growth: in-line to +4%
- Diluted EPS growth: 1-6%
- Core EPS growth: in-line to +4% (range: $6.83–$7.09/share)

## Headwinds
- Commodity costs: ~$150M after-tax
- Tariff costs: ~$400M after-tax
- Net interest & tax: ~$250M after-tax
- FX: ~$200M after-tax tailwind
- Combined net headwind: ~$0.25/share

## Capital Returns
- Cash returned to shareholders: $3.2B ($2.5B dividends + $600M+ buybacks)
- 70th consecutive annual dividend increase; 136th consecutive year of dividend payments since 1890

## CEO Commentary (Jejurikar)
"We're increasing investments to accelerate momentum with consumers despite the challenging
geopolitical and economic environment, while still maintaining our guidance ranges."

"Solid acceleration in top-line results while increasing investments in innovation and demand creation."

## Notable
- First companywide volume growth in over a year
- Gross margin fell 100bps (currency-neutral core basis) for 6th consecutive quarter
- Grooming and Health Care posted 2% volume declines (only segments declining)
