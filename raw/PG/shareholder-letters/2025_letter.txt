# P&G FY2025 Letter to Shareowners (Moeller)
# Source: https://us.pg.com/annualreport2025/introduction-and-fy-results/
# Full annual report: https://us.pg.com/annualreport2025/
# PDF: https://assets.ctfassets.net/oggad6svuzkv/3fjg4i2iSQslCvYlPny0KM/5427f48829bd51885e0fbdd0fe96f31f/2025_annual_report.pdf
# Author: Jon R. Moeller (outgoing CEO; became Executive Chairman Jan 1, 2026)
# Published: ~August 2025; covers FY2025 (ended June 30, 2025)

KEY EXCERPTS AND THEMES:

Performance:
- "P&G delivered continued organic sales and core earnings-per-share growth and returned significant cash
  to shareowners in fiscal year 2025, despite volatile macro-economic, geopolitical and consumer headwinds."
- Organic sales growth +2%; core EPS growth +4% (currency-neutral also +4%)
- Operating cash flow: $17.8B; adjusted FCF productivity: 87%
- "39th consecutive quarter of top-line growth"
- "9th consecutive year of core earnings-per-share growth"

Category performance:
- 9 of 10 categories showed organic sales expansion
- Family Care and Personal Health Care: mid-single digits
- Fabric Care, Home Care, Feminine Care, Hair Care, Grooming, Oral Care, Skin & Personal Care: low single digits
- Baby Care: slight decline
- E-commerce: +12%, now 19% of total company sales

Geographic:
- Focus markets (North America +2%, Europe +3%)
- Enterprise markets +2%, led by Latin America +4%

Capital allocation:
- Increased dividends by 5%; 69th consecutive annual dividend increase
- Returned $16B+ to shareholders ($9.9B dividends, $6.5B share repurchases)
- 135th consecutive year of dividend payments

Strategic outlook:
- "To meet these opportunities and challenges, we need to widen our margin of advantage."
- Five strategic pillars: Portfolio, Organization, Superiority, Constructive Disruption, Productivity
- Acknowledged "category growth rates in many parts of the world are slowing"
- Geopolitical unpredictability and rapid technological change as ongoing challenges

NOTES:
- This is Moeller's final letter as CEO (Jejurikar took over Jan 1, 2026)
- Moeller framed the restructuring as proactive; did not announce it publicly until later
- Full letter PDF download available at annual report downloads page
