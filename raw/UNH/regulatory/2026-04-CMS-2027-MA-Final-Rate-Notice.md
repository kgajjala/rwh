## Headline

- **2027 MA expected average rate change: ≈+2.0%** (final notice).
- This is a **finalized** rate, not the Advance Notice — locks in the FY2027 payment trajectory for MA insurers.
- **Above-prior-year baseline** and meaningfully above the most bearish industry expectations going into the comment period (some sell-side modeling had assumed flat-to-modestly-negative).

## Why It Matters for UNH

- UNH's UnitedHealthcare segment runs ~13M+ MA + Supplement members. CMS programs (Medicare + Medicaid combined) represented ~40% of FY2024 consolidated revenue per the FY2024 10-K. MA is the highest-margin component of that mix.
- **Sensitivity (Section 7 / §8)**: a 1% favorable MA rate change ≈ $1–2B EBIT for UNH's MA book. A ≈+2% finalization vs. a market-implied flat baseline = an incremental **~$2–4B FY2027 EBIT tailwind** vs. the prior expectation.
- This **directly de-risks** the largest known macro overhang on the §6 (Key Risks) table — *"CMS V28 MA risk-adjustment phase-in severity (FY27 final year)"* — and indirectly de-risks the Q2/Q3 2026 sequential-MCR concern by giving management more rate-side cushion to absorb utilization volatility.
