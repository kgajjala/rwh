01Why Does This Company Exist?

Crusoe was founded in 2018 by Chase Lochmiller (CEO) and Cully Cavness (President) on a deceptively simple insight: the United States was burning billions of cubic feet of natural gas every year because it was too expensive and logistically difficult to transport from remote oil fields to market. Oil drillers were legally required to "flare" this associated gas or pay fines — so they burned it into the atmosphere, wasting the energy and emitting massive amounts of CO₂ and methane. Lochmiller saw this waste as stranded power — free or near-free energy sitting at the exact location where you needed to deploy compute.

Crusoe's original model placed modular, mobile data centers at oil well sites to capture this flared gas, convert it to electricity at roughly 1/13th the standard grid cost, and run computing workloads — first Bitcoin mining, then AI training and inference. It was an elegantly self-financing model: oil companies paid Crusoe to solve their flaring compliance problem, and Crusoe monetized that free power by selling compute.

By 2024–2025, the company had evolved far beyond modular flare sites. It is now building gigawatt-scale AI factory campuses — among the largest data centers ever constructed — using a vertically integrated model that encompasses energy sourcing, site acquisition, data center design and construction, power infrastructure, and cloud platform delivery. The November 2024 divestiture of its Bitcoin mining operation to NYDIG was the definitive signal: Crusoe is now an AI infrastructure pure-play.

⚡ The Vertically Integrated "AI Factory" Model

Traditional AI infrastructure is fragmented: power companies, real estate developers, construction firms, data center operators, and cloud software are all separate businesses with misaligned incentives and slow handoffs. Crusoe's thesis is that owning the entire stack from energy capture to cloud API delivery creates a structural speed and cost advantage. When Crusoe builds in Abilene, it controls the power plant, the site, the construction, the GPU cluster, and the software — compressing what would normally take 3–4 years to under 12 months.

  • Energy layer: Flared gas capture, renewable energy (wind/solar/geothermal), behind-the-meter natural gas turbines — power sourced at below-market cost
  • Physical layer: Custom AI-optimized data center design and construction; liquid cooling; purpose-built for dense GPU clusters
  • Hardware layer: NVIDIA H100/H200/GB200 (Blackwell), AMD MI300X/MI355X; GPU memory optimization via Atero AI (acquired Aug 2025)
  • Software/Cloud layer: Crusoe Cloud — GPU IaaS, Managed Kubernetes, Managed Slurm, Managed Inference with MemoryAlloy technology; 99.98% uptime
⚡ The Pivotal Investment Question
Is Crusoe's energy-first vertical integration a durable structural cost moat — or is it a first-mover advantage that well-capitalized hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, Google) will replicate at scale, compressing Crusoe's pricing power just as its massive debt-financed campus buildout reaches peak leverage?
The market fear: Crusoe is building gigawatt campuses at peak AI capex cycle with project finance debt, at a moment when GPU prices are falling, customer concentration is high, and hyperscalers are starting to build their own energy-first campuses. The counter: Crusoe is 5+ years ahead in execution capability, has NVIDIA's full-stack partnership, and the AI compute demand so far exceeds supply that multi-tenant occupancy (now OpenAI, Oracle, AND Microsoft at Abilene) de-risks any single-customer dependency.

07Technology & Product Strategy
🔧 Two-Track Tech Strategy: Physical + Software
Crusoe is unusual in that it operates both a physical infrastructure tech stack (power, cooling, custom data center design) and a software/cloud stack (Crusoe Cloud). The physical differentiation is near-term (execution + energy cost); the software differentiation is the long-term moat that prevents Crusoe from being commoditized into a low-margin data center landlord.
Physical Infrastructure Tech
  • AI-Optimized Data Center Design: Purpose-built for dense GPU clusters — liquid cooling, optimized power density, RDMA networking for multi-GPU training. Not repurposed general-purpose data centers.
  • Power Peninsula™: Crusoe's proprietary energy sourcing framework — integrates behind-the-meter gas turbines, grid access, renewables, and battery storage to maximize reliability and minimize cost. Designed to bypass grid constraints entirely.
  • Crusoe Spark Modular: Partnership with Redwood Materials — modular AI data centers powered by second-life EV battery systems. Extends the energy-first model to smaller-scale, faster-deploy use cases.
  • GE Vernova partnership: Gas turbines for future AI factories — industrial-scale power generation capability.
  • Tallgrass partnership (Wyoming): Co2 sequestration adjacent to Project Jade — long-term carbon capture strategy alongside the gas-powered campus. Forward-looking ESG positioning.

13BAIT Framework Analysis
B — Behavioral
The "Picks & Shovels" Narrative Anchoring Problem
Investors in AI infrastructure suffer from two competing cognitive biases simultaneously. The "picks and shovels" narrative (you don't need to pick the winning AI model; just own the infrastructure they all need) creates FOMO-driven multiple expansion. But immediately after CoreWeave's 250% IPO surge then 60% correction, the market has price-anchored on that volatility. 🔑 The One Thing to Believe
The entire Crusoe bull case rests on a single physical constraint: the US electrical grid cannot add 156GW of AI-specific compute capacity by 2030 through traditional grid-interconnect data centers. The permitting timelines, transmission infrastructure, and utility capacity simply don't exist. Crusoe's behind-the-meter energy model — building its own power plants, not waiting for the grid — is not a cost optimization. It is the only way to deliver compute at the velocity AI demands. If you believe AI compute demand remains supply-constrained for the next 5–7 years (and all evidence suggests it will), then Crusoe's energy-bypass model is not a feature: it is the prerequisite for building at the required speed. That is the thesis.
