# Position Sizing Framework

Applied in Section 11 of every full equity thesis.
Governs how much to allocate and in what form (stock vs. options).

---

## Allocation Tiers

| Conviction | BAIT Overlap | Portfolio % | Form |
|------------|-------------|-------------|------|
| Very High | 3–4 factors | 8–12% | 70% stock / 30% options |
| High | 2–3 factors | 5–8% | 80% stock / 20% options |
| Medium | 1–2 factors | 2–5% | 90% stock / 10% options |
| Watch / Speculative | 0–1 factors | 0.5–2% | Options only or no position |

---

## Staged Entry Rules

Never enter full position at once. Use tranches triggered by price or events:

- **Tranche 1 (40% of target)** — Enter at current price once thesis is confirmed
- **Tranche 2 (35% of target)** — Add on first post-thesis dip of >8%, or next earnings confirmation
- **Tranche 3 (25% of target)** — Add if fundamental thesis strengthens materially (e.g., BAIT signal upgrades)

Abort rule: If stock falls >20% from Tranche 1 entry AND the fundamental reason is a thesis breach (not just market noise), exit and reassess.

---

## Options Overlay Guidelines

Use options only for the portion of allocation designated for options (see tiers above).

| Setup | Structure | When to Use |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| High conviction, defined catalyst | ITM calls (0.7–0.8 delta), 6–9 month expiry | Known earnings date or FDA decision |
| Medium conviction, need leverage | OTM calls (0.4–0.5 delta), 6–12 month expiry | BAIT double overlap, no defined catalyst |
| Hedge existing stock position | OTM puts, 3-month rolling | When valuation stretched or macro risk elevated |
| Income on rangebound holding | Covered calls | When stock at or near bull-case price target |

---

## Exit Discipline

- **Trim at Bull Case**: When stock reaches bull-case target, trim 30–50% of position
- **Full exit**: When thesis fundamentally breaks (not just price falls)
- **Reassess trigger**: Always define a specific price level in Section 14 that triggers mandatory reassessment
- **Never average down into a thesis breach** — averaging down into a narrative dip is fine; averaging into broken fundamentals is not
