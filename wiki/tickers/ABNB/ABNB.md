## Business Overview

Airbnb (NASDAQ: ABNB) is the dominant global alternative-accommodation marketplace — a pure two-sided platform connecting hosts with travelers in 220+ countries. Revenue is a take-rate on Gross Booking Value (GBV): Airbnb collects ≈14–15% of GBV in service fees (roughly 11–12% from guests + ≈3% from hosts). The model is capital-light: no inventory, no real estate, no hotel staff.

**Scale (Q1 2026 / FY2025)**:
- **$2.68B Q1 2026 revenue** (+18% YoY; 15% ex-FX); **$29.2B Q1 GBV** (+19% YoY; 13% ex-FX) — [Q1 2026 8-K / Shareholder Letter](https://s26.q4cdn.com/656283129/files/doc_financials/2026/q1/Q1-26-Shareholder-Letter.pdf)
- **156.2M nights & seats booked** in Q1 (+9% YoY); **ADR $187** (+9% YoY; +4% ex-FX)
- **$12.2B FY2025 revenue** (+12% YoY); **$4.6B FY2025 FCF** (38% margin); **TTM FCF $4.54B** (36% margin)
- **5M+ hosts** globally; cumulative **2.5B+ guest arrivals** lifetime — [10-K FY2024](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1559720/000155972025000010/abnb-20241231.htm)
- **61% of revenue international** — Latin America fastest-growing; Brazil top-5; APAC management-flagged forward vector

**Strategic evolution**: Brian Chesky's "Airbnb 2.0" platform rewrite (2024–2025) unlocked three new verticals alongside core Stays: **Experiences** (relaunched May 2025), **Hotels** (expanding in 2026, Chesky promises updates May 20), and **Services** (launched May 2025). The May 20, 2026 Summer Release is the next formal product moment. App adoption is accelerating — 63% of nights booked on app in Q1 2026 (up from 58% a year prior, +22% YoY app nights growth).

### Primary Source: 10-K Segment Detail (FY2024)

The FY2024 10-K confirms the structural shape of the business: **61% of revenue is generated from listings outside the United States**, with the platform supporting ≈20 local currencies and ≈8,200 employees plus ≈13,000 third-party support workers ([10-K FY2024](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1559720/000155972025000010/abnb-20241231.htm)). Three multi-year MD&A signals genuinely incremental to aggregator data:
- **Take-rate stability at ≈14–15%** has held through 2022–2025 despite multiple host-friction cycles — the market clearing rate is found, not pushed
- **Direct traffic dominance** (≈90% of bookings come direct, not via Google/OTA referral) — structural marketing-cost advantage vs. Booking.com (BKNG spends ≈$7B/yr on performance marketing)
- **Geographic mix shift**: International revenue grew faster than US in each of 2023, 2024, 2025 — Brazil promoted top-10 → top-5 in 2025; APAC the management-flagged forward vector

**Rating: Wide.**

**Vulnerabilities**:
- **Regulatory ceiling in dense urban markets**: NYC effectively eliminated STRs (Local Law 18, 2023); Barcelona phasing out by 2028; EU-wide STR rules effective May 2026
- **Booking.com encroachment**: BKNG's alternative-accommodations share grew 25% → 36% of its bookings 2019→2024
- **Take-rate ceiling**: Host fees are at or near the level the market tolerates without churn
- **Macro discretionary exposure**: Travel is recession-elastic; Middle East conflict already cited as ≈100bps Q2 headwind

### Competitive Landscape

The global alternative-accommodations market consolidated to a ≈71% Big Three share by 2024 ([Skift Research, 2025](https://skift.com/2025/03/14/short-term-rentals-airbnbs-dominance-and-bookings-gains-in-1-chart/)). Airbnb's ≈44% of the $183B short-term-rental revenue category is approximately the sum of the next two players combined.

| Competitor | Model | Approx. STR Share | Read on Moat / Threat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Airbnb (ABNB)** | Pure 2-sided STR + hotels + experiences | ≈44% (2024, +16pts vs. 2019) | Category-verb brand; ≈90% direct traffic; **only** scaled global pure-play |
| **Booking.com (BKNG)** | Multi-format OTA (hotels + alt-accom) | ≈18% (2024, +4pts vs. 2019) | Real and rising threat; $7B/yr performance-marketing; alt-accom 36% of bookings; strongest in EMEA |
| **Vrbo (Expedia / EXPE)** | Whole-home alt-accom only | ≈9% (2024, –2pts vs. 2019) | Losing share; family/whole-home niche; weaker brand outside US |
| **Trip.com (TCOM)** | Asia OTA | small ex-Asia | Dominant China; APAC corridor competitor |
| **Hotel chains (Marriott, Hilton)** | Substitute, not direct | n/a | Substitute for shorter trips; Airbnb's hotel expansion now competes on the booking surface |

**How Airbnb's moat differs**:
1. **Brand-verb status drives direct traffic** (≈90% direct vs. BKNG's heavy paid). Marketing intensity gap ≈13pts of revenue = structural pricing power.
2. **Cross-sell flywheel confirmed**: hotels→homes (55% re-book) and experiences→stays (25% add-on) validate the multi-vertical thesis at real scale.
3. **+16pt share gain (2019→2024)** within a structurally growing TAM — share gain *and* category expansion simultaneously.
4. **Honest tail risk**: BKNG is the well-capitalized #2 with broader inventory; if alt-accom matures to a duopoly, BKNG is the credible challenger. Share data currently favors Airbnb, but the gap is shrinking in EMEA.

## §7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Global travel TAM**: ≈$2T+ overall; lodging ≈$700B; alternative accommodations ≈$183B in 2024 growing share within lodging ([Skift Research](https://skift.com/2025/03/14/short-term-rentals-airbnbs-dominance-and-bookings-gains-in-1-chart/)). ABNB the dominant alt-accommodation brand and a top-3 global lodging brand by GBV.

**Structural dynamics**: Travel remains in a structural up-cycle since post-COVID. Alternative accommodation *share* of total lodging continues rising — consumer preference is genuine, not pandemic-cyclical. The "extended stay / work-from-anywhere" normalized behavior has moved into the platform's ADR and length-of-stay data. World Cup 2026 (US/Canada/Mexico) is a cyclical tailwind for NA supply + GBV in H1 2026.

**Regulatory environment**: Principal structural threat. Asymmetric across markets:
- **US**: NYC effectively closed STRs in 2023 (Local Law 18); state-level fee-disclosure rules under discussion
- **EU**: Barcelona phasing out tourist STRs by 2028; EU-wide STR data-sharing rules effective May 2026; Berlin, Amsterdam, Paris all impose caps/permit regimes
- **Italy**: Hundreds of millions of euro in settlements already paid
- **Pattern**: Patchwork compliance overhead caps growth in specific high-density urban markets. Dispersion across 220+ countries is the natural hedge

**Consumer macro**: Travel spend held up better than general discretionary in 2024–2025. Middle East conflict cited as ≈100bps Q2 2026 GBV headwind. FY2026 tariff-cycle cost pressures could shift mix toward domestic/budget travel — neutral-to-slight-positive for ABNB given alt-accom's price advantage over hotels.

**Currency**: 61% international revenue creates FX translation drag in strong-USD environments. Q1 2026 included ≈5pt FX tailwind to GBV (19% reported vs. 13% ex-FX) — organic growth somewhat below headline.
