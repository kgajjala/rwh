## Business Overview

Axcelis Technologies (NASDAQ: ACLS) designs, manufactures, and services **ion-implantation systems** — capital equipment that injects dopant atoms (boron, phosphorus, arsenic) into silicon wafers to alter electrical properties. Ion implantation is a non-substitutable step in front-end semiconductor fabrication.

- **Product platform**: Purion family — high-current, medium-current, and high-energy implant systems
- **End markets**: Power semiconductors (notably SiC), memory (DRAM, HBM), general mature-node logic
- **Revenue mix**: Systems ≈63% (Q1 2026: $126M / $199M); CS&I ≈37% (Q1 2026: $73M / $199M, ahead of expectations)
- **Scale (FY2025)**: $839M revenue (–17.6% YoY); $4.4B+ market cap; ≈30.7M shares; net-cash-positive

**Strategic transformation**: On October 1, 2025, ACLS and **Veeco Instruments (VECO)** announced an all-stock merger ([Axcelis IR](https://investor.axcelis.com/news-releases/news-release-details/axcelis-technologies-and-veeco-instruments-combine-creating)) — 0.3575 ACLS shares per VECO share; combined EV ≈$4.4B; ACLS shareholders ≈58% post-close. Stockholders of both companies approved the deal February 2026 ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/axcelis-stockholders-approve-merger-with-veeco-302680838.html)). **China SAMR is the only remaining approval** as of Q1 2026 call; expected close 2H 2026. Pro-forma FY2024: $1.7B revenue, ≈44% non-GAAP gross margin, $387M adjusted EBITDA — combined company becomes the fourth-largest US WFE supplier ([Semiconductor Today](https://www.semiconductor-today.com/news_items/2025/oct/veeco-axcelis-021025.shtml)). Russell Low (current ACLS CEO) leads the combined company.

### Competitive Landscape

The implant TAM (≈$2.5–3B) is structurally a duopoly: ACLS + AMAT account for the substantial majority, with small remainders at Sumitomo Heavy Industries and ULVAC. Within that duopoly, ACLS has carved out specialty leadership in SiC and high-energy implant. The Veeco merger expands the combined-co competitive set into broader WFE — placing it in a different peer comparison vs. LRCX/KLAC/AMAT once close completes.

| Peer | Equipment Niche | Approx Revenue (FY25 est) | Read on Threat to ACLS |
|------|----------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| **AMAT** (Applied Materials) | Broad WFE; implant is one tool line | ≈$28B | Direct implant competitor; Varian implant division; cross-tool selling at scale; has not displaced ACLS in SiC despite years of attempts |
| **ASML** | EUV / DUV lithography | ≈$31B | Different process step; not a direct competitor |
| **LRCX** (Lam Research) | Etch / deposition | ≈$18B | Different process step; potential partner |
| **KLAC** | Process control / metrology | ≈$11B | Different process step; not direct |
| **TEL** (Tokyo Electron) | Coater/developer/etch | ≈$15B | Different process step; not direct |
| **Sumitomo / ULVAC** | Implant remainders | Small | Marginal share; no sustained challenge |
| **VECO** (Veeco — soon-to-be-combined) | MOCVD / laser annealing / wet etch / IBD | ≈$700M FY2024 | Pre-merger complementary customer set; not competing on implant |

**How ACLS's moat differs vs. AMAT (the only real implant competitor)**: ACLS has protected SiC implant share in the face of AMAT's broader WFE heft for over a decade. The differentiator is focus + customer-qualification depth: SiC implant requires high-energy capability that AMAT's Varian mainline implant tool was not optimized for. AMAT has entered SiC implant and has not shifted share materially. The tail risk remains that AMAT decides to materially invest in winning SiC share — Q1 2026 call had no new commentary on competitive dynamics here. LRCX/KLAC are different process steps; the combined ACLS+Veeco does not aim to compete with these scale-leaders.

### Moat Vulnerabilities

- **Cyclicality**: 30%+ revenue swings (FY23 $1.13B → FY25 $839M = –26% peak-to-trough)
- **AMAT competition**: Scale and cross-tool leverage remain a long-run threat if AMAT commits to SiC
- **China geopolitical risk**: 40% Q1 revenue; export-control evolution ongoing
- **Veeco integration risk**: Two product cultures merging; dis-synergies are a real possibility
- **Gross margin fragility**: Q1 2026 40.7% shows mix-driven vulnerability when SiC is depressed
