## Business Overview

Booking Holdings (NASDAQ: BKNG) is the world's largest online travel agency by gross bookings, operating a portfolio of global travel platforms: **Booking.com** (accommodations, global market leader), **Priceline** (discount travel, U.S.-centric), **Agoda** (Asia-Pacific accommodations), **Kayak** (travel search/metasearch), **OpenTable** (restaurant reservations), and **Rentalcars.com** (car rentals). The core business connects travelers with accommodation suppliers (hotels, vacation rentals, apartments) and, increasingly, with airlines, car rentals, and attractions through the "Connected Trip" strategy.

Booking.com alone lists 3M+ accommodation properties across 220+ countries. ~90% of revenue is non-U.S. (Europe-heavy mix). Revenue is generated via agency commissions (paid after stay) and merchant fees (payment collected at booking, then remitted to supplier), with merchant rapidly becoming dominant (~70% of gross bookings in 2025). FY2025 revenue was $26.9B with ~$9.1B in FCF — among the highest-quality cash machines in the S&P 500.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding Insight**: Travel is the world's largest discretionary spend category. Matching 3M+ accommodation properties across 220 countries with hundreds of millions of travelers is an impossible coordination problem without an intermediary. Booking.com (founded in Amsterdam, 1996; acquired by Priceline for $133M in 2005) became the world's dominant accommodation marketplace by solving this problem online first and at the greatest scale.

**Current Strategic Vision**: Become the "Connected Trip" — the single platform where a traveler books and manages every element of a trip (accommodation, flight, car, attraction, restaurant). This reduces dependency on any single vertical and builds recurring relationships beyond a single hotel booking.

**Pivotal question**: Does AI disintermediate Booking, or amplify it? The market prices in moderate AI risk (stock is –23% off post-split peak). Management's answer: invest $700M in 2026 into GenAI, Connected Trip, and payments to become the AI answer — not the AI casualty.

4. **Interest Rates** (secondary): Merchant model creates large cash float (advance bookings). Higher rates lift interest income on float; lower rates compress it.
