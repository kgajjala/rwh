## Business Description

Booking Holdings is the world's largest online travel agency (OTA) by gross bookings,
operating a portfolio of global travel platforms: **Booking.com** (accommodations, global
market leader), **Priceline** (discount travel, U.S.-centric), **Agoda** (Asia-Pacific
accommodations), **Kayak** (travel search/metasearch), **OpenTable** (restaurant
reservations), and **Rentalcars.com** (car rentals).

The core business is connecting travelers with accommodation suppliers (hotels, vacation
rentals, apartments) and, increasingly, with airlines, car rentals, and attractions — the
"Connected Trip" strategy. Revenue is generated via agency commissions (paid after stay)
and merchant fees (payment collected at booking, then remitted to supplier), with merchant
rapidly becoming dominant (~70% of gross bookings in 2025).

Booking.com alone lists 3M+ accommodation properties in 220+ countries. The company
operates predominantly in Europe and international markets (~90% of revenue is non-U.S.).

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **Does AI disintermediate Booking (travelers bypass the OTA via AI agents) —
> or does AI amplify Booking's value (fulfillment complexity, supplier relationships,
> and payment rails make Booking MORE necessary in an AI-assisted booking world)?**

The fear: AI assistants (ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity) become the travel
planning layer, cutting out OTAs. The counter-thesis: fulfillment, customer support,
and the inventory aggregation problem is unsolvable for LLMs without Booking's pipes.
Management has invested $700M in 2026 to make Booking the beneficiary, not the casualty.

## Moat Rating

- **Rating**: Wide
- **Sources**:
  - Two-sided network effects: 3M+ properties × 1.2B+ room nights/year flywheel
  - Switching costs: Loyalty (Genius tiers), merchant payment rails, supplier API integrations
  - Scale advantages: Unit economics (CAC/LTV) unmatched at this volume; $9B+ FCF to reinvest
  - Brand: Booking.com is the default travel search verb in Europe; SEO moat
  - Data advantage: 1.2B annual room night data set trains pricing, fraud, and recommendation models
