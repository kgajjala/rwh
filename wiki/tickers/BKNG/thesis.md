## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Question

**Founding Insight**: Travel is the world's largest discretionary spend category. The
friction: matching 3M+ accommodation properties across 220 countries with hundreds of
millions of travelers is an impossible coordination problem without an intermediary.
Booking.com (founded in Amsterdam, 1996; acquired by Priceline for $133M in 2005) became
the world's dominant accommodation marketplace by solving this problem online first and at
the greatest scale.

**Brands**: Booking.com (global accommodations), Priceline (U.S. discounts), Agoda
(APAC), Kayak (metasearch), OpenTable (restaurants), Rentalcars.com

**Current Strategic Vision**: Become the "Connected Trip" — the single platform where a
traveler books and manages every element of a trip (accommodation, flight, car, attraction,
restaurant). This reduces the OTA's dependency on any one vertical and builds a recurring
relationship with travelers beyond the single hotel booking.

**The Pivotal Investment Question**:
> Does AI disintermediate Booking (travelers bypass OTAs by querying AI agents directly)
> — or does AI amplify Booking (fulfillment complexity, payment rails, inventory aggregation
> make Booking MORE necessary as the AI booking layer)?

The market prices in moderate AI risk (stock is 28% off peak). Management's answer: invest
$700M in 2026 into GenAI, Connected Trip, and payments to become the AI answer — not the
AI casualty.

## Section 5 — Competitive Moat

**Rating: Wide**

**Genuine Moat Sources**:
- **Network effects**: 3M+ properties × 1.235B room nights creates flywheel. More properties
  attract travelers; more travelers attract properties. Neither side can leave without losing
  the other. This two-sided market is structurally self-reinforcing.
- **Switching costs**: Genius loyalty program (3 tiers, exclusive discounts), stored payment
  methods, saved preferences, property manager API integrations all create friction
- **Cost advantages at scale**: $9B+ FCF deployed on marketing at cost-per-acquisition that
  smaller OTAs cannot match; Booking.com SEO moat built over 25 years
- **Data moat**: 1.2B+ annual room nights generates pricing, demand, and cancellation data
  no competitor can replicate
- **Brand**: "Booking.com" is the default travel search verb in continental Europe;
  Agoda is #1 or #2 brand awareness in SEA

**Competitive Threats**:
- Google Hotels: using search dominance to surface direct booking options (risk: ongoing,
  moderated by Google's own travel supply issues)
- Airbnb (ABNB): competing in alt-accommodations; not traditional hotels
- AI assistants: the existential debate (see Pivotal Question)
- Direct hotel booking apps: Marriott Bonvoy, Hilton Honors have loyalty bypass risk

**Moat Verdict**: The network + scale combination creates durable advantages. The AI risk
is real but management is investing to be the fulfillment layer, not just the discovery layer.

## Section 8 — Strategic Growth Initiatives

1. **GenAI / Agentic Travel**: Natural language search, AI trip planner, voice-activated
   booking. $700M 2026 reinvestment partially directed here. Converts BKNG from a search
   destination to an AI agent with fulfillment capability.

2. **Connected Trip**: Packaging accommodation + flight + car + attraction into a single
   booking flow. Already ~10% of transactions, growing ~high-20s%. Each additional vertical
   booked increases LTV and reduces competitor intercept risk.

3. **Merchant Model Expansion**: 70% merchant mix in 2025 vs. 25% in 2021. Enables BKNG to
   capture payment float, reduce cancellation economics, and build fintech products (BNPL
   for travel, payments infrastructure).

4. **Asia Growth**: Low-double-digit room night growth in Asia in 2025. Agoda is the APAC
   platform. Asia is underpenetrated vs. BKNG's European dominance — multi-decade runway.

5. **Alternative Accommodations**: 19% room night growth in Q4 2024. Competing with Airbnb
   by listing homes, apartments, and unique stays on Booking.com. Supply is catching up.

6. **Loyalty Program (Genius)**: 3-tier exclusive discount system creates repeat purchase
   incentive. Data flywheel from loyal customers improves personalization → higher conversion.

7. **Attractions**: Ticket and experience bookings grew ~80% in 2025. Still small but the
   fastest-growing Connected Trip vertical.

8. **Sustainability / Net-Zero 2040**: Booking.com has committed to net-zero. Not a short-term
   financial driver, but institutional ESG flows are a tailwind.

## Section 10 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Primary macro drivers for BKNG**:

1. **Global Travel Demand** (dominant driver):
   - International travel volumes recovered above pre-COVID levels in 2024
   - Room nights grew 8% in 2025 (moderation from 25-30% post-COVID recovery bounce)
   - Structural growth driver: global middle class expansion in Asia adds 50-100M new
     international travelers per decade
   - Sensitivity: a global recession (2SD negative GDP shock) historically cuts BKNG room
     nights 30-50% (COVID: –50%). The model is cyclical.

2. **Average Daily Rates (ADRs)**:
   - Hotel ADR growth has been the pricing tailwind post-COVID
   - Management has noted ADR normalization; mix shift toward Asia (lower ADR) may moderate
   - Take rate expansion (merchant vs. agency) offsets ADR normalization

3. **FX (EUR/USD)**:
   - ~79% of revenue reported from Netherlands operations
   - FY2024/2025 had modest FX headwinds; management guides ex-FX growth
   - 1pp EUR/USD move ≈ ~$100-150M revenue impact [Estimate]

4. **Interest Rates** (secondary):
   - Merchant model creates large cash float (advance bookings)
   - Rising rates increase interest income on this float; falling rates compress it
