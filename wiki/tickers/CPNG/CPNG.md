## Business Overview

Coupang (NYSE: CPNG) is the dominant e-commerce platform in South Korea — frequently described as "the Amazon of Korea" — operating an end-to-end logistics network ("Rocket Delivery," primarily next-day and same-day) that covers ~70% of Korea's population within 7 miles of a Coupang fulfillment center. **FY2025 revenue was $34.5B (+14% YoY)**, with **$1.5B FY2025 adjusted EBITDA**. The company has **~14M Rocket WOW members in Korea** (the loyalty/Prime equivalent), each spending approximately 3× more than non-members.

The business operates two reportable segments: **Product Commerce** (Korea-based Rocket Delivery + 3P marketplace + Coupang Eats restaurant delivery + Coupang Play streaming + Korean fintech) and **Developing Offerings** (Taiwan rocket delivery expansion + **Farfetch** luxury marketplace, acquired late 2023 for ~$500M / ~$1B with debt commitment + restaurant delivery in Taiwan + emerging fintech). Q4 2025 revenue was $8.8B (+11% reported, +14% constant currency). Q4 2025 operating income was a **slim $8M** — reflecting management's deliberate reinvestment phase across Taiwan, Farfetch, and content investments.

The strategic thesis: Coupang has built infrastructure (warehouse + fleet + tech) that is irreplicable in Korea and is now in the early innings of margin expansion as Korea matures and Developing Offerings either scale or get curtailed. Korean operating margin has been expanding 30–50 bps annually; Developing Offerings has been a deliberate drag while Taiwan and Farfetch ramp.

## Moat Assessment

**Wide (in Korea)** — logistics infrastructure + WOW membership + scale; among the strongest moats in international e-commerce.

**Sources**:
- **Logistics network**: ~30+ fulfillment centers in Korea covering 70%+ of population within 7-mile radius; same-day "Rocket Fresh" delivery; "Dawn Delivery" (order by midnight, receive by 7am) — capabilities that no Korean competitor has replicated
- **WOW membership**: ~14M subscribers in Korea (out of ~20M households) — extraordinary household penetration; 3× spend vs. non-members; 90%+ retention
- **Geographic concentration**: Korea is one of the most densely populated developed countries — Coupang's network is operationally optimal in a way that wouldn't translate well to larger geographies (e.g., U.S.) but is uniquely deep in Korea
- **Korean e-commerce scale**: ~25–30% market share of Korean retail e-commerce; #1 in nearly every category
- **Vertical integration**: Owns most of the technology stack, fulfillment, and last-mile delivery (vs. Amazon, which uses USPS/UPS/FedEx more in U.S.)

**Vulnerabilities**:
- **Single-country exposure**: ~85%+ of revenue is South Korea; demographic decline (Korea is one of fastest-aging populations globally) is a structural headwind
- **Currency / FX risk**: USD-reporting; KRW weakness compresses dollar revenue
- **Farfetch integration risk**: $500M acquired asset in luxury e-commerce (different model, different geography); tracks toward break-even but risk of write-down remains
- **Taiwan competitive intensity**: Shopee (Sea Limited) is a credible incumbent; Coupang's expansion is unproven economically
- **Regulatory risk in Korea**: Antitrust and labor enforcement — the Korea Fair Trade Commission has been active

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Bom Kim, 2010)**: Korea's e-commerce market was fragmented, slow (3–5 day delivery typical), and dominated by listing-marketplace models. A vertically integrated platform — owning warehouses, trucks, technology — could deliver same-day / next-day at scale and would compound a customer-experience advantage that fragmented players couldn't match. Korea's geographic density made this economically possible in a way the U.S. couldn't replicate cheaply.

**Today**: Coupang is the dominant e-commerce platform in Korea with a logistics network that no domestic competitor has replicated. The WOW membership has reached ~14M (out of ~20M Korean households — extraordinary penetration). Korean operating margin has begun expanding 30–50 bps annually after years of deliberate investment. Developing Offerings (Taiwan + Farfetch + Eats + Play + fintech) is the optionality layer.

**Pivotal question**: When (not if) does the Korean operating margin expansion become visible enough to drive a multiple re-rate from ~10× FY26E EV/EBITDA toward ~16–18×?

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Korean e-commerce TAM**: ~$160B in retail e-commerce (Korea is #5 globally per capita); expected to grow ~6–8% annually. Coupang at 25–30% share = ~$45–50B addressable just in current category — meaningful headroom even on Korean-only TAM.

**Korean retail penetration**: E-commerce ~38% of total retail in Korea (one of the highest globally) — but Coupang's 25–30% share of e-commerce can grow toward 35%+.

**Taiwan e-commerce TAM**: ~$25B; Shopee (Sea Ltd) is the incumbent. Coupang attempting to differentiate on logistics speed.

**Luxury e-commerce (Farfetch TAM)**: ~$60B globally; bifurcating between marketplaces (Farfetch) and direct-to-consumer brand sites; Farfetch is in turnaround mode.

**Demographic context**: Korea has the world's lowest fertility rate (~0.78) and one of the fastest-aging populations. By 2035, Korean working-age population shrinks materially. Long-term, this caps Korean retail TAM growth and makes international expansion strategically urgent.

**Regulatory environment**: Korean Fair Trade Commission (KFTC) has actively investigated Coupang for self-preferencing, marketplace fees, and labor practices. Periodic fines but no structural remedy yet.
