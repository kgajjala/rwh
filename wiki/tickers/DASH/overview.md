# DASH — DoorDash

## Business Description

DoorDash (NYSE: DASH) is the dominant U.S. food delivery marketplace, operating a three-sided platform connecting consumers, merchants (restaurants, grocers, retailers), and delivery drivers ("Dashers"). Through the 2025 acquisitions of Deliveroo (£2.9B, closed Oct 2025) and SevenRooms (~$1.2B), DoorDash is evolving from food delivery into a broader local commerce operating system.

**Core revenue streams:**
- **Marketplace commissions** — Typically 15-30% of order value charged to merchants; core revenue engine
- **Consumer fees** — Delivery fees, service fees, small-order fees; partially offset for DashPass subscribers ($9.99/month)
- **Advertising** — High-margin promoted listings, sponsored placements, banner ads for merchants; growing rapidly (Amazon advertising analog)
- **DashMart / white-label logistics** — DoorDash Drive and Wolt Drive for merchants wanting logistics without marketplace listing
- **SevenRooms** — Restaurant CRM, reservations, and guest management software; deepens merchant relationship beyond delivery

**Operating brands:** DoorDash, Wolt, Deliveroo, DashMart, DoorDash Drive, SevenRooms

## Moat Assessment

**Narrow (Network Effects + Scale; requires continuous reinvestment)**

Sources of competitive advantage:
- **Three-sided network effect**: Largest consumer base (~56M MAU), most merchants (~590K partners), most drivers (~8M Dashers in 2024) — the flywheel is self-reinforcing
- **U.S. market share dominance**: 60-67% of U.S. food delivery market share — roughly 3× Uber Eats and 6× Grubhub; stable and slightly increasing since 2020
- **DashPass subscriber lock-in**: 35M+ subscribers across DashPass/Wolt+/Deliveroo Plus at year-end 2025; behavioral loyalty mirrors Amazon Prime effect
- **"Deep Red" AI logistics system**: Years of accumulated routing, delivery time, and Dasher matching data creates operational advantage difficult to replicate

**Moat limitations**:
- Low switching costs for consumers — most will multi-home across apps for best prices/promos
- Uber Eats is a formidable #2 with cross-platform bundling advantage (Uber One: rides + delivery, 46M members)
- Commodity-like core service — food arrives the same way regardless of platform
- Requires continuous reinvestment to defend; no "cash cow" characteristics

## Pivotal Investment Question

**Is DoorDash building a durable local commerce platform with improving margins, or is it a dominant but structurally challenged marketplace that requires perpetual capital infusion to defend its position?**

The bull answer: Advertising monetization (Amazon-style), international scale via Deliveroo + Wolt, grocery/retail expansion, and autonomous delivery will expand EBITDA margins from ~2% of GOV today to 4-5% over time — making the current valuation justified.

The bear answer: Food delivery is not a high-margin, high-moat business; the market is paying 28x trailing EBITDA for a business where Buffett and Munger would want a much wider margin of safety, and a $130 probability-weighted expected value vs. $176 current price implies the stock is already fairly-to-slightly-overvalued.
