## 1. Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight**: Restaurant takeout and delivery was fragmented, expensive, and slow. DoorDash built the logistics infrastructure and marketplace to commoditize local delivery, making it economically viable at scale. The company is now extending the same logistics stack to grocery, retail, convenience, and pharmacy — becoming the operating system for local commerce.

**Pivotal question**: Is DoorDash building a durable, high-margin local commerce platform, or is it the dominant player in a structurally difficult, low-margin industry that requires perpetual capital infusion?

**The market paradox**: At $176 (38% off the 52-week high), the stock appears "on sale." But the probability-weighted expected value using reasonable 5-year scenarios is ~$130 — suggesting the stock may still be slightly overvalued even after the selloff. The Buffett framing: this is a "good business at a premium price" rather than a "wonderful business at a fair price."

## 4. Revenue Mix & Business Model

**Revenue streams**:
- Marketplace commissions (15-30% of order value): ~65-70% of revenue
- Consumer fees: ~15-20%
- Advertising: ~5-10% (fastest-growing; near-pure-profit margin)
- DashMart/Wolt Drive/white-label: ~5%
- SevenRooms (acquired 2025): small contributor; restaurant CRM/SaaS growth

**Gross Order Value (GOV)**: ~$100B+ in FY2025 across DoorDash, Wolt, and Deliveroo
**EBITDA margin as % of GOV**: ~2.5% in FY2025; targeting 4-5% long-term
**Adj EBITDA margin as % of revenue**: ~20% in FY2025

**Business model evolution**: From food delivery to local commerce (grocery, retail, convenience, alcohol, pharmacy) to restaurant operating system (SevenRooms) to autonomous delivery (Dot robot; Serve Robotics partnership).

## 5. Competitive Moat

**Rating: Narrow (network effects + scale; requires continuous defense)**

| Moat Source | Strength | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| Three-sided network effect | Strong | 56M MAU + 590K merchants + 8M Dashers; largest in U.S. |
| U.S. market share dominance | Strong | 60-67% share; 3× Uber Eats; widening since 2020 |
| DashPass/subscription lock-in | Moderate | 35M subscribers; higher order frequency, lower churn |
| Deep Red AI logistics | Moderate | Years of routing data; operational advantage in ETA accuracy |
| Advertising flywheel | Moderate (growing) | High-margin layer maturing; mirrors Amazon advertising evolution |

**Moat vulnerabilities**:
- Uber One (rides + delivery bundle, 46M members) is a credible alternative in key metros
- Consumer multi-homing is common — price/promo sensitivity limits true lock-in
- Regulatory risk: city fee caps, federal gig worker reclassification (AB5-style) would destroy unit economics
- Munger concern: DoorDash has never faced a sustained price war from a well-capitalized competitor; if Amazon/Uber decided to aggressively subsidize, margins could evaporate

## 6. Management & Leadership

**Tony Xu (Co-Founder, CEO)**: The dominant force. Long-term oriented — willing to sacrifice short-term earnings for platform investment (the November 2025 selloff was triggered by his "several hundred million in incremental 2026 spend" announcement). Sold ~$218M via routine 10b5-1 plan sales in trailing 6 months — large in absolute terms.

**Board director Alfred Lin** (a16z): Purchased ~$100M — a positive insider signal that partially offsets CEO selling.

**Capital allocation philosophy**: Aggressive — $5.1B in acquisitions in 2025 (Deliveroo + SevenRooms), $5B buyback authorized but $0 executed. The non-execution of the buyback at a 38% discount to recent highs is a signal: management either sees better uses for cash (M&A) or is waiting for lower prices. Buffett would note this as a potential red flag on capital allocation discipline.

**$5B buyback caveat**: At $176, the buyback could retire ~6.5% of shares. The decision not to execute it yet, after the 38% selloff, is ambiguous.

## 7. Strategic Growth Initiatives

1. **International expansion via Deliveroo**: £2.0B standalone revenue; $200M adj EBITDA contribution targeted for 2026; applies Wolt playbook to Western Europe and Middle East; combined with Wolt covers ~1 billion people in 40+ countries

2. **Advertising monetization**: Growing toward Amazon-like advertising flywheel; near-pure-profit layer; significant margin expansion lever as marketplace matures

3. **Non-restaurant verticals (grocery, retail, pharmacy)**: Approaching positive unit economics by H2 2026; TAM expansion from ~$300B restaurant delivery to ~$1T+ local convenience economy

4. **Autonomous delivery (Project Dot)**: In-house robot "Dot" launched late 2025; Serve Robotics partnership; long-term structural reduction in Dasher cost per order

5. **SevenRooms**: Restaurant CRM, reservations, loyalty — moves DoorDash beyond delivery into the full restaurant operating system; increases merchant stickiness and data depth

6. **New unified global tech platform**: "Several hundred million" in 2026 investment; consolidating Wolt + Deliveroo + DoorDash on one tech stack; one-time cost to unlock long-term operational efficiency

## 9. Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Food delivery market sizing**:
- U.S. restaurant delivery TAM: ~$120-150B annually; DoorDash at 60-67% share + $100B+ GOV is approaching saturation penetration
- Global food delivery: ~$300-400B; DoorDash/Wolt/Deliveroo addresses significant portion of Western markets
- Local convenience beyond food: ~$1T+ TAM per DoorDash management; grocery/retail/pharmacy are the growth frontier

**Industry dynamics**:
- Market has consolidated from 4+ major players to essentially a DoorDash/Uber Eats duopoly in the U.S.
- Unit economics have improved dramatically as scale increased — DoorDash went from -$3 per order to slightly positive
- Dasher costs per order are the primary variable expense; autonomous delivery is the long-term solution

**Consumer discretionary risk**: Food delivery is a "nice to have" — in a recession, consumers cook at home. The March 2020 COVID surge proved delivery can spike; a deep recession would test the base order frequency.

**Regulatory environment**: New York City (15% commission cap), San Francisco (20% cap) are precedent-setters. Federal gig worker legislation is the existential risk — converting Dashers to W-2 employees would require minimum wage + benefits, potentially adding $3-5/delivery in labor costs.

## 12. BAIT Framework

### B — Behavioral: MODERATE
The -38% decline from the 52-week high after Q3 2025 (which missed EPS estimates) and Q1 2026 guidance that undershot consensus created meaningful negative sentiment. However, the behavioral signal is less clean than BKNG or RKT: (1) Q4 2025 beat was strong but DoorDash has a pattern of under-guiding then beating, so the market is now partially discounting beats; (2) the stock was at a genuinely elevated valuation ($286) before the selloff — this is a partial mean reversion, not pure behavioral panic on a healthy stock.

### A — Analytical: MODERATE
Key analytical edges:
- SBC declining from $2.6B to $1.3-1.4B in 2026 is a huge improvement in earnings quality that P/E comparisons don't fully reflect
- "Several hundred million" incremental 2026 investment is one-time opex, not a permanent margin impairment
- Deliveroo's $200M EBITDA contribution in 2026 is underweighted in current consensus models
- Advertising revenue growth rate is underappreciated in margin expansion models

But limits: The analysis' own 5-year scenario PW EV (~$130) is below the current price ($176), suggesting analytical work doesn't resolve to a clear mispricing.

### I — Informational: MODERATE
Key primary disclosures that beat media coverage:
- Q4 2025 beat was strong (orders +32%, EBITDA $780M) but post-earnings recovery was short-lived
- CFO guidance of H2 2026 being "significantly stronger than H1" — market is focused on near-term miss
- Deliveroo Q4 contribution slightly exceeded the $45M target — integration tracking ahead of plan
- Alfred Lin (a16z board member) purchased ~$100M — positive signal that got little media attention

### T — Technical: WEAK
- $5B buyback authorized but $0 executed — no mechanical floor under the stock
- Stock at 38% below 52-week high but no clear technical catalyst in the near term
- No index recomposition or split catalyst
- Q1 2026 earnings will be the next hard catalyst (May 2026)

### BAIT Verdict: Double Overlap (B+A) — Moderate Conviction, Below Intrinsic Value Threshold
The behavioral overselling from Q3 miss and investment spending announcement is real. The analytical complexity (SBC, Deliveroo contribution, H2 seasonality) creates some edge. But the 5-year scenario analysis suggests the stock is fairly-to-slightly-overvalued at $176 even after the selloff. The BAIT signal here is weaker than RKT (which has a cleaner behavioral mispricing). Conviction: Low-Moderate. Best entry: $140-150 or below.

## 15. Bottom Line

**DASH is a Watch/Cautious Starter position — compelling business, questionable valuation at current prices.**

The thesis in one sentence: DoorDash has the dominant local commerce marketplace in the U.S. with a growing platform of advertising, grocery, and international verticals — but paying 28x trailing EBITDA for this position requires projecting many years of aggressive growth and margin expansion that may or may not materialize.

**What Buffett would say**: "I admire the business, but I don't have enough confidence in what this looks like in 10 years to pay 33x EBITDA for it." The business lacks the natural margin structure of Coca-Cola or See's Candies — it requires continuous reinvestment.

**1-year target**: N/A from source analysis (5-year scenarios only). Analyst consensus ~$277-280 implies +57% upside — but consensus was also $280+ before the 38% selloff.
**3-year base case**: ~$130-150 range based on linear interpolation of 5-year scenarios
**Action**: Watch. Start 1% starter position at current $160-180 if high conviction; full 2% position at $120-140 where margin of safety is adequate.
**Verdict**: Watch / Cautious Starter (not a strong buy at current prices; better entry exists on further weakness)

**Analyst consensus comparison**: Strong Buy (34B/10H/0S), median target $277-280. The significant premium of consensus to PW EV analysis suggests analysts are more optimistic about margin expansion and international success than a base case warrants.
