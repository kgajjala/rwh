## Business Overview

eBay (NASDAQ: EBAY) is a global online marketplace connecting buyers and sellers across categories with a particular emphasis on used, refurbished, vintage, and collectible "non-new-in-box" inventory — the segments least directly competed by Amazon or Shopify-powered DTC. FY2025 revenue was $11.1B (+8% YoY) and Gross Merchandise Volume (GMV) was $79.6B (+7% YoY) ([eBay Q4 2025 earnings release, 2026-02-18](https://investors.ebayinc.com/financial-information/quarterly-results/)). Q4 2025 revenue grew 15% (13% FX-neutral) and GMV grew 10% (8% FX-neutral) — the strongest quarterly growth in years.

The business has reaccelerated after a multi-year decline period (2018–2023) via a strategic pivot into **focus categories**: trading cards (1Sansone-Pollack acquisition + Goldin), motors parts & accessories, luxury (handbags, watches via Authenticate), refurbished electronics, and pre-owned apparel (Depop acquisition). The 2024–2025 "AI-first marketplace" investment cycle (magical listing, AI search, image-based discovery) is now translating to GMV reacceleration. Q1 2026 guidance is for GMV $21.5–21.9B (+10–12% FX-neutral) and revenue $3.00–3.05B (+13–15% FX-neutral) — confirming sustained reacceleration.

Capital return is aggressive: $2B incremental buyback authorization announced with Q4 2025 earnings, dividend raised 7% to $0.31/quarter (March 2026 payment). Combined buyback + dividend yield is meaningfully high for a tech platform name. Recent insider activity has been net selling (66,910 shares sold trailing 90 days) — consistent with executive 10b5-1 patterns following stock appreciation.

## Moat Assessment

**Narrow** — durable network effects in non-new-in-box / collectible categories where Amazon's "everything store" advantage is weakest, but mainstream e-commerce continues to compete for the "long tail."

**Sources**:
- **Category leadership in collectibles / non-NIB**: Sports cards, vintage, luxury authentication, refurbished — the segments least vulnerable to Amazon
- **Trust/authentication infrastructure**: eBay Authenticity Guarantee for sneakers, handbags, watches, trading cards — competitive moat in luxury/collectibles
- **Two-sided seller base**: ~18M+ active sellers; international marketplace network
- **Depop**: Gen Z-targeted resale; aligned with circular economy thesis
- **Goldin acquisition**: Premium auction platform for sports cards / collectibles
- **Capital-light economics**: 25–30% adj EBITDA margins; high FCF conversion

**Vulnerabilities**:
- **Amazon and Shopify**: Dominant in new-in-box mainstream e-commerce; eBay loses share each year in this segment
- **Walmart / Target online + Marketplace**: Encroaching on lower price points
- **Facebook Marketplace + Instagram Shop**: Free, social-driven, growing in C2C
- **Mercari, Poshmark, Depop competition**: Fragmenting C2C resale
- **Buyer demographics aging**: eBay buyer base skews older than younger marketplaces
- **GMV reacceleration sustainability**: Recent Q4 +10% GMV growth could be one-time tailwind from AI features + macro
- **PayPal divorce legacy**: Historically benefited from PayPal lock-in; that advantage is gone

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Has eBay successfully pivoted from a declining horizontal e-commerce site into a structurally-growing collectibles + circular-economy marketplace, justifying an inflection re-rate from the historical 11–13× P/E to 16–20× — or is the recent Q4 2025 +10% GMV reacceleration an AI-feature-driven one-off that will fade in 2026–2027?

The bull case requires (a) AI-first product investments to deliver durable buyer engagement, (b) focus categories (cards, luxury, refurb, Depop) to grow >20%, (c) margin expansion through advertising / payments monetization, and (d) capital return (~10%+ combined yield) supporting equity through any GMV deceleration. The bear case argues eBay's core marketplace continues structural decline, focus categories cannot offset the base, and the $97 stock has already priced in the inflection.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Pierre Omidyar, 1995)**: Online auctions could create perfect-information markets for hard-to-value, unique, or vintage items — items the new "everything store" (early Amazon) was poorly suited for. Trust mechanisms (feedback ratings) plus payment integration (later PayPal) would solve the C2C friction problem.

**Today**: eBay has refocused around the original insight — non-new-in-box, collectibles, vintage, refurbished, luxury — where the auction/best-offer model + authentication infrastructure + trust system create durable value. The horizontal "everything-marketplace" ambition has been ceded to Amazon. The strategic question is whether the focused-category model is a structural growth business or simply a slower decline.

**Pivotal question**: Is the Q4 2025 +10% GMV growth print + Q1 2026 +10–12% guide a real inflection (focus categories + AI features + Depop integration finally compounding) or a temporary uplift before a return to LSD growth in core marketplace?

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**TAM**: Global e-commerce ~$6T+; non-new-in-box / resale / collectibles ~$200–400B near-term, expanding with circular economy + Gen Z preferences. eBay's $80B GMV captures a meaningful share of the focused subset.

**Structural dynamics**: Resale / circular economy has tailwinds — environmental, generational, value-driven. Collectibles (sports cards, vintage, luxury watches/handbags) saw a major bull cycle 2020–2022, with volumes now stabilizing at elevated levels. Authentication-as-a-service is a recurring competitive battlefield (StockX, GOAT, Fanatics in cards).

**Competitive intensity**: Amazon dominates mainstream; specialists (StockX, Fanatics, Mercari, Poshmark, Depop, Vinted) fragment C2C; eBay's wedge is the breadth + authentication infrastructure. Fanatics buying Topps and pushing into collectibles is the most strategic 2024–2026 competitive risk in a high-margin focus category.

**Macro**: Consumer discretionary spending is mixed; resale tends to be counter-cyclical (recession-friendly).
