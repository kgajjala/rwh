## Business Overview

Figma (NYSE: FIG) is the leading collaborative design and prototyping platform for digital products, used by ~95% of Fortune 500 design teams. The product began as a browser-based UX/UI design tool and has expanded into a multi-product collaborative-creation platform: Figma Design, FigJam (whiteboarding), Dev Mode (developer handoff), Figma Slides, and **Figma Make** (AI-assisted code/design generation, launched 2025). FY2025 (calendar year) revenue was **$1.056B (+41% YoY)** with Q4 2025 revenue of **$303.8M (+40%)** — the company crossed the $1B annual revenue threshold for the first time ([Figma Q4 2025 release, IR 2026 Feb](https://investors.figma.com/news-releases)).

The company famously had a $20B acquisition by Adobe announced in 2022 that was abandoned in December 2023 due to regulatory pressure (UK CMA, EU). Figma went public July 31, 2025 at $33/share, surged to a peak of $142.92 first-day, and has subsequently fallen to **$17.47** as of April 24, 2026 — a peak-to-current decline of **–88%** and substantially below the $33 IPO price. The drawdown reflects (a) staggered lock-up expirations with 54.1% of shares under extended lock-ups expiring in tranches through August 2026, (b) AI-tool competitive overhang (Cursor, Vercel v0, Lovable, ChatGPT canvas), and (c) sentiment whiplash on a stock that opened at peak unsustainable valuation.

Net Dollar Retention reached **136%** in Q4 2025 (highest in 10 quarters), and customers spending >$1M ARR grew **+68% YoY** to 67 — fundamentals do not match the price action ([Figma Q4 2025 transcript](https://investors.figma.com/news-releases)).

## Moat Assessment

**Narrow** — strong product-led network effects within design teams, but the surrounding category is being disrupted by AI-native tools at the same time Figma is racing to incorporate AI into its own stack.

**Sources**:
- **Network effects within design teams**: Multi-user collaboration is sticky; once a team's design files live in Figma, switching is painful

- **Lock-up overhang**: 54.1% of shares under extended lock-up expiring in 2026 tranches creates ongoing supply pressure

- **Mike Krieger (Anthropic CPO) board resignation** April 14, 2026 — modest negative signal on AI-partnership depth ([Reuters search](https://www.reuters.com/site-search/?query=Krieger+Figma+board)) [link pending]

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Dylan Field, Evan Wallace, 2012)**: Browser-native, real-time multi-user editing of vector design files was technically possible (using WebGL + operational transforms) but no incumbent (Adobe, Sketch) had the architecture to deliver it. By rebuilding the design tool around collaboration as the primary primitive — not file syncing or one-user-at-a-time editing — Figma could turn design into a team sport and capture the design-team workflow.

**Today**: The collaboration thesis won decisively for traditional design workflows. The new, harder question is whether design itself remains a discrete workflow as AI-driven code generation collapses the design-to-code pipeline.

**Pivotal question (restated)**: Is AI a tailwind for Figma (as the platform integrates Make and other AI features) or an existential threat (as engineers + PMs increasingly bypass design files entirely)?

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Design tool TAM**: The traditional collaborative-design tool market is ~$5–8B and growing low-double-digit. Figma is the dominant player. The AI-expansion (Make, code-generation surfaces) potentially expands TAM into the broader productivity/creation category — but exposes Figma to far larger competitors (OpenAI, Microsoft, Google).

**Structural dynamics**: SaaS valuation multiples have compressed broadly since 2021. Even high-quality SaaS like Figma trades far below 2021 multiples. The category is crowded with AI-native challengers, several of which are venture-funded with multi-year runway to compete on price/features.

**Lock-up dynamics (specific to FIG)**: This is dominating price action. With 54.1% of shares under extended lock-ups expiring in tranches tied to Q1 and Q3 2026 earnings dates, every print is also a supply event. Until lock-ups normalize (post-August 2026), price action is structurally pressured.
