## Business Overview

Figma (NYSE: **FIG**) is the leading collaborative design and prototyping platform for digital products, used by **≈95% of Fortune 500 design teams**. Founded in 2012 by Dylan Field and Evan Wallace, the company built a browser-native, real-time multiplayer design tool by combining WebGL with operational-transform collaboration primitives — an architecture Adobe and Sketch could not match without ground-up rewrites.

**What Figma sells (FY2025)**:
- **Figma Design** (per-editor SaaS) — the core product, used by product designers, PMs, and engineers
- **FigJam** — collaborative whiteboarding (vs. Miro / Mural)
- **Dev Mode** — developer-handoff workflow (specs, code snippets, design tokens)
- **Figma Slides, Figma Sites, Figma Buzz, Figma Draw** — newer products launched 2024–2025 rounding out a "design platform"
- **Figma Make** (launched 2025, GA July) — AI-assisted code/design generation; **>50% of $100K+ ARR customers use weekly** by Q4 2025; ≈60% of Make files created by non-designers ([Q4 2025 release](https://investor.figma.com/news-events/news/news-details/2026/Figma-Announces-Fourth-Quarter-and-Fiscal-Year-2025-Financial-Results/default.aspx))

**FY2025 scale**:
- **$1.056B revenue** (+41% YoY) — first $1B-revenue year; Q4 alone was **$303.8M (+40%)**
- **136% NDR** for $10K+ ARR cohort in Q4 (highest in 10 quarters)
- **67 customers >$1M ARR** (+68% YoY); 1,250+ customers >$100K ARR
- **88% gross margin** (FY); **14% non-GAAP operating margin** (Q4); **13% adjusted FCF margin** (Q4)
- **$1.7B cash and marketables**

**The Adobe inheritance**: A $20B Adobe acquisition agreement was abandoned 2023-12-18 under UK CMA + EU + DOJ pressure. Adobe paid Figma a **$1B termination fee** — which funded the AI build-out and the path to IPO. That outcome is now part of why Figma exists as an independent public company.

**The post-IPO drawdown**: Figma went public 2025-07-31 at $33/share, surged to a **$142.92 first-day peak** (≈$55B market cap, ≈52× P/S), and as of 2026-05-08 sits at **$20.66** — **–86% from peak, –37% from IPO price**. Three forces compounded: (1) staggered lock-up expirations with **54.1% of shares under extended lock-ups** unwinding through August 2026, (2) AI-disruption competitive overhang that escalated materially with Anthropic's Claude Design launch (2026-04-17), and (3) sentiment whiplash off an unsustainable peak. Q4 2025 fundamentals — NDR 136%, +41% revenue, 67 $1M+ customers (+68% YoY) — do not match the price action, but the competitive narrative has grown more concrete.

## 3. Competitive Moat & Landscape

**Moat Rating: Narrow but Widening.** Strong product-led moat within the traditional design-team workflow (browser-native multiplayer, dev-handoff stickiness); the *category itself* is being reshaped by AI-native code generation, and Figma's moat-extension bet is to be the **collaboration surface** connecting humans + AI agents in product creation. The April 2026 Claude Design launch is the most concrete downward pressure on moat assessment since ingest.

### Moat Sources

| Source | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Browser-native real-time multiplayer | **Strong** | Multi-year architectural lead; Adobe XD discontinued; Sketch never matched it. The original moat. |
| Enterprise penetration / network effects | **Strong** | ≈95% of Fortune 500 design teams; once team files live in Figma + Dev Mode is in the engineering workflow, switching cost is high. |
| Dev Mode workflow lock-in | **Moderate-Strong** | Developer-handoff specs, code-snippets, design tokens — extends moat from designers into engineering org. |
| Brand / "design verb" | **Moderate** | "Figma" is the default tool reference among modern product teams; harder to dislodge than feature parity suggests. |
| Multi-product platform (8 products) | **Moderate (widening)** | 70%+ customers on 3+ products; cross-sell is real, but newer products are less proven than the core. |
| AI-native repositioning (Make + UCP-like surfaces) | **Moderate (developing)** | Make adoption >50% of $100K+ customers weekly by Q4 — empirical evidence the platform is capturing AI value. |

### Moat Vulnerabilities

- **Claude Design (Anthropic, 2026-04-17)**: The most material new development since ingest. Claude Design generates designs, prototypes, and slides from prompts; prototypes hand off directly to Claude Code as structured implementation bundles. It does not replace Figma's collaborative-canvas workflow but it **eliminates the awkward step between mockup and code** — the exact workflow Dev Mode and Make occupy. Critically, Mike Krieger stepped off Figma's board three days before Claude Design launched, signaling Anthropic's competitive intent directly. This is now the highest-severity AI threat.
- **AI-native code-generation tools more broadly** (Cursor, Vercel v0, Lovable, ChatGPT Canvas, Google Stitch) — let developers + PMs skip dedicated design files. If engineering teams stop needing design files, the collaboration moat is on the wrong substrate.
- **Adobe is back**: Adobe Express + Firefly + Photoshop incorporate generative design; despite the abandoned $20B deal, Adobe remains a well-capitalized direct competitor.
- **Lock-up overhang** is mechanical, not fundamental — but creates persistent supply pressure through Aug 2026.

### Competitive Landscape

Direct competition spans three layers — traditional design tools, AI-native creation tools, and adjacent collaboration tools.

| Competitor | Type | Approx. Position | Threat Vector |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adobe** (XD discontinued; Express + Firefly + Photoshop) | Public, $250B+ mkt cap | Legacy design giant; XD failed; Firefly/Express now hosting generative design | Re-entry via AI generative tools + creative cloud bundle pricing |
| **Canva** | Private, ≈$32B last valuation (2024) | **Largest direct private competitor**; ≈190M MAU; non-designer/marketing focus; Affinity acquisition (2024) brings pro-design closer | Non-designer / SMB creation TAM; building toward enterprise design |
| **Anthropic Claude Design** | AI-native, launched 2026-04-17 | Prompt-to-prototype; direct Claude Code handoff; backed by Claude Opus 4.7 | **New highest-severity threat** — eliminates the mockup-to-code step Dev Mode and Make occupy; well-resourced ([VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-just-launched-claude-design-an-ai-tool-that-turns-prompts-into-prototypes-and-challenges-figma)) |
| **Microsoft Designer + Loop** | Bundled in M365 | Free in Microsoft 365; integrates Copilot | Enterprise bundling pressure on per-seat pricing |
| **Sketch** | Private, niche | Legacy macOS-native design tool; market share continues to erode | Niche only — not a serious threat |
| **Penpot** | Open-source, free | Open-source Figma alternative; institutional/EU adoption | Long-tail price/lock-in pressure; not a direct enterprise threat |
| **Cursor / v0 / Lovable / Stitch (Google)** | AI-native | Code-first creation; design surface is generated on demand | **Existential** — bypasses the dedicated design-file workflow entirely if the bear case plays out |
| **Miro / Mural** | Whiteboard-collab | Direct FigJam competitors | FigJam is the response; mostly contained |

**How Figma's moat differs**: Figma's defensibility is the **combined** collaboration substrate (browser-native real-time multi-user) + **the dev-handoff workflow** + **Auto Layout / Variants tooling depth** (years of accumulated design-system primitives) + **the cross-functional adoption pattern** (designers + PMs + engineers all in the same file). No single competitor combines all four. Adobe has the brand and creative-cloud bundle but not the multiplayer collaboration substrate; Canva has scale but not enterprise design-system depth or dev-handoff; Claude Design eliminates the mockup-to-code step but does not replace the collaborative canvas where large teams coordinate across dozens of live files.

**Empirical evidence the moat is holding (as of Q4 2025)**: $1M+ ARR cohort +68% YoY; international revenue +45%; 70%+ multi-product adoption; Make >50% of $100K+ weekly. The April 2026 Claude Design launch is post-Q4, so the Q1 2026 print (May 14) will be the first data point on whether enterprise NDR holds under the new competitive pressure.

**Honest tail-risk read**: Claude Design is the most precisely targeted competitive threat Figma has faced because it directly attacks the mockup-to-code bridge. Google Stitch (integrated with Workspace, free, well-resourced) is the second most concerning. If 2026 data shows enterprise design teams routing around Figma via Claude Design rather than through Make, the bear case becomes the base case.

- Lock-up pressure as a permanent overhang rather than a known schedule with a known terminal date
