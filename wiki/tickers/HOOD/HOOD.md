## Business Overview

Robinhood Markets, Inc. (NASDAQ: HOOD) is a U.S. retail brokerage and financial-services platform. Founded 2013 by Vlad Tenev and Baiju Bhatt with the insight that *commission-free* mobile-first trading would unlock the under-40 retail demographic, Robinhood IPO'd July 2021, survived a near-existential 2022–2023 crypto/retail-trading bear market, and has emerged as a structurally profitable multi-revenue platform.

**Scale (Q1 2026 / FY2025)**:
- 27.4M Funded Customers (+6% YoY)
- $307B Total Platform Assets (+39% YoY); peaked at $324B at YE2025
- 4.3M Robinhood Gold subscribers (~16% of funded base; 40% of new customers)
- $17.7B Q1 net deposits (22% annualized growth rate); $68B FY2025
- ARPU: $157 Q1 2026 (+8% YoY); $191 Q4 2025
- Cash: $5.0B; refreshed $1.5B buyback authorization

**Five-stream revenue model (Q1 2026)**:
- **Options PFOF (~24%)** — $260M, +8% YoY, the most consistent transaction line
- **Crypto (~13%)** — $134M, –47% YoY, highest cyclicality
- **Equities PFOF (~8%)** — $82M, +46% YoY
- **Event Contracts / Prediction Markets (~14%)** — $147M, +320% YoY (Rothera), the new structural growth vector
- **Net Interest Revenue (~34%)** — $359M, +24% YoY (margin lending + sweep + securities lending)
- **Gold + Other (~8%)** — $135M (Gold $50M, +32%); subscription compounds

**Defining 2025–2026 transformation**:
- **TradePMR closed (Feb 2025, ~$300M)** — RIA custody platform competing with Schwab Advisor Services and Fidelity Wealth
- **Bitstamp closed (June 2025, ~$200M)** — 50+ global crypto licenses + institutional client base + crypto-as-a-service infrastructure
- **S&P 500 inclusion (Sept 22, 2025)** — replaced Caesars; +15% on announcement; index-fund forced buyer
- **Rothera prediction-markets exchange (2026)** — vertically integrated event-contracts venue; $147M Q1 revenue at +320% growth
- **Trump Accounts (2026)** — government-program partnership; 5.5M of 60M eligible American children signed up; B2G channel
- **Robinhood Banking + private banking (2025–2026)** — direct deposit attach 40% of Gold

CEO Vlad Tenev (co-founder, ~50 sells / 0 buys over 5 years; CEO since founding); CFO Shiv Verma (succeeded Jason Warnick mid-2025).

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Can Robinhood's *non-crypto* franchise — Gold subscriptions, net interest revenue, event contracts/Rothera, retirement, RIA custody (TradePMR), and international (Bitstamp + Canada/Singapore) — durably absorb the cyclical drawdowns in crypto transaction revenue (Q1 2026: –47% YoY) so that the company compounds adj EBITDA through-cycle, rather than re-experiencing a 2022-style –$1B net loss when retail engagement next contracts?

The answer hinges on three things: **(1) revenue diversification** — has the 2021 PFOF concentration (77% of revenue) genuinely shifted to a balanced model with NII + Gold + event contracts as ballast; **(2) Gold subscription LTV** — is the 4.3M / +36% YoY growth retentive enough to be a durable annuity layer, and does the 40% direct-deposit attach create banking-stickiness; **(3) Rothera/event contracts durability** — is +320% growth a structural new asset class or a 2026-specific election/political-event surge that compresses in 2027.

## Section 3 — Competitive Moat & Landscape

### Moat Verdict: **Narrow-and-Widening**

Robinhood does not yet have SCHW's scale moat ($11.8T AUM vs. HOOD $307B; 46.5M vs. 27.4M accounts), but it has built a *cohort moat* (under-40 active trader; Gen Z 4× over-indexed) and is building durability layers on top.

**Sources of moat**:
1. **Brand + UX with the under-40 cohort** — single-tap mobile, gamified UX, fractional shares, $0 commissions. 4.3M Gold subs at +36% YoY suggests pricing-power emergence ($75/yr). Gen-Z customer acquisition cost meaningfully below incumbents [Estimate].
2. **Active-trader feature lead** — Tenev (Q1 2026): *"If you're an active trader, we want you to feel like you're at a disadvantage trading anywhere besides Robinhood."* Options + futures + 24/5 trading + Robinhood Legend desktop.
3. **Vertical integration in event contracts (Rothera)** — Robinhood owns the exchange; competitors (Kalshi, Polymarket) don't have the integrated brokerage distribution.
4. **Bitstamp infrastructure** — 50+ global crypto licenses are durable, regulatory-moated, hard to replicate.
5. **Direct-deposit / banking attach** — 40% of Gold customers direct deposit; converts brokerage into primary financial relationship.

**Vulnerabilities**:
- No scale moat vs. SCHW / Fidelity / Vanguard
- Crypto revenue cyclicality (–47% Q1 2026) reveals real exposure
- PFOF model regulatory tail
- Customer concentration in active traders — bear-market AUM/engagement contraction risk
- Tenev insider selling pattern (50 sells / 0 buys over 5 yrs) is a negative governance signal

Honest tail-risk: in a bear market, customers may consolidate to incumbents (SCHW/Fidelity) for *safety* — HOOD's brand is stronger in bull cycles than in flight-to-quality cycles.

- **Demographic tailwind**: $84T multi-decade wealth transfer from Boomers → Gen X / Millennials / Gen Z. HOOD over-indexed to inheritor cohorts.
