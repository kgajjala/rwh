## Business Overview

Intuit (NASDAQ: INTU) is a vertical SaaS platform that serves consumers, small and mid-market businesses, and accountants across four product lines: **TurboTax** (consumer and assisted tax preparation, ~70% of U.S. DIY e-filed returns), **QuickBooks** (de-facto SMB accounting standard; cloud engine is QuickBooks Online — QBO — with ~7.5M paying subscribers internationally), **Credit Karma** (free credit scores and financial-product marketplace, 100M+ U.S. members, acquired 2020 for $8.1B), and **Mailchimp** (email/marketing automation, acquired 2021 for $12B, now integrated within the Global Business Solutions Group as the marketing layer). FY2025 revenue was **$18.83B** (+15.6% YoY), with TTM revenue (as of January 2026) reaching **$20.1B**. FY2026 full-year guidance (reaffirmed after Q2) is **$20.997B–$21.186B** (+12–13% YoY). (*[Management guidance, Q2 FY26 earnings call, February 26, 2026](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/26/intuit-intu-q2-2026-earnings-call-transcript/)*)

The company has repositioned as an **"AI-driven expert platform"**: in FY2026 Intuit formalized a multiyear partnership with **Anthropic** and added all four core apps to **OpenAI's GPT directory**, while launching proprietary **Intuit Assist** agentic workflows across QBO and TurboTax. Over 3M customers used AI agents in Q2 FY26 with 85%+ repeat engagement, and accounting agents categorized 237M transactions in January 2026 alone. Management's framing — pairing AI with human intelligence (HI) for high-stakes financial decisions — is structurally different from the horizontal-LLM threat narrative that has driven the stock down 51% from its 2025 highs. (*[Q2 FY26 earnings call, February 26, 2026](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/26/intuit-intu-q2-2026-earnings-call-transcript/)*)

## Section 5 — Strategic Growth Initiatives

1. **AI agents (Intuit Assist across all four surfaces)**: 3M+ customers used AI agents in Q2 FY26; 85%+ repeat engagement; 237M QBO transactions categorized in January 2026. The AI layer reduces cost of service delivery (allowing more customers per human expert) while improving upsell conversion (AI identifies complexity early, routes to Live tier). Monetization is indirect today — the catalyst is when AI enables premium-tier pricing.

2. **Mid-market wedge (QBO Advanced + IES)**: **~40% growth** as of Q2 FY26. IES is targeting a $50B+ mid-market TAM that NetSuite dominates but with inferior accountant-channel relationships. New IES construction edition launched Q2 FY26. Accountant-sourced deals reaching one-third of new IES contracts — a structural distribution advantage. This is the growth vector most underweighted by consensus.

3. **Credit Karma re-acceleration**: +23% growth in Q2 FY26 as personal loans, credit cards, and auto insurance verticals re-open after the 2022–2023 credit tightening. 100M+ member base gives Intuit extraordinary reach for financial product marketing; the question is how far the credit cycle re-opening extends into 2026–2027.

4. **TurboTax Live (assisted tier) expansion**: Converting free/low-complexity filers to the $100–$300 ARPU assisted tier is the primary moat reinforcement against both IRS Direct File and horizontal AI. The ~600 local service centers are proving incremental demand for in-person hybrid tax help — 5.1M touchpoints in early February 2026 vs. 4.2M for all of last season.

5. **FedNow / instant payments layer**: Intuit completed FedNow Service certification in April 2026, positioning QBO payroll and payments for real-time settlement. Long-term this strengthens the payments flywheel and reduces settlement risk. Not a near-term revenue driver but a strategic position worth monitoring. (*[Intuit IR, April 9, 2026](https://investors.intuit.com)*)

6. **Mailchimp-QBO marketing flywheel**: The original thesis for the $12B acquisition was cross-sell between marketing automation and accounting; execution has lagged. Customer churn and slow SMB acquisition remain headwinds. The integration with QBO (CRM + marketing + accounting in one) remains the long-term value proposition but is a 3–5 year horizon story, not FY2026.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**U.S. Tax Preparation TAM**: The U.S. consumer and SMB tax preparation market is approximately $15B annually. Intuit captures ~25–30% by returns and a disproportionately higher share of revenue (closer to 40–50% of industry revenue) because TurboTax commands premium ARPU in the DIY segment and grows its assisted tier. Total U.S. individual tax returns: ~160M per year; approximately 80M are e-filed DIY; TurboTax serves ~55–60M of those. The structural trend is toward DIY over professional preparers, which favors Intuit's model. IRS Direct File is the counter-trend, but its scope is constitutionally limited to federal returns (state integration requires separate state systems, many of which are not free).

**SMB Software and Payroll TAM**: The U.S. SMB accounting, payroll, and payments market is a $50B+ opportunity. Intuit is the dominant player at small-business scale but sub-10% penetrated in the $10M–$500M mid-market that IES is targeting. The mid-market TAM expansion is the primary valuation re-rating catalyst.

**AI Macro**: The horizontal LLM narrative has compressed Intuit's multiple by ~50% from 35× to 17× non-GAAP P/E. The bull counter — validated by Q2 FY26 data — is that vertical software with proprietary data, workflow integration, and a human-expertise layer is more valuable in an AI world, not less. A recession would materially compress Credit Karma (estimated 30–40% revenue decline in a severe scenario) but Intuit's overall business would remain profitable given GBSG and Consumer's recession-resilience (tax filings are legally mandatory).
