## Business Overview

Intuit (NASDAQ: INTU) is a vertical SaaS platform serving consumers, small/mid-market businesses, and accountants across four product lines: **TurboTax** (consumer + assisted tax prep, dominant U.S. share), **QuickBooks** (the de-facto small-business accounting standard, with QuickBooks Online — QBO — as the cloud SaaS engine), **Credit Karma** (free credit scores + financial-product marketplace acquired 2020 for $8.1B), and **Mailchimp** (email/marketing automation acquired 2021 for $12B). FY2025 (ending July 2025) revenue was $18.8B, growing to a guided $20.997B–$21.186B for FY2026 (+12–13%). [Source: Intuit FY26 guidance, Feb 2026]

The company has repositioned around AI: in FY26 it formalized partnerships with **Anthropic** and **OpenAI** (all four core apps in OpenAI's directory), launched proprietary "Intuit Assist" agents across QBO and TurboTax, and pushed mid-market expansion via **QBO Advanced** and **Intuit Enterprise Suite (IES)** (~40% growth in Q2 FY26). Credit Karma rebounded sharply in FY26 (+23% in Q2) after multi-year stagnation as personal loans, credit cards, and auto-insurance verticals re-accelerated. [Source: INTU Q2 FY26 press release, Feb 26 2026]

## Moat Assessment

**Wide** — multi-source moat across switching costs, brand, network effects (accountant ecosystem), and data scale.

**Sources**:
- **QuickBooks switching costs**: SMB accounting data + payroll + bank feeds + tax filing tightly integrated; migration cost is high in time and audit-trail risk.
- **Accountant network effect**: ~600K accountants trained on QB; new SMBs default to what their accountant uses.
- **TurboTax brand + product moat**: ~70% of DIY e-filed returns; "Live" assisted tier converts price-sensitive into higher-ARPU users.
- **Credit Karma data flywheel**: 100M+ U.S. members → underwriter-quality data → better personalization → better conversion.
- **AI advantage from proprietary data**: Per management, customer data is contractually walled off from external models — a defensible AI position vs. horizontal LLM disruptors.

**Vulnerabilities**:
- AI horizontals (ChatGPT, Claude) competing on the consumer tax + bookkeeping use case ("just use the chatbot")
- IRS Direct File expansion threatens the free/low-end TurboTax funnel
- Credit Karma is highly cyclical to credit-card and personal-loan origination
- Mailchimp has under-delivered vs. acquisition price

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Scott Cook, 1983)**: Tax and small-business accounting were paper-based, painful, and error-prone. A consumer-friendly software product (Quicken → TurboTax → QuickBooks) could automate the workflow and capture a recurring annuity from every U.S. household and SMB. The insight was distribution: get into the workflow at the start of the tax year (or business formation) and you own the customer for life.

**Today**: Intuit is the dominant U.S. vertical SaaS for consumer tax (TurboTax), small-business accounting (QuickBooks Online), free credit/financial marketplace (Credit Karma), and email marketing (Mailchimp). The AI shift has reframed each business: in management's framing, AI is a tailwind (Intuit Assist embedded across all surfaces, agentic workflows in mid-market); the bear framing is that horizontal LLMs commoditize the workflow and compress terminal pricing.

**Pivotal question**: At ~17× FY26E EPS (down from 35×+ at the highs), does INTU offer a wide-moat compounder at a deep value multiple — or is the multiple compression *correctly* repricing the AI threat to terminal economics?

### B — Behavioral: **Strong**

The –51% drawdown from $813 to $396 is one of the deepest peak-to-trough moves in INTU's public history. The narrative driver is clean: AI horizontals will commoditize tax + bookkeeping. Empirically, FY26 results contradict the narrative — Q2 FY26 grew 17% with QBO Advanced + IES +40% and Credit Karma +23%, *while* the AI partnerships were being announced. The market is treating AI partnerships as bear evidence (model risk) rather than as competitive moat-building. This is a textbook behavioral mispricing — narrative-led, not data-led.

### A — Analytical: **Strong**

- **Earnings power**: $23 non-GAAP EPS in FY26 at 17× → INTU has compressed to a value multiple while still growing 12–13% top-line and 14–15% EPS
- **Mid-market underweighted**: IES + QBO Advanced at +40% growth is a $1B+ run-rate business that the consensus largely models as a side-business
- **Credit Karma re-acceleration**: +23% recovery is a $400M+ swing factor not yet in long-term models
- **AI as moat amplifier**: Walled customer data + workflow integration is *more* valuable in an AI world; consensus models treat it as risk
