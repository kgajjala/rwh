## Business Description

Eli Lilly and Company is a global pharmaceutical company founded in 1876, headquartered
in Indianapolis, Indiana. It discovers, develops, manufactures, and markets human
pharmaceutical products across multiple therapeutic categories.

**Core franchises (FY2025)**:
- **Incretin / GLP-1 franchise** (dominant): Mounjaro (tirzepatide, T2D, ~$23.5B in 2025)
  + Zepbound (tirzepatide, obesity, Q4 2025 run rate ~$17B annualized). Combined
  tirzepatide revenue was the fastest-scaling pharmaceutical franchise in history.
- **Oncology**: Verzenio (abemaciclib, breast cancer, ~$6.2B), Jaypirca, Retevmo, Cyramza
- **Immunology**: Taltz (ixekizumab, ~$2.5B), Ebglyss (lebrikizumab, atopic dermatitis)
- **Neuroscience**: Kisunla (donanemab, Alzheimer's — approved 2024), Jardiance (heart
  failure), Emgality
- **Legacy/Other**: Humalog, Basaglar, Trulicity (declining)

Lilly's transformation from a ~$28B revenue company in 2021-2022 to $65B in 2025 — and
targeting $80–83B in 2026 — is one of the most dramatic reinventions in large-cap pharma.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **Can Lilly sustain a premium multiple after the tirzepatide ramp, or will
> manufacturing constraints, oral GLP-1 competition (orforglipron + Novo/Roche/Viking),
> and eventual patent cliffs compress the multiple to peer-group levels?**

Bull: tirzepatide is a decade-long blockbuster in obesity + T2D + OSA + further
indications; orforglipron (oral GLP-1) extends the franchise; pipeline is deep; $50B+
manufacturing moat is being built. Bear: GLP-1 oral competition creates pricing pressure;
capital-intensive manufacturing absorbs FCF; tirzepatide LOE ~2036 is the wall.

## Moat Rating

- **Rating**: Wide
- **Sources**:
  - Intangible assets: ~20 years of tirzepatide patent protection; incretin expertise moat
  - Scale: $50B+ committed U.S. manufacturing investment creates physical capacity moat
  - Brand/physician relationships: 150-year diabetes franchise with endocrinologists
  - Pipeline depth: Retatrutide (triple agonist, Phase 3), orforglipron (oral GLP-1, filed),
    broad oncology and neuro pipeline
  - First-mover: Zepbound has fastest formulary adoption in obesity drug history
