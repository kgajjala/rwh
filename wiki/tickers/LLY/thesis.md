## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Question

**Founding Insight**: Disease causes suffering. Eli Lilly founded the company in 1876 to
produce standardized pharmaceutical products at a time when medicines were inconsistent
and unreliable. 150 years later, Lilly has become the most valuable pharmaceutical company
in history at its peak (>$800B) on the back of a single insight: GLP-1 receptor agonism
(incretins) could treat not just type 2 diabetes, but the global obesity epidemic.

**The Tirzepatide Revolution**: Lilly's tirzepatide (Mounjaro for T2D; Zepbound for
obesity) is the first dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. It outperforms all prior GLP-1
drugs in weight loss efficacy (up to 22.5% body weight loss vs. 15% for semaglutide). The
obesity market is estimated at 500-750M addressable patients globally, with <5% currently
treated. This is the largest pharmaceutical market opportunity in history.

**Current Strategic Vision**: Expand tirzepatide into every obesity-adjacent indication
(OSA, NASH, heart failure, osteoarthritis); develop the next generation (retatrutide,
oral GLP-1 orforglipron); build manufacturing capacity to serve global demand; reaffirm
leadership in oncology (Verzenio), immunology (Taltz, Ebglyss), and neuroscience (Kisunla).

**The Pivotal Investment Question**:
> Can Lilly sustain a premium multiple (27x FY2026E EPS at $936) after the tirzepatide
> ramp — when oral GLP-1 competition from Novo, Roche, Viking, and others could create
> pricing pressure, and eventual patent cliffs compress the multiple to pharma-peer levels?

The bull: tirzepatide has a decade+ patent life; oral GLP-1 (orforglipron) extends the
franchise; manufacturing moat ($50B committed) creates durable capacity advantage. The
bear: competition in oral GLP-1 is intense; pricing pressure from CMS negotiation under
IRA is rising; manufacturing buildout absorbs FCF; stock at $936 prices in flawless execution.

## Section 5 — Competitive Moat

**Rating: Wide**

**Genuine Moat Sources**:
- **Intangible assets**: Tirzepatide patent portfolio (~20 years protection); decade+ of
  GLP-1 and GIP receptor clinical expertise that cannot be easily replicated
- **Manufacturing scale**: $50B+ committed to U.S. manufacturing since 2020; $6.5B Texas
  API plant for orforglipron. Capacity IS the moat in a market with 500M+ addressable
  patients and global supply constraints
- **Physician relationships**: 150-year diabetes franchise with endocrinologists and PCPs;
  Humalog brand loyalty translates to Mounjaro/Zepbound prescribing habits
- **Pipeline depth and diversification**: Retatrutide (triple agonist, Phase 3), orforglipron
  (oral GLP-1 filed), Kisunla (Alzheimer's ramp), strong oncology pipeline — multiple shots
  on goal beyond tirzepatide
- **First-mover in obesity**: Zepbound fastest formulary adoption in obesity drug history;
  payer coverage expanded dramatically in 2024-2025

**Competitive Threats**:
- **Novo Nordisk**: semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) — established brand, but inferior weight
  loss vs. tirzepatide. Novo's oral semaglutide is already on market.
- **Oral GLP-1 competition**: Roche (CT-388 dual agonist, Phase 2), Viking Therapeutics
  (VK2735, Phase 3 oral results positive), Structure Therapeutics — multiple oral GLP-1s
  in development. Orforglipron's first-mover advantage in oral is real but not permanent.
- **IRA drug pricing**: Medicare drug price negotiation could compress Mounjaro pricing
  post-exclusivity period (2028 earliest for small molecule negotiation trigger)
- **Biosimilar competition on legacy drugs**: Humalog, Basaglar facing biosimilar pressure

**Moat Verdict**: Wide, but concentrated. The manufacturing investment is the sustainable
differentiator — no competitor can build a $50B manufacturing network in 2-3 years.

## Section 8 — Strategic Growth Initiatives

1. **Orforglipron (oral GLP-1)**: FDA/EMA/PMDA submissions received; priority review
   voucher accelerates U.S. timeline. A once-daily pill (vs. weekly injection) dramatically
   expands the addressable market — needle-averse patients are a massive unserved segment.

2. **Retatrutide (triple agonist)**: GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon triple agonist. Phase 3 showing
   26.6% weight loss — potentially superior to tirzepatide. The pipeline behind the pipeline.

3. **Tirzepatide Indication Expansion**: OSA (sleep apnea — FDA approved 2024), OA
   (osteoarthritis, positive data), potential cardiovascular indication, NASH/MASH. Each
   new indication extends the patent value and expands the addressable market.

4. **U.S. Manufacturing Build-out**: $50B+ committed. Creates capacity moat vs. oral
   GLP-1 competitors who will face the same supply scaling challenge Lilly solved first.

5. **Kisunla (donanemab) Ramp**: Alzheimer's disease early treatment. FDA approved 2024.
   $30B+ annual market potential if broadly adopted; current ramp is early stage.

6. **Global Access**: U.S.-government partnership to expand access to GLP-1 medicines
   to millions; pricing/access agreements with payers in Europe, Japan, emerging markets.

7. **Oncology Pipeline**: CDK4/6 franchise (Verzenio) growing to $6.2B+ globally;
   Jaypirca (CLL), Retevmo (RET) continue to scale.

8. **AI in Drug Discovery**: Lilly has partnerships and internal programs to use AI/ML
   to accelerate the drug discovery pipeline — long-term pipeline optionality.

## Section 10 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Primary macro drivers for LLY**:

1. **GLP-1/Obesity Drug Market Penetration** (dominant):
   - Currently <5% of 500-750M obese/overweight adults treated with GLP-1s globally
   - U.S. employer insurance coverage for obesity drugs expanding (~40% of large employers
     offering in 2025, up from 15% in 2022)
   - CMS/Medicare coverage expansion could add 5-10M patients if legislated
   - The penetration curve from 5% → 20% over 10 years is the fundamental thesis

2. **U.S. Drug Pricing (IRA)**:
   - Inflation Reduction Act allows CMS to negotiate small-molecule drugs 7 years after
     launch (biologics = 13 years). Tirzepatide (small molecule) could face negotiation
     ~2028-2030.
   - Pricing headwind is real but manageable if volume growth exceeds price compression
   - Lilly leadership has been vocal in opposing IRA drug pricing as a policy matter

3. **Manufacturing Capacity**:
   - Supply constraints were the limiting factor for Zepbound in 2023-2024; management says
     resolved in 2025. If orforglipron launches, all manufacturing capacity questions restart.

4. **Global Reimbursement**:
   - Europe, Japan, emerging markets are 2-5 years behind U.S. in obesity drug coverage
   - This is a decade-long secular tailwind for Lilly's international revenue growth
