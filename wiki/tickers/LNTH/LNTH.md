## Business Overview

Lantheus Holdings (NASDAQ: LNTH) is the U.S. market leader in PSMA-targeted PET imaging for prostate cancer, with a growing multi-product PET radiodiagnostic platform spanning neuroendocrine tumors and Alzheimer's disease. Its flagship product, **PYLARIFY** (piflufolastat F 18), is the #1-ordered PSMA PET imaging agent in the U.S., with Q1 2026 revenue of $240.9M (–6.5% YoY on net price; doses +5.8% YoY). The company completed a strategic transformation in 2025–2026: divesting its legacy SPECT business (to SHINE Technologies, effective January 1, 2026), acquiring Life Molecular Imaging (Neuraceq amyloid PET) and Evergreen Theragnostics (OCTEVY NET diagnostic + RLT manufacturing/CDMO + LNTH-2503), and most recently signaling a strategic narrowing toward pure radiodiagnostics — exploring "value-maximizing alternatives" for radiotherapeutic pipeline assets including LNTH-2503 and the Evergreen CDMO, while retaining near-commercial PNT2003 (Lu-177 LUTATHERA radioequivalent, tentatively FDA approved March 2026).

FY2025 total revenue was $1,541.6M. FY2026 guidance of $1.4–1.45B (reaffirmed after Q1 2026 beat) reflects an expected ongoing PYLARIFY net price decline driven by competitive pricing from Posluma (Blue Earth/Bracco) and Illuccix/Gozellix (Telix), partially offset by volume growth and the Neuraceq/DEFINITY diversification. Three FDA milestones remain in 2026: **LNTH-2501** (PDUFA June 29, 2026 — NET PET diagnostic), **MK-6240** (PDUFA August 13, 2026 — tau PET for AD), and **PNT2003 full approval** (≈June 2026 pending 30-month Hatch-Waxman stay expiration — Lu-177 dotatate RLT for GEP-NETs). Leadership is in flux: interim CEO Mary Anne Heino has narrowed the CEO search to a "small number of highly qualified candidates" as of May 2026; no appointment announced.

## Section 3 — Competitive Moat & Landscape

**Moat Rating: Narrow**

LNTH built a genuine first-mover advantage in PSMA PET: the largest cyclotron + radiopharmacy network in the U.S. (≈22 PMF sites), established workflows in >3,000 academic and community oncology centers, and brand recognition among urologists and radiation oncologists. However, the PSMA PET category has attracted well-capitalized competitors with FDA-approved PSMA agents, and the core moat source — PYLARIFY's pricing premium — is structurally under pressure.

| Moat Source | Strength | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| PSMA PET first-mover scale | Strong but eroding | #1-ordered PSMA agent; established in >3,000 U.S. sites; doses +5.8% YoY Q1 2026 |
| Radiopharmaceutical distribution infrastructure | Strong | ≈22 PMF sites with high-energy cyclotrons; F-18 110-min half-life requires local manufacturing — natural moat vs. distant competitors |
| Physician workflow stickiness | Moderate | Established ordering patterns; switching friction real but not prohibitive |
| Multi-product PET platform breadth | Moderate | PSMA + NET (LNTH-2501 pending) + amyloid (Neuraceq) + tau (MK-6240 pending) + RLT (PNT2003) |
| DEFINITY cardiac ultrasound franchise | Moderate-strong | Near-monopoly in cardiac contrast; stable cash generator |
| Neuraceq / amyloid PET (AD) | Growing | Second most utilized; fastest-growing amyloid PET agent; benefits from anti-amyloid therapy rollout |

**Vulnerabilities**:
- PYLARIFY pricing not contractually protected; Posluma and Illuccix/Gozellix active on dose pricing.
- Manufacturing moat replicable over time — Telix and Curium have built U.S. distribution.
- Radiotherapeutic review signals LNTH is retreating from the full theranostics vision — reduces long-term differentiation vs. Novartis (Locametz + Pluvicto pair) and narrows the TAM addressable by LNTH directly.
- CEO vacancy is an execution risk at the critical multi-product commercial launch window.

The most dangerous competitor near-term remains Posluma because its TPT reimbursement pass-through status (to Sept 30, 2026) creates a structural pricing advantage. When Posluma's TPT expires, competitive dynamics should partially normalize — a discrete date-anchored catalyst that the market is underappreciating.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Nuclear medicine / radiopharmaceuticals TAM**: The global radiopharmaceutical market was ≈$6.5–7B in 2024, growing at ≈9–12% CAGR through 2030, driven by theranostics and PET diagnostic expansion. U.S. PSMA PET ≈$1.5B (mature); SSTR+ NET PET ≈$300M; tau PET for AD ≈$300–500M (emerging); amyloid PET ≈$500M+ (growing with anti-amyloid therapy). Total U.S. PET radiodiagnostic market is $3–5B and expanding. *(Size estimates: [Estimate] based on product market disclosures in filings and company presentations)*

**PSMA PET market growth**: Management cited "low teens" growth for the full PSMA PET category in 2026, down from "high teens to low 20s" in 2025 — market growth is decelerating as early-adoption phase matures. PYLARIFY is growing doses in a decelerating market while giving up net price.

**CMS reimbursement dynamics**: The most company-specific macro risk. CMS transitioned PYLARIFY from ASP-based to mean unit cost (MUC)-based reimbursement for ≈20% of patients in CY2025. Posluma's TPT status (extended to Sept 30, 2026) creates a temporary structural pricing advantage. When Posluma's TPT expires, pricing dynamics should partially normalize — a discrete September 2026 inflection.

**FDA regulatory environment**: Broadly supportive of radiopharmaceutical innovation — PYLARIFY, Pluvicto, Lutathera, Posluma, Locametz, PYLARIFY TruVu, PNT2003 (tentative) all approved in recent years. 505(b)(2) pathway (used by LNTH-2501) historically high approval rate. Fast Track designation for MK-6240 signals FDA engagement. No systemic regulatory headwinds.

**Isotope supply chain**: PYLARIFY uses F-18 (cyclotron-produced; 110-min half-life → local production); LNTH-2501 uses Ga-68 (generator or cyclotron); PNT2003 uses Lu-177 (reactor-produced; Evergreen CDMO provides supply depth). TruVu's larger batch sizes address F-18 efficiency. No acute supply disruption currently.

**Theranostics secular trend**: Every approved RLT (Pluvicto, Lutathera) creates companion diagnostic demand pull — patients must be PET-imaged before and during therapy. Lantheus captures diagnostic volume but, if radiotherapeutic review results in externalizing LNTH-2503, will not capture the higher-margin therapeutic economics directly (PNT2003 is the exception as a near-commercial asset).
