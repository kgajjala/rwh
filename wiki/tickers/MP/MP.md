## Business Overview

MP Materials (NYSE: MP) owns and operates **Mountain Pass** in San Bernardino County, CA — the only fully-integrated rare-earth mining and processing site in North America and one of the world's richest NdPr orebodies. Founded via SPAC in 2020 when CEO James Litinsky acquired Mountain Pass out of bankruptcy (original Molycorp had failed in the 2010s price crash), MP's founding insight was that the mine's world-class resource + China's structural rare-earth monopoly created an asymmetric rebuild opportunity. The company produces **NdPr (neodymium-praseodymium) oxide** — the critical input for permanent magnets used in EV motors, wind turbines, defense systems (F-35, missiles, drones), and consumer electronics.

**FY2025 scale**: $224.4M revenue (+10% YoY); record 2,599 MT NdPr oxide production (+101% YoY); Q4 2025 marked the inflection to positive Adjusted EBITDA ($39.2M) driven by $51M PPA income from the DoD price floor. The Magnetics segment generated $21.9M Q4 revenue and $8.4M Q4 EBITDA — first commercial sintered NdFeB magnet shipments in December 2025. In July 2025 the transformational **DoD partnership** closed: $400M convertible preferred equity + warrants + $150M loan + **10-year NdPr offtake at $110/kg floor** — making DoD MP's largest shareholder on an as-converted basis (~15%). Commercial anchor customers include **Apple** and **General Motors** (GM Ultium platform magnets). In February 2026 MP announced the **"10X" campus** — a $1.25B, 120-acre magnet manufacturing facility in Northlake, TX targeting 2028 commissioning — completing the mine-to-magnet vertical integration thesis at scale.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Rare-earth supply structure**: China controls ~85–90% of global NdPr mining and ~95% of refining and magnet manufacturing. Bloomberg Intelligence projects China's share declining from 90% (2024) to 69% by 2030 as Western supply chains develop — but this is still a decade-long reshoring, not a near-term event. MP benefits from this transition asymmetrically. *[Source: [Bloomberg Intelligence press release](https://www.bloomberg.com/company/press/world-scrambles-for-rare-earths-to-erode-chinas-dominance-from-90-to-69-market-share-and-gain-pricing-power-according-to-bloomberg-intelligence/)]*

**TAM and demand drivers**:
- Permanent magnet market: ~$20B+ globally; growing with EV penetration (each EV uses ~1–3 kg of NdPr magnets), wind turbines, defense electronics
- NdPr market: ~$10B projected annual value in 2026 under current trade tensions; small deficit 2025–2027 (2,000–6,000 MT); tight balance 2028–2030 per analyst projections *[Source: [Critical Minerals News — NdPr price forecast](https://critical-minerals-news.com/rare-earths-price/)]*
- "Physical AI economy" framing (Litinsky Q4 2025): rare-earth magnets as essential infrastructure for robotics, drones, EVs, and AI-compute cooling systems

**NdPr pricing**: Spot price ranged $40–250/kg over 8 years; China-driven. Current spot is below $110/kg (triggering PPA mechanism). MP's DoD floor re-anchors U.S. economics; the longer-term question is whether non-Chinese NdPr will trade at a premium to Chinese spot (which the trade decoupling thesis supports) or remain linked to Chinese pricing.

**Policy tailwinds**:
- **IRA domestic-content EV credits**: Magnets in U.S.-built EVs with U.S.-sourced rare earths qualify — creates a pull-through demand advantage for MP magnets in GM/Ford/Tesla vehicles
- **Defense Production Act Title III**: Direct DoD procurement authority for strategic materials
- **One Big Beautiful Bill Act** (signed July 4, 2025): $150M loan to MP; broader strategic-minerals funding
- **China export controls on rare earths** (ongoing escalation): Each PRC restriction announcement drives incremental re-rating of non-Chinese supply; MP is the primary U.S. beneficiary
