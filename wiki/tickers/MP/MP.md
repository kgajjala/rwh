## Business Overview

MP Materials (NYSE: MP) owns and operates **Mountain Pass** in San Bernardino County, CA — the only fully-integrated rare-earth mining and processing site in North America and one of the world's richest NdPr orebodies. Founded via SPAC in 2020 when CEO James Litinsky acquired Mountain Pass out of bankruptcy (original Molycorp had failed in the 2010s price crash), MP's founding insight was that the mine's world-class resource + China's structural rare-earth monopoly created an asymmetric rebuild opportunity. The company produces **NdPr (neodymium-praseodymium) oxide** — the critical input for permanent magnets used in EV motors, wind turbines, defense systems (F-35, missiles, drones), and consumer electronics.

**Q1 2026 scale**: $90.6M revenue (+49% YoY); 917 MT NdPr oxide production (Q1 record, +63% YoY); 1,006 MT NdPr sales (+117% YoY); Adj. EBITDA $36.6M. The Magnetics segment delivered $21.1M Q1 revenue (+306% YoY) and $9.6M Adj. EBITDA. PPA income from the DoD $110/kg floor was $42.3M in Q1 — total revenue + PPA of $132.9M. In Q1 2026 the company **broke ground on the 10X campus** (Northlake, TX); Apple prepayments reached **$72M on-balance-sheet** ($32M received February 2026); and heavy rare earth separation commissioning at Mountain Pass is set to begin in Q2 2026. In July 2025 the transformational **DoD partnership** closed: $400M convertible preferred equity + warrants + $150M loan + **10-year NdPr offtake at $110/kg floor** — making DoD MP's largest shareholder on an as-converted basis (≈15%). Next earnings: **August 6, 2026**.

**Revenue model**: MP earns on (1) NdPr oxide spot above $110/kg; (2) PPA income when spot < $110 (DoD pays difference — Q1 guided realized pricing "low-to-mid $90s/kg" meaning PPA is active); (3) Magnetics margin on value-added sintered magnets. Every $10/kg NdPr above the floor flows through at high incremental margin on ≈900+ MT/quarter production = ≈$9M/quarter EBITDA sensitivity.

**Forward-looking shifts**: The 10X campus ($1.25B, Northlake TX, 2028 commissioning) will dramatically shift the mix. $500–600M capex in FY2026 alone. At full ramp, Magnetics is expected to contribute multi-hundred-million-dollar annual revenue — transforming MP from an oxide producer to a magnet manufacturer. Additionally, the heavy rare earth (HREE) separation circuit at Mountain Pass, set for Q2 2026 commissioning, adds a new revenue stream (terbium, dysprosium, and intermediates) not yet in consensus models.

**Competitive tail risk**: China retaliating via NdPr spot flooding below $110 is the most asymmetric risk — but MP's DoD floor effectively protects against this at the oxide level. The floor doesn't fully protect Magnetics margins (magnet pricing is commercial). China's October 2025 export controls (suspended until November 2026) create near-term uncertainty about whether NdPr and magnet prices will spike or normalize — MP benefits from either direction (spike = above-floor revenue; control = premium for non-Chinese supply). Lynas building U.S. separation capacity could compress the U.S.-domicile premium over a 5-year horizon — the race is whether MP has secured enough customer lock-in first.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Rare-earth supply structure**: China controls ≈85–90% of global NdPr mining and ≈95% of refining and magnet manufacturing. Bloomberg Intelligence projects China's share declining from 90% (2024) to 69% by 2030 as Western supply chains develop — but this is still a decade-long reshoring. *[Source: [Bloomberg Intelligence press release](https://www.bloomberg.com/company/press/world-scrambles-for-rare-earths-to-erode-chinas-dominance-from-90-to-69-market-share-and-gain-pricing-power-according-to-bloomberg-intelligence/)]*

**China export control status (critical update)**: China's October 2025 extraterritorial rare-earth export controls — which would require licenses even for non-Chinese-made magnets containing any Chinese-origin REE inputs — have been **suspended until November 2026**. However, export license processing remains slow and unpredictable; NdPr prices reached near 1 million CNY/ton in early 2026 (≈80–90% YoY increase) under tight supply cycles before partially normalizing. The November 2026 reinstatement deadline is a material tail risk / positive catalyst for MP depending on policy direction. *[Sources: [CSIS China export controls analysis](https://www.csis.org/analysis/chinas-new-rare-earth-and-magnet-restrictions-threaten-us-defense-supply-chains); [China Briefing 2026](https://www.china-briefing.com/news/chinas-rare-earth-export-controls-impacts-on-businesses/); [Clark Hill — China "pause" analysis](https://www.clarkhill.com/news-events/news/china-hits-pause-on-rare-earth-export-controls-and-what-it-means-for-supply-chains/)]*

**TAM and demand drivers**:
- Permanent magnet market: ≈$20B+ globally; growing with EV penetration (each EV uses ≈1–3 kg of NdPr magnets), wind turbines, defense electronics
- NdPr market: tight supply 2025–2027 (2,000–6,000 MT annual deficit); balance point 2028–2030 per analyst projections *[Source: [Critical Minerals News — NdPr price forecast](https://critical-minerals-news.com/rare-earths/)]* 
- "Physical AI economy" framing (Litinsky Q4 2025 + Q1 2026): rare-earth magnets as essential infrastructure for robotics, drones, EVs, and AI-compute systems
- CEO comment on Q1 2026 call: access to NdPr oxide will remain "the binding constraint" for Western magnet production "for at least the next 5 years" — structural supply tightness thesis

**NdPr pricing**: Spot remains below $110/kg (DoD PPA active); Q2 2026 guided "low-to-mid $90s/kg." China-driven spot volatility is intrinsic; the DoD floor re-anchors U.S. economics. The November 2026 export control reinstatement could spike Western-market premiums for non-Chinese NdPr — MP would benefit.

**Policy tailwinds**:
- **IRA domestic-content EV credits**: U.S.-built EVs with U.S.-sourced rare earths qualify — pull-through demand advantage for MP magnets in GM/Ford/Tesla vehicles
- **Defense Production Act Title III**: Direct DoD procurement authority for strategic materials
- **One Big Beautiful Bill Act** (signed July 4, 2025): $150M loan to MP; broader strategic-minerals funding
- **China export controls (suspended Nov 2026)**: Each PRC restriction announcement drives incremental re-rating of non-Chinese supply; MP is the primary U.S. beneficiary
