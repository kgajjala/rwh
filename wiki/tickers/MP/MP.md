## Business Overview

MP Materials (NYSE: MP) owns and operates **Mountain Pass** in San Bernardino County, CA — the only fully-integrated rare-earth mining and processing site in North America and one of the world's richest rare-earth orebodies. The company produces **NdPr (neodymium-praseodymium) oxide** — the critical input for permanent magnets used in EV motors, wind turbines, defense systems (F-35, missiles, drones), and consumer electronics. FY2025 revenue was **$224.4M (+10% YoY)** with record NdPr oxide production of **2,599 metric tons** (more than doubled YoY). Q4 2025 marked an inflection to **positive adjusted EBITDA**. ([MP Materials Q4 2025 press release](https://investors.mpmaterials.com/news-releases); [SEC EDGAR — MP filings](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0001801368&type=10-K))

In July 2025, MP announced a **transformational public-private partnership with the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD)**: a $400M convertible preferred equity investment, warrants, $150M loan, **10-year offtake** with a **$110/kg NdPr price floor**, and pathway for DoD to become MP's largest shareholder (15% on as-converted basis). Anchor commercial customers include **Apple** and **General Motors**, with magnet production for GM ramping through 2026 from MP's new Independence facility (Texas). The Magnetics segment — sintered NdFeB magnet manufacturing — launched commercially in 2025, completing the mine-to-magnet vertical integration thesis.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (James Litinsky, 2017 acquisition out of bankruptcy)**: Mountain Pass had been mothballed during the 2010s rare-earth-price crash that bankrupted prior owner Molycorp. Litinsky's thesis was that (1) the mine's resource was world-class and irreplaceable, (2) Chinese supply concentration in rare earths was a structural national-security vulnerability the U.S. would eventually have to solve, and (3) re-starting the orebody at the bottom of the cycle could be massively asymmetric. The company went public via SPAC in 2020.

**Today**: MP is the only fully-integrated U.S. rare-earth platform — Stage 1 (mining + concentrate, operational), Stage 2 (oxide separation, ramping), Stage 3 (magnet manufacturing at Independence, TX, launched 2025). The DoD partnership in July 2025 transformed the equity story from "commodity producer" to "national-security strategic asset." Apple and GM are anchor commercial customers.

**Pivotal question**: Is the appropriate framework "EV/EBITDA on 2027 numbers" (which would suggest the stock is fully priced) or "strategic-asset optionality through 2035 with multiple ratchets — Magnetics scale, NdPr price normalization above $110, Saudi joint venture, additional DoD contracts" (which would suggest material upside)?

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Rare-earth global supply**: ~85% of NdPr mining + ~95% of refining is Chinese-controlled. The structural undersupply of non-Chinese rare-earth supply is the macro thesis driver.

**TAM**: Permanent-magnet market is ~$20B+ globally and growing with EV penetration + wind turbines + defense systems. NdPr oxide + metal + magnet vertical is the highest-value supply-chain segment.

**Pricing**: NdPr spot has ranged $40–250/kg over the past 8 years. China-driven swings dominate. The $110/kg DoD floor represents a strategic re-anchoring of U.S. economics.

**Policy**: One Big Beautiful Bill Act (signed July 4, 2025) provides $150M MP loan; DPA Title III; IRA EV credits with domestic-content rules favor MP magnets in U.S.-built EVs.

**EV cycle**: GM's NdPr magnet ramp through 2026 ties MP economics to the broader EV adoption curve.
