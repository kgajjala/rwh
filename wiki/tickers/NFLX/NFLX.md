## Business Overview

Netflix (NASDAQ: NFLX) is the world's largest subscription streaming video service with **325M+ paid memberships** as of Q1 2026. Revenue mix: subscription fees (paid plans across Standard, Standard with Ads, Premium tiers) plus a fast-growing advertising layer (Standard with Ads). Content slate spans original films, original series, licensed series, sports/live (NFL Christmas, WWE Raw, FIFA Women's World Cup, World Baseball Classic, boxing), unscripted, anime, regional language. The company has shifted from pure paid-subscriber growth to a **revenue-engagement-margin** framework where ARM (average revenue per member), engagement hours, and ad revenue substitute for the subscriber count headline.

In November 2025, Netflix executed a **10-for-1 stock split** (record date Nov 10, 2025; ex-date Nov 17, 2025) — the company's first split since 2015. Pre-split highs ~$1,341 = post-split ~$134.10.

Q1 2026: Revenue $12.25B (+16% YoY), beat $12.17B consensus; on track for **$3B advertising revenue in 2026** (doubling YoY); ad-tier accounted for **>60% of sign-ups in ads markets**; 325M+ paid memberships [Source: Netflix Q1 2026 earnings, April 16, 2026]. Q2 guide of $12.5B (below $12.6B consensus) and EPS guide of $0.78 (below $0.84 consensus) caused a **9.7% selloff post-print**.

## Moat Assessment

**Wide** — global content engine + scale + technology stack + recommendation system + brand.

**Sources**:
- **Scale**: 325M+ paid memberships; ~$45B annual revenue base = funds $18–20B annual content budget (~3× any single competitor's pure-streaming spend)
- **Global content engine**: Production hubs in 50+ countries; non-English originals (Squid Game, Money Heist, Lupin) drive disproportionate engagement
- **Recommendation algorithm**: Hours engaged per member is industry-best; reduces churn and content cost per engaged hour
- **Tech infrastructure**: Open Connect CDN, internal tooling = lowest cost-to-stream globally
- **Advertising platform**: Just past inflection — $3B run-rate 2026, projected $7B+ by 2028; high incremental margin
- **Brand**: "Netflix and chill" — category-defining; primary streaming destination for global majority

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Penetration saturation in mature markets (US, UK, NL); growth must come from APAC + LatAm + ad-tier monetization
- Disney (Disney+/Hulu/ESPN), Amazon Prime Video, YouTube (free), and various national champions (Tencent, NBCU Peacock, Paramount+) all compete for engagement hours
- Content cost inflation (sports rights especially — NFL, NBA, F1)
- FX exposure on ~50% international revenue
- Password-sharing crackdown gains have largely been harvested
- Reed Hastings stepped down from board in 2024 (founder departure event)

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Does Netflix's advertising business scale to $7–10B by 2028 (at near-software-margin economics) — combined with low-double-digit ARM growth and modest subscriber growth — to compound revenue at 12–14% with operating leverage to ~30% op margin, justifying a sustained 30–35× P/E?

The bull case requires (a) ad revenue tracking $3B 2026 → $5B 2027 → $7B+ 2028, (b) sports/live continuing to lift engagement without breaking the cost model, (c) margin expansion from current ~28% to 32%+ by 2028. The bear case is that ad revenue plateaus around $5B, content cost inflation outpaces subscription pricing power, and engagement growth stalls — re-rating the multiple to 22–25× and the stock to ~$80.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Reed Hastings + Marc Randolph, 1997)**: DVD-by-mail subscription would beat Blockbuster's late-fee model on convenience and economics. The *real* genius was the bet (~2007) that streaming would replace physical distribution. The 2013 pivot to original content (House of Cards) created the modern Netflix.

**Today**: Subscription-funded global content engine + emerging ad business + sports/live experiments. The company has decisively won the streaming wars on engagement-per-member basis.

**Pivotal question**: Can advertising + ARM growth + modest sub growth compound revenue 12–14% for 5 more years at expanding margins, or is the business approaching maturity at ~$50B revenue with mid-single-digit growth?

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Global SVOD TAM**: ~$120B 2025 → ~$180B 2028 (Wall Street consensus). Netflix at ~$45B is ~38% share — saturating in mature markets, growing share in APAC/LATAM.

**Engagement competition**: YouTube has ~10× the daily watch hours of all SVOD combined among teens. The competition is not for $/sub; it is for time-spent share. Live sports + short-form / podcasts are Netflix's responses.

**Advertising**: Connected TV (CTV) ad market ~$30B in 2025 → ~$45B by 2028. Netflix's $3B 2026 target is roughly 7% share entering the CTV market — meaningful runway.

**Macro**: Subscription business is moderately recession-resilient; advertising layer is more cyclical. Mix shift toward ad-tier increases cyclicality slightly.
