## Business Overview

On Holding AG (NYSE: ONON) is a Swiss premium athletic and lifestyle brand founded in Zurich in 2010 by Caspar Coppetti, David Allemann, and former Ironman triathlete Olivier Bernhard. The founding insight — that a garden-hose-prototype cushioning system could combine performance with Swiss design aesthetics — evolved into the proprietary CloudTec sole architecture that defines On's visual identity. The brand competes in the $140B global athletic footwear market anchored on premium positioning: no permanent discounting, controlled retail distribution, and an elite athlete ecosystem headlined by co-investor Roger Federer, Grand Slam champion Iga Świątek, and Tour players Ben Shelton and Jack Draper.

**FY2025 scale**: CHF 3.0B in net sales (+30% reported, **+35.6% constant currency**), record full-year gross margin of **62.8%**, adj. EBITDA margin of ~18.8%, and year-end cash of CHF 1.02B (highest in company history). The DTC channel — on-running.com plus 67 owned stores — contributed **41.8%** of net sales (up 110bps YoY), with over 60% of apparel and accessories revenue flowing through DTC. Revenue is reported in three geographies: Americas (U.S.-led, largest), EMEA (brand origin market), and APAC (fastest growth — surpassed CHF 1.0B in FY2025). Key growth levers beyond footwear include apparel (up 46% c-c in Q4 2025, growing from 5% to a target ~15% of mix), APAC expansion (China + Japan + Korea retail buildout), and the Cyclon subscription shoe concept.

**How ONON's moat differs from Hoka (most direct peer)**:
- **Price point**: On's flagship Cloud series retails at $150–200; Hoka Clifton/Bondi at $140–165. On has held or expanded prices while maintaining 63%+ GM; Hoka's parent Deckers has seen gross margin pressure at ~55% blended.
- **Design identity**: On's Swiss industrial-design aesthetic (CloudTec pods, alien-looking sole) is more visually distinctive than Hoka's maximalist foam stack. This creates a stronger lifestyle / fashion crossover — ONON sells in concept stores and fashion retailers; Hoka remains primarily run-specialty.
- **DTC mix**: On's 41.8% DTC is materially above Hoka's DTC penetration, giving On better margin and data ownership.
- **Evidence**: Despite aggressive Hoka distribution buildout and celebrity endorsements, ONON revenue grew at +35.6% c-c FY2025 vs. Hoka mid-teens growth — the premium consumer is choosing both but is engaging more with On's aspirational positioning.

**Honest tail-risk competitive read**:
- **Hoka** is well-capitalized (Deckers is a $15B company) and growing rapidly in trail/ultra running — the primary On stronghold. Hoka's Mafate Speed 5 and Speedgoat compete directly with On's Cloudultra and Cloudwander.
- **Nike** has deep resources to defend premium running and could narrow the design gap if ONON's aesthetic becomes commoditized.
- **DTC commoditization**: As every major brand shifts to DTC, On's 41.8% DTC advantage will narrow over 3–5 years.

## Section 4 — Management & Leadership

**Marc Aurel Breu (CEO 2022–2026, CFO until 2022)**: Brought institutional discipline to what had been a founder-driven commercial build. Gross margin expansion from 56% to 63% under his CFO + CEO tenure is the clearest evidence of operational credibility. Departed as CEO effective March 2026; transitioned to advisory role.

**David Allemann + Caspar Coppetti (Co-founders, now Co-CEOs effective ~March 2026)**: Allemann has led brand and design since founding — the *"We are not following trends; we are co-creating culture"* framing is his. Coppetti has led commercial strategy. The co-CEO transition brings the brand's two design/commercial architects back to the top role at a time when On is scaling into apparel, APAC, and lifestyle — precisely the growth vectors that require brand integrity above operational efficiency. Market initially sold off on transition uncertainty.

**Martin Hoffmann (CFO, departing mid-2026)**: Served as CFO and interim co-CEO (alongside Marc Maurer in the prior structure). Long tenure; the 2026 guidance call was delivered under his watch. **Incoming CFO Frank Sluis** joins May 2026 — a transition that introduces short-term execution opacity on financial planning and capital-allocation signaling.

**Olivier Bernhard (Co-founder, advisory)**: The technical athlete-founder; still active in product development. The garden-hose prototype origin story remains the R&D DNA.

**Roger Federer (Co-investor + global ambassador)**: Strategic stakeholder since 2019. The most important non-executive in ONON's capital structure from a brand signal standpoint. His transition from active professional to ambassador may modestly reduce court presence, but his brand longevity is multi-decade.

**Capital allocation**:
- **M&A**: None; organic growth focus
- **Buybacks**: Limited; balance sheet cash CHF 1.02B held for regional expansion
- **Dividend**: None
- **Capex**: CHF 73M FY2025 (+21% YoY) — regional warehouse build-out, retail footprint expansion, tech investment

## Section 5 — Strategic Growth Initiatives

1. **Apparel expansion (5% → 15% revenue mix, 3-year target)**: The highest-conviction internal growth lever. Q4 2025 apparel +46% c-c; over 60% of apparel flows through DTC. As apparel mix increases, gross margin expands (apparel carries higher DTC mix than footwear). The customer-acquisition signal (6% → 10% of new customers acquired through apparel) is the leading indicator that apparel is a top-of-funnel, not just upsell, play.

2. **APAC buildout (highest-growth region, CHF 1B+ FY2025)**: China + Japan + Korea retail expansion. China Tmall top-5 in $140+ footwear; China in-store traffic doubled. APAC grew +85% c-c in Q4 2025. This is the most underpenetrated large market — APAC at ~17% of revenue vs. Americas ~58% — and the one with the longest growth runway.

3. **DTC mix shift (target: >45% by 2027E)**: Continued shift from wholesale to own.com + owned retail. Each 1pt gain in DTC mix = ~30bps gross margin *[Estimate]*. 67 stores planned to expand. DTC gives superior customer data for targeting, product development feedback, and inventory management.

4. **Tennis platform**: Świątek (#1 WTA) + Shelton (top-10 ATP) + Draper give ONON credible court presence. Tennis-shoe SKUs (The Roger Clubhouse, etc.) create premium lifestyle crossover. The on-court category is small but carries outsized brand-signal value relative to revenue.

5. **Innovation pipeline**: LightSpray (3D-sprayed uppers, reduces waste and assembly time); Cyclon (subscription shoe with plant-based materials, circular-economy concept); Cloudwander (hiking crossover) expanding TAM. Innovation keeps premium narrative alive and justifies ASP growth.

6. **Hiking + outdoor lifestyle**: Cloudwander + Cloudaway + Cloudventure positioned in trail/hike segment. The Hoka-vs-On battle for trail running premium is still open. APAC mountain culture (Japan, Korea, China) gives ONON a natural trial base for trail/hike.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Athletic footwear TAM**: ~$140B globally *[Analyst consensus]*; premium segment ($100–300 retail price) growing faster than mass. ONON has <2% global share — substantial raw runway, though premium-segment share is meaningfully higher.

**Running + trail premium sub-segment**: $15–25B global *[Estimate]*. The most attractive sub-segment for ONON — over-indexed relative to total footwear. Nike (Vaporfly + Pegasus), Hoka (Clifton + Bondi + Mafate), ONON (Cloud + Cloudsurfer + Cloudmonster), Adidas (Adizero + Ultraboost), Asics (Nimbus + Cumulus). The $150–250 retail tier is the highest-margin, highest-loyalty zone.

**Tariff macro (2026 primary variable)**: U.S. tariff escalation on Vietnam/China-sourced footwear is the single most material near-term variable for ONON and the broader footwear sector. Nike (35–40% Vietnam sourcing), Adidas (similar), Hoka (Vietnam-heavy) all face identical structural risk. ONON has attempted to diversify but remains substantially Asia-sourced. The 2026 guidance assumes 20% tariff; actual tariff levels and any exemptions or pauses remain uncertain. Tariff escalation could result in industry-wide price increases — which ONON's brand premium supports better than commodity-positioned peers.

**Swiss franc**: CHF has been structurally strong vs. USD (Swiss National Bank policies + safe-haven flows). ONON reports in CHF; ~62% of revenue is USD-denominated (Americas). CHF strength is a persistent structural headwind to reported revenue growth relative to operational performance. FX risk is specifically tied to the USD/CHF rate — not a commodity or interest rate risk.

**Consumer discretionary environment**: Premium footwear has historically shown relative resilience in mild recessions (aspirational consumers protect premium purchases) but is not immune to severe downturns. ONON's price points ($150–250) are discretionary luxury in emerging markets but approaching commodity in affluent U.S./EU consumer segments. The brand's aspirational positioning creates some downside protection.

**Athleisure secular trend**: The work-from-anywhere + health-conscious lifestyle trend that accelerated in 2020–2023 is a durable tailwind, but growth is decelerating from peak levels. ONON's expansion into lifestyle (Cloudaway, Cloudnova) and apparel aims to capture the athleisure spending wallet as running-specific growth moderates.

## Section 10 — BAIT Framework

### B — Behavioral: **Strong**

The –41% drawdown from $61.29 (late 2025) to $36.25 compresses a series of narrative shifts into a single price: (1) the 2026 EBITDA margin guide held flat at ~18.8% rather than expanding to the ~19.5%+ consensus expected, (2) Swiss franc strength created an 800bps reported/c-c gap that makes headline revenue look slower than it is, (3) U.S. tariff escalation on Asian footwear became the dominant macro narrative for consumer discretionary, and (4) the CEO/co-CEO leadership transition introduced execution uncertainty. Each of these factors individually would justify a modest de-rating; all four together in a six-month window created an oversold condition. Yet the underlying brand metrics through Q4 2025 — 63.9% quarterly gross margin (record), +85% c-c APAC growth, +46% c-c apparel growth, apparel now acquired 10% of new customers — are *better* than at the $61 peak. The selloff is behavioral: narrative-led macro mispricing on a brand that hasn't fundamentally deteriorated.

### A — Analytical: **Moderate**

- **Constant-currency vs. reported gap**: FY2025 c-c growth was 35.6% vs. 30% reported — a 560bps distortion that sell-side often reports as the lower headline number. The underlying demand signal is materially stronger than reported figures suggest.
- **Gross margin runway**: 62.8% FY2025 → ≥63% 2026E is still expanding even with tariff headwinds — proving that air-freight reduction and DTC mix shift are more powerful gross-margin tailwinds than tariffs are headwinds. Consensus has not fully modeled the medium-term gross margin trajectory.
- **Apparel optionality**: Growing from 5% to 15% of revenue is an ~$450M revenue lift not in most base-case models. The 10% new-customer-through-apparel signal suggests the mix shift is accelerating.
- **APAC as an underpenetrated compounder**: At ~17% of revenue with +85% c-c Q4 growth, APAC at anything close to Americas penetration would be a step-change in total revenue and mix-quality.

### I — Informational: **Moderate**

- The Q4 2025 transcript explicitly frames tariff guidance as a conservative floor (*"based on the 20% tariff regime"* — any de-escalation = upside). This is a clear asymmetric setup not well-amplified in media coverage.
- The apparel-as-customer-acquisition-channel signal (6% → 10% of new customers from apparel) is buried in the transcript's Q&A section; most coverage focuses on footwear comps.
- Co-CEO transition mechanics and the Breu→Allemann+Coppetti handoff context is poorly understood — the Finviz data shows the market reacted, but primary-source context of what the co-CEOs' stated strategic priorities are is not yet widely available.

### T — Technical: **Weak**

- Clear downtrend from $61 → $31 → current $36 range — no technical reversal confirmed
- $31.41 recent 52-wk low tested in March 2026; current +15% above that level suggests tentative base formation
- No mechanical catalysts (no buyback program, no imminent index rebalancing, no options expiry setup identified)
- Short interest declining (6.3% → 4.8% MoM) — covers happening but no squeeze trigger

### BAIT Verdict

**Double overlap (B Strong + A Moderate)**. Conviction: **Moderate**. The behavioral mispricing case is compelling and analytical edges are real, but tariff risk has empirical teeth and the leadership transition is genuinely unresolved. The technical picture remains weak; a Q1 2026 beat-and-raise would be the catalyst to flip I and T positive, which would upgrade conviction to High.
