## Business Overview

On Holding AG (NYSE: ONON) is a Swiss premium athletic and lifestyle footwear and apparel brand founded in Zurich in 2010. The company designs CloudTec-cushioned running shoes, expanded into apparel, and operates a hybrid DTC + wholesale model. FY2025 net sales reached **CHF 3.0B**, +30% reported and **+35.6% at constant currency**, with a record full-year **gross margin of 62.8%** and adj. EBITDA margin guidance of 18.5–19% for 2026. Q4 2025 sales were CHF 743.8M (+22.6% reported, +30.6% constant-currency), with a record quarterly gross margin of 63.9%. [Source: On Holding Q4 2025 press release, 2026-03-03]

The brand has scaled with a focus on premium positioning (no permanent discounting, athlete endorsements, top-tier marketing — Roger Federer co-investor and brand ambassador since 2019) and a controlled retail footprint (67 global stores). Tennis (Iga Świątek, Ben Shelton), running (Hellen Obiri), and hiking are core sport pillars.

## Moat Assessment

**Narrow** — brand-led premium positioning with limited switching costs but high consumer affinity and pricing power.

**Sources**:
- **Brand premium**: 30% global brand awareness, growing rapidly; CloudTec aesthetic is instantly recognizable
- **Pricing discipline**: Record 62.8% gross margin (FY25) reflects no permanent discounting; rare among growth footwear brands
- **Athlete ecosystem**: Federer (co-investor), Świątek, Shelton, Obiri — top-tier endorsements punch above ad spend
- **DTC mix**: Direct channel ~40%+ of sales — higher margin, owns customer relationship
- **Premium retail footprint**: 67 stores (vs. wholesale-only competitors) — brand-control flywheel

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Footwear is fashion — premium tier is cyclical and trend-sensitive
- Multi-homing across athletic brands (Nike, Hoka, Adidas, New Balance) with no real switching cost
- Hoka (DECK) is the most direct comp and competes hard on running technology
- FX exposure: Swiss franc strength is an ~800bps headwind to reported revenue
- Tariff exposure: U.S. is largest market; Asia-sourced footwear faces tariff escalation risk
- Brand fade risk if athlete endorsements wane or running-shoe trend cools

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Olivier Bernhard, David Allemann, Caspar Coppetti, 2010)**: A former Ironman triathlete cut a garden hose into pieces and glued it to the sole of a running shoe — the prototype of the CloudTec design. The insight: cushioning + structured rebound at the heel-strike could be both performance-credible and aesthetically distinctive. A premium-positioned, designer-led running brand could capture share from Nike/Adidas in the high-end segment by combining Swiss design heritage with athlete-driven proof.

**Today**: On is a global premium athletic + lifestyle brand with CHF 3B in sales, 30% brand awareness, and a 67-store footprint. Federer's 2019 investment + brand-ambassador role created a Hollywood-grade signal effect. The company has expanded from running into tennis, hiking, training, and lifestyle — but running shoes remain the core.

**Pivotal question**: At ~$36, ONON trades at ~20× 2026E EBITDA with the brand still compounding 23%+ constant-currency — is this a brand-compounder mispriced by FX/tariff macro, or a fashion-cycle peak?

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Athletic footwear TAM**: ~$140B globally. ON has <2% share — long runway in raw share terms but premium-segment share is higher.

**Premium running**: Nike (Vaporfly + Pegasus), Hoka (Clifton + Bondi), ON (Cloud + Cloudmonster), Adidas (Adizero + Ultraboost), Asics (Nimbus + Cumulus). Premium pricing tier ($150–250) is the most attractive sub-segment — ON is over-indexed here.

**Tariff macro**: U.S.-Asia footwear tariffs are the active 2026 variable. ON's Vietnam + China sourcing is exposed; mitigation strategies include price increases (already partly executed) and sourcing diversification.

**FX**: Swiss franc has been a persistent headwind. Reporting in CHF with U.S. revenue exposure means dollar weakness flows through as reported headwind even when local-currency growth is strong.
