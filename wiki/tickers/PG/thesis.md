## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding Insight**: P&G (founded 1837) exists to provide branded consumer goods —
household necessities like laundry detergent, diapers, razors, and shampoo — with reliable
quality at a premium price. The mission: serve consumers with superior products in daily-use
categories, leveraging brand equity to sustain pricing power across economic cycles.

**Today**: ~$84B in revenue, ~$15.7B net income. Dividend King with 66+ consecutive years of
dividend increases. Sells in 180+ countries. Top brands: Tide, Pampers, Gillette, Head &
Shoulders, Pantene, Oral-B, Charmin, Bounty, Febreze, Olay, Vicks.

**Current Challenges**: The company is navigating a perfect storm — tariff headwinds of
$1–1.5B annually, consumer trade-down to private labels, a CEO transition (Moeller →
Jejurikar, effective January 1, 2026), and a restructuring (7,000 job cuts, brand divestitures,
market exits). Four consecutive quarters of below-consensus organic growth preceded the
restructuring announcement.

**The Pivotal Investment Question**:
> Is PG's 2025–2026 drawdown (from ~$174 to ~$143) a macro-driven overreaction — a temporary
> tariff + leadership transition headwind creating a value entry at a historically elevated
> dividend yield — or does it reflect genuine structural deterioration (private-label
> encroachment, permanent pricing power erosion, growth deceleration) that justifies a
> permanently lower multiple?
>
> The market has re-rated from ~25–26x P/E to ~20x. The question is whether 20x is fair
> value for 0–4% organic growth, or whether execution improvement under Jejurikar can
> re-expand the multiple.

## Section 5 — Competitive Moat

**Assessment**: Wide Moat (but under pressure from structural headwinds)

| Moat Source | Strength | Current Status |
|-------------|----------|----------------|
| Brand equity | Strong | Intact; Tide/Pampers/Gillette retain #1 positions globally |
| Distribution scale | Strong | Global shelf presence; retailer relationships |
| R&D and product innovation | Moderate | Continuous but incremental; private-label closing quality gap |
| Consumer switching costs | Moderate-Low | Habit formation, but trade-down to private label is real during economic stress |
| Pricing power | Strong historically | Under pressure; mid-single-digit price increases risk accelerating volume losses |

**Moat Assessment Context**: The tariff environment of 2025 exposed a vulnerability —
pricing power that drives margins is also the mechanism through which volume can be lost to
private-label alternatives. The April 2025 guidance cut and restructuring announcement
represent a management acknowledgment that volume trends are not immune to over-pricing.
The market's P/E compression (from ~26x to ~20x) reflects a structural growth rate
de-rating, not just a temporary headwind.

**Compared to peers**: Colgate-Palmolive (CL) trades at ~18x forward P/E; Unilever at ~18x.
PG's 20x premium requires demonstrating superior organic growth execution vs. peers.

## Section 6 — Management & Leadership

**CEO Jon Moeller (outgoing)**: Led PG through COVID pricing strength and the 2022
commodity cost surge. Announced guidance cut (April 2025) citing tariffs, then announced
own succession.

**CEO Shailesh Jejurikar (incoming, effective January 1, 2026)**: 36-year P&G veteran,
former COO. Named as successor in July 2025 announcement. Described as a "continuity pick"
rather than a change agent. First earnings as CEO: Q2 FY2026 (January 22, 2026) — EPS $1.78
vs. $1.87 expected — not a confidence-building debut.

**Management Assessment**: Continuity leadership in a turnaround situation. The board did
not bring in an outside change agent, which limits the re-rating premium associated with new
leadership. Market will need 2–3 earnings cycles under Jejurikar before re-rating the stock
on management quality. Restructuring charges ($1.5–2.0B over FY2026–FY2027) will create
GAAP noise for the next 12–18 months.

> Key quote on CEO transition: "This could be more like the passing of the baton in a long
> race rather than shaking things up." — Brian Jacobsen, Annex Wealth Management

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Consumer Staples Sector Context**: PG is the archetypal defensive consumer staples stock.
The sector typically outperforms in recessions (non-cyclical demand) and underperforms in
bull markets (lower growth). The 2025–2026 period is unusual: PG is underperforming even
within the consumer staples sector (XLP), which itself is approximately flat over 1 year
while PG is -18%.

**Move Classification Analysis (5-Year)**:

| Factor Type | Frequency | PG-Specific Dynamic |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| EXT-MACRO (Rate cycles, inflation, geopolitics) | ~40% of major moves | P/E contingent on low rates; 26x requires <2% rates; 20x more defensible at 4–5% |
| INT-FUND (Earnings misses, guidance cuts) | ~40% of major moves | 2025–2026 decline driven by genuine intrinsic deterioration — 4 consecutive misses |
| EXT-MARKET (Defensive rotation) | ~20% of major moves | PG benefits when tech/growth sells off; temporary |
| INT-PEOPLE (CEO transition) | ~10% of major moves | Jejurikar transition added uncertainty premium |

**Key Macro Insight**: Unlike the 2022 drawdown (primarily macro/rate-driven), the 2025–2026
decline has a genuine intrinsic component — four consecutive below-consensus organic growth
quarters, a guidance cut, and leadership/restructuring uncertainty. This makes the current
level harder to call as a "macro overreaction" entry point.

**Most predictive driver**: Interest rates and consumer staples sector sentiment (extrinsic)
over short horizons; organic volume growth and pricing power execution (intrinsic) over longer
horizons. When both are negative simultaneously (as in 2025), drawdowns are severe.

## Section 15 — Bottom Line

**Verdict**: Watch / Conditional — insufficient upside for high-conviction positioning

PG is a Dividend King in "show me" mode under a new CEO mid-restructuring. The stock is
neither screaming cheap nor obviously overvalued at ~20x forward earnings. The dividend
yield (~2.94%) is at its highest since 2019, providing an income floor. However, the
lack of a clear catalyst for multiple re-expansion and the ongoing organic growth deceleration
(0–4% guided) limits the risk/reward vs. higher-conviction growth names.

**1-Year Target**: $155–$165 (Base case, contingent on Q3 FY2026 execution). Requires
organic growth acceleration to 3–5%.

**3-Year Target**: $165–$180 (Bull case requires sustained growth re-acceleration and
multiple normalization). Total return including dividends: ~20–30% over 3 years.

**Portfolio %**: Small or watch position recommended. Lower priority vs. SHOP, BKNG, SCHW.

**Key Monitoring Triggers**:

| Trigger | Date | What to Watch |
|---------|------|--------------|
| Q3 FY2026 Earnings | April 24, 2026 | Organic sales growth rate, tariff cost update, restructuring progress |
| Dividend Ex-Date | ~April 2026 | Confirms dividend sustainability; Q-o-Q income floor |
| CEO Jejurikar public strategy | As disclosed | Any new strategic direction vs. continuity signal |
| Private-label market share data | Ongoing | Nielsen/IRI scanner data showing category share trends |
| Tariff policy developments | Ongoing | Any US-China or import tariff changes affecting household goods |

**Thesis Break Signal**: Further guidance cut below 0% organic growth, or dividend
cut/freeze, would signal fundamental deterioration beyond current thesis.

**Historical Context**: At $143.35, PG is at mid-2023 levels. The stock sits at the 43rd
percentile of its 5-year range. The last time PG was a sustained multi-year underperformer
was the 2016–2018 activist investor period (Nelson Peltz/Trian Fund) — which ultimately
resolved with a board seat and strategic improvements. A similar re-rating catalyst
(activist pressure, transformative CEO, portfolio rationalization) could provide the spark
for multiple expansion.
