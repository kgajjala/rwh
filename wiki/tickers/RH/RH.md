## Business Overview

RH (NYSE: RH) is a luxury home furnishings retailer and lifestyle brand operating 75 immersive Design Galleries across the US, Canada, and Europe (as of January 31, 2026: 69 North America + 6 Europe). The brand spans RH Interiors, Modern, Contemporary, Beach House, Ski House, Outdoor, Baby & Child, Teen, and an expanding luxury hospitality / restaurant ecosystem (RH Guesthouse New York, RH Yountville, integrated restaurants in 25 galleries). The luxury sub-brand **Waterworks** (acquired ~2016) adds premium bath/kitchen ($198M FY2025 revenue at 52.9% gross margin).

**FY2025 (ended January 31, 2026)**: Revenue $3.44B (+8.1%); Adj EBITDA $596.5M (17.3% margin); Net income $124.8M; FCF $252M; inventory reduced from $1.02B to $819M (–20%, a major positive operational signal). Revenue growth of +15% on a 2-year basis outpaced all major home furnishings peers — Arhaus +7%, Wayfair +4%, West Elm 0%, Pottery Barn –6%, Ethan Allen –15% — even during the worst housing cycle in decades.

The multi-year saga: Gary Friedman (CEO since 2001/2017) deployed ~$2B+ in buybacks at elevated prices (FY2023 alone: $1.25B), leaving net financial debt of ~$2.35B and annual interest expense of $225M. Simultaneous headwinds — US housing freeze (existing home sales at multi-decade lows), China-sourcing tariff regime ($190M Q4 impact), and international expansion pre-opening costs — compressed margins from 29% adj EBITDA peak (FY2021) to a guided 14–16% for FY2026.

Spring 2026: RH launches **RH Estates** — new ultra-premium line featuring RH Bespoke furniture and RH Couture upholstery, described internally as *"the most prolific collection of new products in our history"* and *"a value proposition that we believe will be disruptive across multiple markets"* ([RH FY2025 10-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1528849/000110465926037992/rh-20260131x10k.htm)).

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Is RH at the generational trough of a cyclical luxury home drawdown — where inventory destocking is complete, product transformation creates a demand catalyst, and a housing recovery inflects operating leverage from 17% to 25%+ adj EBITDA margins — or is the equity a highly-leveraged option on a recovery that may be 2–3 years further away, with shareholder litigation and persistent tariff complexity compressing the equity value before the recovery arrives?

The bull case requires: (a) housing recovery 2027–2028, (b) tariff resourcing completed H2 2026, (c) RH Estates and international galleries (Madrid, Paris, London, Milan) generating material revenue contribution by 2028, and (d) debt-free trajectory by 2029. The bear case: housing freeze extends to 2028, tariffs remain unpredictable (IEEPA struck down Feb 20, 2026 then replaced by new tariffs), Germany Gallery impairments repeat in other international markets, and leverage stress forces dilutive financing.

### Business Model Levers

The operating leverage thesis depends on two things working simultaneously:
1. **Revenue per gallery inflection**: The ~70 North America galleries need to collectively grow revenue toward $5–6B (from ~$3.1B today) — requires housing recovery + RH Estates product upgrade + memberships growth.
2. **International contribution**: Six galleries generating minimal contribution today vs. a potential 20–30% of global revenue by 2030; the $20–25B global target requires successful replication of the US model across Europe, UK, and potentially Asia.

**Hospitality economics** (per FY2025 10-K forward-looking): *"Our view is that [hospitality] is an integrated component of our core business that we expect to generate approximately 65% of the aggregate rent of associated galleries."* This effectively means restaurant/hospitality helps subsidize gallery real estate costs.

## Section 5 — Strategic Growth Initiatives

1. **Product transformation: RH Estates** (Spring 2026): Launch of ultra-premium RH Bespoke furniture and RH Couture upholstery. Management calls it the most prolific product launch in industry history. Key test: does ASP expansion offset lower unit velocity in a down housing cycle?

2. **International expansion** (2026–2030): RH Madrid, RH Paris, RH London, RH Milan planned for 2026. Each Design Gallery requires 2–3 year ramp to full contribution. Pre-opening costs: ~270bps drag on FY2026 adj EBITDA, 420bps in Q1. The international JV (equity method) turned from –$11.4M loss (FY2024) to +$5.0M income (FY2025) — early positive signal.

3. **Tariff resourcing** (H2 2026 target): 40% of core assortment resourcing from China to Vietnam, Indonesia, Mexico, Italy. Supreme Court struck down IEEPA tariffs Feb 20, 2026 — but new tariffs then announced under alternative authority. Ongoing uncertainty is the single most quantified near-term P&L risk.

4. **Real estate monetization** ($200–250M/yr): Planned asset sales to support FCF and debt paydown. FY2026 adj FCF guide of $300–400M includes this. Key question: whether buyers exist at acceptable prices in a high-rate environment.

5. **Debt paydown trajectory**: Target debt-free by 2029. Net financial debt: $2,347M. Annual adj FCF: $300–400M guide. At $200M per year of pure debt reduction (stripping asset sales), 2029 target requires ~$600M+ in organic paydown capacity — requires margin recovery.

6. **RH Guesthouse expansion**: Second RH Guesthouse (Aspen) under construction after New York flagship. Brand halo in the $200B North American hotel market.

7. **New business extensions**: RH Residences (branded luxury real estate), RH Media (content/publishing), The World of RH (digital platform) — all in early-stage ideation/launch. Optionality but not near-term revenue.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**TAM**: US home furnishings market ~$200B; premium/luxury subset (~$800+ furniture, branded) ~$30–40B. RH at $3.44B captures ~9–10% of the luxury sub-segment — highest branded share in US premium home. Global luxury home market is fragmented; RH's $20–25B long-term global target assumes ~2–3% global luxury home market share.

**The Housing Cycle Driver**: Home furnishings is among the most cyclically-sensitive retail categories. The demand driver is not housing *prices* but housing *turnover* (existing home sales) — approximately 5.5–6.0M/yr at full velocity vs. multi-decade lows in 2024–2026. New movers spend 3–5× more on home furnishings in the first year than established residents. RH's Gallery format and high-ticket SKUs are particularly sensitive to this variable.

**Tariff Regime (April 2026 status)**: Highly uncertain. IEEPA tariffs struck down by US Supreme Court (Feb 20, 2026), then new tariffs announced under alternative statutory authority scheduled to expire in 150 days absent Congressional action. Management: *"There remains substantial uncertainty regarding the duration of existing and newly announced tariffs, potential changes or pauses to such tariffs."* The tariff headwind is both a P&L drag AND a supply-chain disruption (resourcing takes 12–18 months per category; backorder/special orders impacted Q4 by ~$30M).

**Consumer macro**: High-end luxury home demand bifurcated — ultra-HNW (~top 0.1%) relatively resilient; upper-middle-class luxury (the core RH buyer) has retrenched as housing affordability stress constrains move-up activity. Analyst commentary April 2026: *"even the ultrarich are worried about spending on their homes."*

**Competitive dynamics**: RH's 2-year revenue growth of +15% vs. Pottery Barn –6% and Ethan Allen –15% during the cycle trough demonstrates durable share gains — the Gallery transformation and product elevation are working even in the worst environment. This is the bull case's most underappreciated data point.
