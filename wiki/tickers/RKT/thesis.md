## 1. Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight**: The American homeownership process — search, offer, finance, close, refinance — is absurdly fragmented. Realtors, mortgage brokers, banks, servicers, title companies, and appraisers operate as disconnected silos with no shared data and significant consumer friction. Rocket's thesis is that a single technology platform with shared data and AI can do all of this better, faster, and cheaper, capturing a fee at every step of the homeownership lifecycle.

**Pivotal question**: Is this platform transformation real and durable, or is RKT still fundamentally a rate-sensitive origination company that will compress in a sustained high-rate environment?

**The $50B question**: At current EV of ~$50B, the market is pricing RKT somewhere between "large cyclical" and "early platform." The bear case is that Redfin and Mr. Cooper are expensive acquisitions that dilute ROIC without meaningfully reducing cyclicality. The bull case is that the flywheel is just beginning to compound — and a 2026-2027 rate relief environment reveals the full earnings power.

## 9. Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**The housing lock-in effect**: 85-90% of outstanding mortgages have rates below 5%. With 30-year rates at 6.5-7.0%, almost nobody wants to sell. This "lock-in effect" has suppressed both purchase volumes and refi volumes simultaneously — creating the trough that Rocket is navigating.

**The unlock scenario**: Any sustained move of 30-year rates toward 5.75-6.25% would simultaneously (a) unlock purchase volume as sellers decide to move, (b) trigger a refi wave as underwater borrowers escape 7%+ mortgages, (c) increase Rocket's origination volume at high GOS margins. Even a 50-75bps rate relief = industry-wide $300-500B+ volume increase.

**Mortgage market sizing**:
- 2023 trough: ~$1.3T originations
- 2024 recovery: ~$1.6-1.8T
- Normal market: ~$2.0-2.5T
- Refi boom potential: $3.0-4.0T (2020 analog)

**Market share dynamics**: Rocket gained market share during the trough (competitors exited). Non-bank lenders now control ~65%+ of origination vs. banks. Rocket at 25% of non-bank = ~16% total market share. This structural shift toward non-bank is Rocket's tailwind.

## 12. BAIT Framework

### B — Behavioral: STRONG
A -38.5% drawdown from the 52-week high in the 30 days following a Q4 2025 earnings beat (adj revenue $1.7B vs. $1.56B guide, EBITDA $592M) is a textbook behavioral overreaction. The selloff is attributable to macro anxiety (Trump tariffs, re-inflation fears, rate uncertainty) — not company-specific deterioration. Krishna pre-announced "4-year volume high" on CNBC Squawk Box before earnings; the Q4 numbers confirmed. The market gave back the entire post-earnings bounce and more. Sentiment is now pricing in bear case.

### A — Analytical: MODERATE-STRONG
The Up-C collapse makes historical EPS comparisons numerically misleading — most analysts still haven't cleanly articulated pro-forma earnings. MSR fair value changes distort GAAP revenue by billions in either direction — most media summaries cite GAAP revenue, missing the adjusted picture entirely. Q4 EBITDA of $592M, if annualized, implies $2.37B run-rate EBITDA vs. EV of $50B = ~21x — which sounds expensive until you model $3.5-5B 2027 EBITDA potential. Q1 2026 guidance of $2.6-2.8B adj revenue (annualized $10-11B) has beat 4 consecutive quarters; consensus hasn't yet adjusted for synergy pull-forward.

### I — Informational: MODERATE
Primary disclosures underappreciated by media:
1. Krishna's Squawk Box "4-year volume high" pre-announcement — a public signal most coverage missed
2. Quantified AI operating leverage data (800K chats/month, 2.5x conversion, half headcount) — proof points most summaries omitted
3. CFO Brown's explicit statement that Mr. Cooper synergies are "ahead of the original end-of-2027 target" — consensus hasn't priced in the timeline pull-forward
4. Redfin mortgage attach rate 27% → 40% in 4 months — faster integration than anticipated by any analyst

### T — Technical: WEAK-MODERATE
Limited technical catalysts vs. BKNG:
- No stock split announced; no index inclusion imminent; Dan Gilbert's control limits buyback optionality
- However: Q1 2026 earnings April 30 is a 25-day hard catalyst
- Stock is at/near 30-day lows; RSI likely oversold
- Up-C collapse increased public float from ~7% to ~35% — institutional ownership should be growing as the company becomes a proper C-corp and enters the institutional-ownership universe

### BAIT Verdict: Double Overlap (B+A) — Moderate Conviction
RKT shows meaningful behavioral overselling post-beat and genuine analytical complexity that obscures true earnings power. However, the BAIT signal is less clean than BKNG (B+A+T triple overlap) because: (1) the bear case has real fundamental merit (rate sensitivity, integration risk, goodwill); (2) the technical tailwinds are absent (no split, no buyback); (3) the informational edge is narrower. The behavioral fear appears disproportionate — a 38.5% drawdown on a beat-and-raise quarter — but the platform thesis requires more execution proof before achieving triple/quadruple BAIT status.

## 15. Bottom Line

**RKT is a moderate-conviction, 0.75-1.5% portfolio position — a higher-risk, higher-potential-reward bet than SCHW or BKNG, sized accordingly.**

The thesis in one sentence: Rocket Companies has assembled the most complete homeownership platform in history (search via Redfin + Compass, origination via #1 lender, servicing via #1 servicer) at a moment when AI is allowing it to do the same volume with half the headcount — and the housing market is just beginning to unlock after 3 years of rate-induced freeze.

**1-year target**: ~$18-22 (base case); achievable if Q1 2026 confirms Q4 trajectory ($2.6-2.8B adj revenue guidance — beat 4 consecutive quarters)
**3-year target**: ~$22-35 (base to moderate bull); requires integration synergy delivery and stable-to-improving rates; does not require a refi boom
**Portfolio allocation**: 0.75-1.5%; options-enhanced with LEAPS to structure asymmetric payoff
**Form**: 65% stock / 35% LEAPS

**Where to push back vs. SCHW/BKNG**: RKT carries more idiosyncratic execution risk (acquisition integration, Kelman departure, RESPA) AND more macro sensitivity (no international diversification like BKNG; no NII hedge like SCHW). The $10.6B goodwill and complex accounting make this harder to underwrite with precision. Sizing at 1% is appropriate; going above 1.5% requires significantly higher conviction on integration thesis or rate path.

**Verdict**: Buy (Speculative). Start Tranche 1 at $14-16. Add at $10-12 if macro deterioration creates an entry. Trim/reassess at $24+ or if any thesis-break trigger fires.
