## Business Overview

Charles Schwab Corporation (NYSE: SCHW) is the largest U.S. retail brokerage by client assets. Following the 2020 acquisition of TD Ameritrade — fully integrated by year-end 2025 — Schwab sits in a duopoly with privately-held Fidelity at the top of the U.S. retail brokerage stack. As of Q1 2026, the firm holds **$11.8T in total client assets** across **~46.5M client accounts** (the modest sequential dip from $11.9T at YE2025 reflects market drawdown more than asset outflow; Q1 core net new assets of $158B was a Q1 record).

Revenue is a four-stream model: (1) **Net Interest Revenue** (~49% of FY2025 revenue) — the spread Schwab earns on client cash sweep balances held at Schwab Bank; the dominant earnings engine and the most rate-sensitive line. NIM recovered from a 2.33% trough (2023–2024 "cash sorting") to 2.90% in FY2025 and is guided to 2.85–2.95% for 2026 with management explicitly flagging "upward momentum" given the Fed-on-hold rate environment. (2) **Asset Management & Admin Fees** (~24%) — fees on Schwab Funds, Schwab Intelligent Portfolios, advisor programs, and increasingly active ETF monetization. (3) **Trading Revenue** (~9%) — order flow (PFOF) plus options/derivatives commissions; Q1 2026 daily-average trades hit a record 9.9M (+34% YoY) but revenue per trade declined as traders take smaller, shorter-duration positions. (4) **Other** (~18%) — banking products, margin lending, Schwab Advisor Services (the #1 RIA custodian by assets).

The defining 2023–2025 dynamic — clients shifting low-yielding sweep cash into money-market funds while Schwab plugged the funding gap with expensive FHLB/CD borrowings (peak ~$97B in 2023) — was declared structurally resolved in 2H2025: high-cost debt was reduced to $5.1B by year-end, and sweep cash *rebuilt* organically (+$13.5B in Q3 2025 alone, +$8B sequential in Q1 2026). Q1 2026 EPS of $1.43 (+38% YoY) put the firm on track to *exceed* the prior $5.70–$5.80 FY26 EPS guidance range; management plans to refresh formal guidance in July.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Charles Schwab, 1971)**: Brokerage commissions were fixed by regulation at $70+ per trade. Schwab's bet was that the true product was the *platform and custody*, not the commission. The 1975 May Day deregulation validated the model; Schwab built the discount brokerage industry. Fifty years later, the founding insight reached its logical endpoint — *zero* commissions — and Schwab now monetizes the float on client cash (NII) and value-added services (advisory, asset management, banking).

**Today**: Post-TD Ameritrade, Schwab is America's #1 retail brokerage at $11.8T client assets. The strategy is "asset monetization" — gather assets at scale (brand, platform, zero commissions, RIA channel), then earn spread/fee income on those assets.

**Pivotal question**: Is the post-2025 earnings power — clean balance sheet + TD synergies + 2.85%+ NIM + 5.1% organic NNA growth + AI/digital-assets/private-markets/active-ETF monetization layers — durable enough to justify a re-rating, or has the market correctly identified a high-quality but rate-dependent business whose fair multiple is 17–19×?

**Primary macro drivers**:
