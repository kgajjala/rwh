## Business Description

Charles Schwab Corporation is the largest U.S. retail brokerage by client assets, with
**$11.9 trillion** in total client assets and 46.5 million client accounts as of end-2025.
Following the 2020 acquisition of TD Ameritrade (integration fully completed 2025), Schwab
is effectively a duopoly with Fidelity at the top of retail brokerage.

**Revenue streams (FY2025)**:
1. **Net Interest Revenue** (~49% of revenue): Interest on client cash sweep balances held
   in Schwab Bank, minus interest paid. NIM recovered to 2.90% in 2025, up from ~2.33%
   during the 2023–2024 "cash sorting" trough.
2. **Asset Management & Admin Fees** (~24%): Fees on proprietary funds (Schwab Funds,
   CSIM), Schwab Intelligent Portfolios, and managed account programs.
3. **Trading Revenue** (~9%): Order flow revenue (PFOF) and options commissions.
   Commission-free equity trading has made this a smaller share.
4. **Other** (~18%): Banking products, margin lending, advisor services.

The defining 2023–2025 dynamic was **"cash sorting"** — clients moved low-yielding sweep
cash into higher-yielding money market funds, crimping NII. This fully reversed in 2H2025
as rates stabilized and high-cost FHLB debt ($97B peak → $5.1B by Q4 2025) was retired.
TD Ameritrade integration complete unlocks synergies.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **Is Schwab's NII recovery (NIM at 2.90%; 2026 target 2.85–2.95%) the start of a
> multi-year earnings normalization that justifies a re-rating, or will Fed rate cuts,
> Fidelity price competition, and zero-commission commoditization cap the earnings ceiling?**

Bull: Cash sorting is over; high-cost debt fully retired; TD Ameritrade synergies now flowing;
$519B net new assets in 2025 (5.1% organic growth); NII compounds at 10-15% in a stable
rate environment. Bear: A rate cut cycle compresses NIM back toward 2.0%; Fidelity and
Vanguard (private entities) compete irrationally; PFOF regulatory risk; retail trading
volumes fall in a bear market.

## Moat Rating

- **Rating**: Narrow-Wide
- **Sources**:
  - Switching costs: IRA/retirement account portability is costly (tax implications, admin
    friction, platform familiarity — especially post-TD Ameritrade absorption)
  - Scale: $11.9T AUM = massive cost advantage in technology, clearing, and custody
  - Brand: "Schwab" = low-cost, trustworthy retail brand built over 50+ years
  - RIA network: Schwab Advisor Services is the #1 RIA custodian by assets
- **Pressure**:
  - Zero-commission world has eroded trading revenue as moat source
  - Fidelity (private) and Vanguard compete on cost without profit obligations
  - NII is rate-sensitive: significant rate cut cycle compresses the engine
