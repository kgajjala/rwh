## Business Description

Shopify is the world's largest independent e-commerce platform, founded in 2006 by Tobias
Lütke after he built a storefront to sell snowboards online and found no adequate platform
existed. The mission: make commerce better for everyone by providing the essential internet
infrastructure for retail businesses of any size.

Shopify powers millions of merchants in 175+ countries, processed $378B in GMV in 2025,
and commands over 14% of US e-commerce market share. It operates as a platform-of-platforms:
online stores, POS hardware, payments (Shopify Payments / Shop Pay), shipping, capital/lending,
B2B wholesale, and now the infrastructure layer connecting merchants to AI agents via the
Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP).

**Business model**: Platform infrastructure. Shopify collects a subscription fee plus a
percentage of every transaction. Owns no inventory, runs no warehouses, carries no retail
risk. Revenue splits between Merchant Solutions (~73%) and Subscription Solutions (~27%).

## Competitive Moat — Wide and Widening

**Assessment**: Wide Moat

**Sources of Moat**:

1. **Switching costs (primary)**: Online store + payments + POS + shipping + lending +
   advertising + AI storefronts are fully integrated. Enterprise brands like GM, L'Oréal,
   Estée Lauder do not switch lightly. 11+ consecutive quarters of 25%+ growth (ex-logistics)
   demonstrates retention.

2. **Platform network effects**: 875M+ unique shoppers. Shop Pay network creates consumer-side
   lock-in. Shop App native GMV grew +94% YoY in 2025.

3. **Data and AI advantage**: $378B GMV across millions of merchants — one of the most diverse
   commerce datasets on the internet. Powers Catalog's specialized LLMs and Sidekick AI.

4. **First-mover in agentic commerce**: Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) co-developed with
   Google, endorsed by Visa, Mastercard, Stripe, Adyen, AmEx, Target, Walmart, Etsy, Wayfair,
   and 20+ others. Agentic Storefronts activating for all stores by default late March 2026.
   OpenAI's retreat from "Instant Checkout" (only ~30 merchants onboarded) validates that
   e-commerce fulfillment complexity flows through Shopify, not around it.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Does AI disrupt Shopify (LLM tools let merchants build without a platform, or AI agents
> bypass checkout entirely) — or does AI amplify Shopify (multi-channel, multi-currency,
> multi-agent commerce complexity makes Shopify's infrastructure MORE necessary, and its
> UCP/Catalog become the connective tissue between merchants and every AI surface)?

The market prices the former. Primary data — 15x growth in AI-originated orders, UCP
co-developed with Google, OpenAI routing checkout to Shopify storefronts — supports the
latter. The thesis is an "AND" not an "OR": AI surfaces discover, Shopify handles fulfillment.
