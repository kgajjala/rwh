## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding Insight**: In 2004, Tobias Lütke wanted to sell snowboards online, found no
adequate platform, and built one. That store became Shopify in 2006. Mission: make commerce
better for everyone by providing essential internet infrastructure for retail businesses of
any size.

**Today**: World's largest independent e-commerce platform. Millions of merchants in 175+
countries. $378B GMV in 2025. 14%+ US e-commerce market share. Platform-of-platforms: online
stores, POS hardware, payments, shipping, capital/lending, B2B wholesale, and now AI commerce
infrastructure via the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP).

**In Buffett terms**: Shopify is the toll booth on entrepreneurial commerce. It collects a
subscription fee plus a percentage of every transaction. No inventory, no warehouses, no
retail risk — pure platform, pure infrastructure.

**The Pivotal Investment Question**:
> Does AI disrupt Shopify (LLM tools let merchants build without a platform, or AI agents
> bypass checkout entirely) — or does AI amplify Shopify (multi-channel, multi-currency,
> multi-agent commerce complexity makes Shopify's infrastructure MORE necessary, and its
> UCP/Catalog become the connective tissue between merchants and every AI surface)?
>
> The market fears the former. The data — 15x growth in AI-originated orders since Jan 2025,
> UCP co-developed with Google, endorsed by 20+ retailers/payment providers, and OpenAI's
> retreat from Instant Checkout (only ~30 merchants onboarded) — supports the latter.

## Section 5 — Competitive Moat

**Assessment**: Wide Moat (Wide and Widening)

| Moat Source | Strength | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| Switching costs | Primary | Full stack integration (store + payments + POS + shipping + lending + ads + AI). GM, L'Oréal, Estée Lauder migrations signal enterprise stickiness |
| Platform network effects | Strong | 875M+ unique shoppers; Shop Pay network; Shop App native GMV +94% YoY |

| Agentic commerce first-mover | Emerging | UCP co-developed with Google; 20+ endorsers incl. Visa, Mastercard, Stripe, Target, Walmart |

**OpenAI's Checkout Retreat validates the moat**: OpenAI abandoned "Instant Checkout" after
only ~30 Shopify merchants were successfully onboarded. Revised model routes users to Shopify
merchant storefronts for checkout — exactly the "AND" thesis.

## Section 7 — Strategic Growth Initiatives

1. **Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)**: Open standard for AI agent commerce. Co-developed
   with Google, endorsed by 20+ retailers/payment providers including Visa, Mastercard, Stripe,
   Adyen, AmEx, Target, Walmart, Etsy, Wayfair. Supports REST, MCP, A2A, and AP2 protocols.
   Described as the "TCP/IP of commerce."

2. **Agentic Storefronts**: Activating by default for ALL Shopify stores late March 2026
   across ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, Gemini, Copilot. AI traffic up 7x, orders up 11x since
   Jan 2025. OpenAI charges 4% fee; Google charges nothing.

3. **$2B Share Buyback**: First meaningful capital return program. Executing via pre-arranged
   algorithmic trading. Signals management's undervaluation view.

4. **B2B Commerce**: GMV +96% in FY2025, +84% in Q4. Still small base but expanding rapidly.
   Enterprise B2B customers consolidating D2C + wholesale on Shopify.

5. **International Expansion**: Nearly half of merchant base outside North America. Europe
   GMV +49%. Payments in 16 new regions. International revenue +36% — accelerating.

6. **Shop Campaigns**: Revenue doubled, adoption tripled in 2025. High-margin advertising
   revenue leveraging Shopify's commerce dataset.

7. **Enterprise / Plus Migration**: Major brands joining: General Motors, Sonos, L'Oréal,
   Benetton, Keurig Dr. Pepper, Amer Sports (Wilson, Salomon). Plus MRR is 34% of total.

affects cross-border merchants), consumer confidence in SMB spending segments, and SaaS
multiple compression from broader rate environment.

**Key stat**: 5-year revenue CAGR of ~31.6% at $11.6B scale makes Shopify one of the
fastest-growing large-cap technology companies globally.
