## Business Overview

SharkNinja (NYSE: SN) is a global designer and marketer of differentiated consumer products under the Shark and Ninja brands, spanning **eight subcategories**: cleaning appliances (Shark vacuums, steam mops), cooking (Ninja air fryers, blenders, ice cream makers, grills), beauty (Shark FlexStyle, beauty hair tools), beverages (Ninja CREAMi, slushie makers), food preparation, fans/heaters/air purifiers, outdoor cooking (Ninja Woodfire), and increasingly **personal care + smart home + outdoor lifestyle**. The business spun off from JS Global in 2023 and has compounded revenue +20%+/year since.

The model is "asset-lite manufacturing": SharkNinja designs and markets; contract manufacturers (~70% China, with Vietnam/Thailand/Malaysia/Cambodia/Indonesia diversification underway) build. Sales channel is ~60% U.S. domestic (Amazon, big-box, DTC) and ~35% international, growing.

**Q4 2025 results** (released February 11, 2026): Net sales $2.1B (+17.6% YoY), domestic +15.7% to $1.37B, international +21.4% to $729M; adjusted EBITDA $395M (+36%) at 18.8% margin (+250 bps YoY); adjusted GM 48.2% (+40 bps despite tariff drag); adj diluted EPS $1.93 vs. $1.40 prior year [Source: SharkNinja Q4 2025 press release, IR, February 11, 2026]. **FY2026 Guide**: net sales +10–11%; adj EBITDA $1.27–1.28B (+12–13%); adj diluted EPS $5.90–6.00 (+12–14%) — **assumes current tariff levels persist** (20% China/Vietnam, 19% Indonesia/Thailand/Malaysia/Cambodia).

## Moat Assessment

**Narrow** — innovation cadence + brand + category share; not a structural moat.

**Sources**:
- **Innovation engine**: ~25+ new product launches/year; consistently first-mover in adjacent categories (Ninja CREAMi, Shark FlexStyle were category-defining)
- **Brand portfolio**: Shark (cleaning) + Ninja (cooking) — both have strong unaided awareness in U.S.; ~$5–7B combined retail value
- **Channel scale**: Amazon top-seller status in 30+ subcategories; major shelf at Costco, Target, Walmart, Best Buy
- **Asset-lite model**: ROIC structurally higher than traditional appliance peers; ~25%+ ROIC vs. 10–15% for Whirlpool/Helen of Troy
- **Vertical integration in design + IP**: Patent portfolio defends key features; design-led culture

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Categories are not durable franchises — Dyson, Bissell, Cuisinart, KitchenAid all credible competitors
- Tariff exposure: ~70% China-manufactured at peak; mitigation in progress but a real margin drag
- Channel concentration: Amazon is ~30%+ of revenue; pricing/policy risk
- Innovation pipeline must keep delivering — a single missed cycle (e.g., a flop CREAMi-class launch) could compress growth
- Macro: discretionary durable goods exposure
- JS Global parent overhang: still ~40%+ ownership; share lockups + secondary risk

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Mark Barrocas, SharkNinja CEO)**: Mass-market appliance categories were dominated by tired incumbents (Hoover, Cuisinart, Conair, Black+Decker) running on legacy designs. By combining (a) consumer-insight-led product design, (b) fast iteration cycles (12–18 months to market), and (c) value-tier pricing with premium-tier features, SharkNinja could disrupt category after category — and the rolling product launch cadence would compound revenue without ever depending on a single hit.

**Today**: ~$6.8B revenue, 8+ subcategories, expanding into beauty/outdoor/personal care. Spun from JS Global in July 2023; trades publicly with strong post-IPO compounding.

**Pivotal question**: Does the rolling-innovation flywheel sustain? If yes, $6 EPS in 2026 → $9–10 by 2028 at the same multiple = $170–190 stock. If no — single failed cycle + tariff drag — and the stock re-rates lower with no growth offset.

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Small appliance / housewares TAM**: ~$60–80B globally; U.S. ~$25B. SharkNinja at ~$6.8B is ~10% U.S. share + growing international — material runway in addressable categories (especially beauty + outdoor + personal care expansion).

**Tariff environment**: Trump-era reciprocal tariffs effective 2025 + China-specific carve-outs created major mid-cycle disruption for China-manufacturing-heavy CPG. SN management's FY26 guide bakes in 19–20% rates as steady-state. Any escalation = guide cut; any rollback = upside.

**Consumer macro**: Discretionary durables sensitive to home equity + housing turnover (vacuum/cleaning correlated to moves) + disposable income (cooking gadgets correlated to gift-giving cycles). Inflation moderation in 2025–2026 has been net supportive.

**Retail consolidation**: Big-box (Target, Best Buy, Walmart) is disciplined on shelf; Amazon dominates DTC. SN's premium shelf positions are a defensible asset.
