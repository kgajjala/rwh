## Business Overview

Tesla (NASDAQ: TSLA) is the leading western pure-play EV manufacturer plus a vertically integrated energy storage business plus an AI / autonomy / robotics platform. The company splits operationally into: (1) **Automotive** — Model 3, Y, S, X, Cybertruck, plus the imminent Cybercab and an entry-level "Model 2" successor, (2) **Energy Generation & Storage** — Megapack utility-scale + Powerwall residential, (3) **Services & Other** — supercharging, used cars, parts, (4) **Optionality** — FSD (Full Self-Driving) software, Robotaxi network (launched in Austin June 2025; Dallas + Houston unsupervised April 2026), Optimus humanoid robot, Dojo training compute.

Q1 2026 revenue $22.38B (+16% YoY); GAAP net income $1.45B; gross margin 21.1% (highest in years); FCF $1.44B (sharp positive surprise vs. –$1.57B consensus); 358,023 vehicles delivered (missed by ~7,600 units); 50,000+ inventory build [Source: Tesla Q1 2026 earnings, April 22, 2026]. Cybercab production "officially begun" per Musk on Q1 call [Source: Bloomberg, April 24, 2026].

## Moat Assessment

**Narrow / Wide hybrid** — Narrow on auto manufacturing alone; potentially Wide if FSD/robotaxi monetizes at scale.

**Sources**:
- **Charging network**: Supercharger is the de facto North American DC fast-charging standard (NACS adopted by Ford, GM, Rivian, Hyundai, etc.) — true infrastructure moat
- **Manufacturing scale**: 6 vehicle factories globally; lowest unit cost in EV space
- **Vertical integration**: Cells (4680), software (FSD stack), powertrain, battery
- **FSD data flywheel**: ~7B+ miles of FSD data; Dojo training compute; potential winner-take-most autonomy moat
- **Brand**: Tesla = EV in U.S. consumer mind despite eroding NPS post-2024
- **Energy business**: Megapack pricing power; ~$15B+ annual run-rate at high-20s% margin

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Auto demand stagnating: deliveries ~1.79M in 2024, ~1.8M in 2025, 358K Q1 2026 (annualized ~1.4M *if* Q1 is run-rate, though seasonality favors H2)
- **Inventory build of 50K+ units in Q1 2026** signals demand softness vs. production
- BYD has surpassed Tesla in global EV unit sales; legacy OEMs (GM, Ford, Hyundai/Kia) closing technology gap
- Chinese EV penetration capped by tariffs/policy; international Tesla growth depends on Europe (Berlin) and emerging markets
- FSD / robotaxi value is *projected*; current revenue contribution from autonomy is ~$0.5B-1B (FSD purchases + subscriptions); robotaxi is sub-scale
- Musk political alignment in 2024–2025 cycle hurt brand globally (especially Europe)
- Key-person risk: Musk is non-substitutable on AI/autonomy + has ~6 other major commitments (xAI, SpaceX, X, Neuralink, Boring Co, $TRUMP era post)

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Is Tesla a Toyota-margin auto company with optional AI/robotaxi upside that markets are *over*-pricing at $376 / ~$1.4T market cap — or is the FSD/robotaxi/Optimus optionality real enough that today's price is *underpaying* for a winner-take-most autonomy and humanoid-robot platform?

The bull case requires (a) FSD reaching unsupervised L4 in 12+ states by year-end 2026, (b) Cybercab production scaling from 0 to 100K+ units in 2027, (c) Optimus shipping in low-volume commercial form by 2027 — at which point Tesla would re-rate as a tech/AI platform (>40× P/E) rather than an auto co (~10× P/E). The bear case is that FSD remains L3 supervised (lawsuit / regulatory drag), Cybercab is a Cybertruck-style modest success, and auto margins compress further as competition arrives — pushing the multiple back toward the auto peer set.

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Eberhard/Tarpenning 2003; Musk became CEO 2008)**: Original mission was to accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy. The "secret master plan" (2006) was: (1) build an expensive low-volume car (Roadster) to fund (2) a less-expensive medium-volume car (Model S/X) to fund (3) an affordable mass-market car (Model 3/Y) plus solar/storage. Master Plan Part 3 (2023) added the Cybercab + Optimus + dramatic vertical integration of energy.

**Today**: Tesla is the most valuable auto company globally. ~80% of revenue from auto, ~10% from energy (fastest-growing), ~5% services, ~5% other. The market cap (~$1.4T) is *not* justified by the auto P&L alone; it embeds substantial autonomy / robotaxi / Optimus / energy optionality.

**Pivotal question**: Does FSD reach unsupervised L4 across most U.S. metros within 18 months (justifying the platform multiple) or does it remain a supervised L3 product with regulatory drag (forcing a re-rating to ~12–15× auto-peer P/E, implying ~$140 stock)?
