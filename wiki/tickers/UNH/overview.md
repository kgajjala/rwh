## Business Description

UnitedHealth Group is the largest U.S. health insurance and healthcare services company
by revenue ($447.6B in FY2025). Operates two primary segments:

**UnitedHealthcare** (~$344.9B revenue, FY2025):
- The insurance/managed care arm: employer-sponsored commercial plans, individual
  plans, Medicare Advantage (MA), Medicaid, and global plans
- 49.8 million medical members at year-end 2025
- Medicare Advantage: ~$430B total MA market, UHC is the #1 MA provider
- MA is the primary source of 2025 margin pressure

**Optum** (~$270.6B revenue FY2025, partially intersegment):
- **Optum Health**: value-based care clinics, employed physicians, surgical centers
  — earnings collapsed from ~$7.9B adj. in 2024 to ~$2.3B adj. in 2025
- **Optum Insight**: healthcare data analytics, consulting, Change Healthcare
  (cyberattack in 2024 continues to reverberate)
- **Optum Rx**: PBM (pharmacy benefits manager), #2 by script volume

The vertical integration thesis — insurer + PBM + care delivery + analytics — is what
differentiates UNH from pure insurers. In 2025, this thesis faced its hardest test.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **Is the 2025 margin collapse (MLR 89.1%, CEO resignation, suspended guidance,
> Optum Health implosion) a recoverable operating stumble — or the beginning of
> structural unraveling of the vertical integration thesis under regulatory and
> political scrutiny?**

Bull: cyclical Medicare Advantage cost surge + value-based care ramp normalizes MLR to
86–87% by 2026–2027; returning CEO Stephen Hemsley is the architect of UNH's prior decade;
$277 stock is a 3-year low with P/E ~13x on normalized earnings. Bear: DOJ antitrust
investigation into vertical integration, political heat post-Thompson assassination, Optum
Health structural losses, 1.3M MA member exits in 2026, MLR stays elevated.

## Moat Rating

- **Rating**: Wide
- **Sources**:
  - Scale: #1 or #2 in virtually every insurance market — cost advantages in underwriting
  - Switching costs: employer plan changes are expensive (2–3 year cycle inertia)
  - Data advantages: 50M+ member claims, Rx, lab, clinical data = proprietary actuarial edge
  - Network effects: Optum's physician/PBM network attracts more members → more data
  - Regulatory moat: ERISA compliance, state insurance licensure, PBM relationships
- **Pressure**:
  - DOJ antitrust investigation (vertical integration)
  - CMS Medicare Advantage rate-setting is annual regulatory risk
  - Change Healthcare cyberattack fallout still reverberating
  - Political/reputational exposure (CEO Brian Thompson assassination, Dec 2024)
