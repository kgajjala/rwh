## Business Overview

Wingstop Inc. (NASDAQ: WING) is a high-growth, **98% franchised** chicken-wing-and-tender QSR concept founded in Garland, TX in 1994; IPO'd 2015. As of FY2025 year-end (Dec 27, 2025): **3,056 system locations across 47 U.S. states and 18 international countries / U.S. territories** — 2,586 domestic, 470 international. Wingstop earns three revenue streams from a system that generated **$5.3B in system-wide sales (+12.1% YoY) in FY2025**: (1) royalties of 6% of franchisee gross sales, (2) advertising-fund contributions of 5.5% (raised from 4% in 2025), (3) franchise development fees on each new opening. ~80% of orders flow through digital channels — structurally higher digital mix than peer QSR.

Reported FY2025 revenue of **$696.9M (+11.4%)** is the royalty/fee line, not system-wide sales. Adjusted EBITDA of **$244.2M (+15.2%; ~35% margin)** demonstrates the operating leverage of the asset-light model. **Net income of $174.3M (+60.3%)** was inflated by a one-time gain on the sale of an investment; **adjusted EPS of $4.08 (vs. $3.75 prior year, +9%)** is the cleaner profitability signal. The defining concern entering 2026: **first domestic same-store sales decline in 22 years (–3.3% FY2025; Q4 2025 –5.8%)**, which has driven a –51% drawdown from the $388 52-week high.

## Section 3 — Competitive Moat & Landscape

**Verdict: Narrow — and arguably widening on the AUV + digital + Smart Kitchen leg.** Brand and franchise economics are real but shallower than wide-moat franchisors like McDonald's. AUV leadership ($2.0M domestic — best-in-class among emerging QSR concepts) is the most defensible piece.

### Moat Sources & Vulnerabilities

| Source | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **AUV leadership** | Strong | $2.0M domestic AUV; $2.5M corporate AUV — top-tier among multi-unit QSR concepts; sustains franchisee demand for new units |
| Brand recognition (wings) | Moderate | Top-3 unprompted awareness in chicken-wings category |
| Franchisee switching costs | Moderate | $390K–$887K invested per unit; brand training; digital integration |
| Digital flywheel (~80% mix) | Moderate–Strong | Proprietary data layer; ~60M-user database (+20% YoY); structurally higher than QSR avg of 20–30% |
| **Smart Kitchen technology** | Moderate (new in FY25) | Fully deployed across **all domestic restaurants** in 2025; 15% YoY delivery-time reductions; speed-of-service to <10 min in ~50% of stores |
| Operational simplicity | Moderate | Lowest-labor QSR format; ~17% franchisee net margin; ~30–40% cash-on-cash — sustains demand for new openings |

**Vulnerabilities**:
- No consumer switching costs — multi-homing trivial; pivot to BWW, Cane's, Zaxby's, McDonald's value menu is frictionless
- No proprietary recipe IP
- Wing-input commodity volatility hits franchisee COGS (avian flu cycles)
- McDonald's / BK value campaigns directly pressure value-seeking QSR consumers — visible in FY25's –3.3% SSS print
- International execution at scale unproven — early-market AUVs below domestic
- Multiple-compression history: 40–60× peak EV/EBITDA was justified by 17.5% / 5.6% SSS in 2023 / 2024 — premium evaporated within 12 months when SSS turned

**How WING's moat differs**:
- **AUV-per-square-foot leadership**: WING units are typically <2,000 sq ft (vs. Cane's ~3,500 sq ft) yet generate $2.0M AUV. Real-estate efficiency is the structural advantage that sustains 19% unit growth without market saturation in dense markets.
- **80% digital order mix** is structurally above peer (BWLD <40%, Cane's <30%) — the 60M-user database is a *proprietary* customer asset that competitors cannot replicate without comparable digital pivot history.
- **Royalty-model insulation**: Unlike vertically-integrated chicken concepts (Cane's, BWW), WING earns 6% gross royalties insulated from franchisee margin pressure. When wing-input costs spike, franchisees absorb COGS pressure — WING's revenue line is unaffected.
- **International runway**: WING's 470 international units = ~15% of system. Cane's, Chick-fil-A, BWW all have <5% international. WING's intl-first roadmap (UK, Saudi, Australia, India 2026) is the asymmetric long-duration moat extension.

**Tail-risk peer**: **Raising Cane's** — well-capitalized (private, ~$5B+ revenue), aggressive store-build, single-protein simplicity model directly mimics WING's playbook. If Cane's moves into franchising at scale (currently ~95% company-owned), the competitive dynamics shift materially. *[Watch]*

**What genuinely changed**: The FY2025 10-K (filed Feb 18, 2026) is the first WING annual filing where consumer-discretionary trade-down language and same-store-sales-decline risk move from generic to specifically quantified. The chicken-supply / commodity risk language has been consistent across all five years; tariff and geopolitical-supply-chain language was added in FY2024 and intensified in FY2025 (consistent with broader QSR sector). AI / digital-disruption risk language has been *less* prominent than peers — WING's framing is that ~80% digital mix is a moat, not a vulnerability. **Implication for thesis**: management views the SSS reset as cyclical (recoverable via Smart Kitchen / loyalty / advertising) rather than structural (saturation) — and this framing is explicit in FY25 MD&A. The Q1 2026 print is the test of whether that framing is correct.

- **Regulatory**: No active material regulatory threat. Franchise-disclosure regulation (FDD) is stable. Chicken-supply tariffs intensified in FY24/FY25 but did not pass through to franchisee COGS materially.
