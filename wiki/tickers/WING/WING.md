## Business Overview

Wingstop Inc. (NASDAQ: WING) is a high-growth, asset-light franchise restaurant company focused exclusively on cooked-to-order chicken wings and tenders. Founded in 1994 in Garland, TX; IPO'd in 2015. As of end-2025: **3,056 system locations** in 47 U.S. states and 15 countries — 98% franchised. Wingstop earns royalties (6% of franchisee gross sales), advertising fund contributions (5.5% of gross sales, raised in 2025), and franchise fees on every transaction across the system. Reported FY2025 revenue of $697M is the royalty/fee line, not system-wide sales ($5.3B).

The brand is laser-focused: wings, fries, simple menu, with ~80% of orders coming through digital/delivery channels — a structurally higher digital mix than peer QSR chains. Recent international expansion (Australia, Saudi Arabia, Netherlands new in 2025) is the next growth vector. The defining concern entering 2026: **first domestic same-store sales (SSS) decline in 22 years (–3.3% in FY2025)**, which has driven a –51% drawdown from the 52-week high of $388.14.

## Moat Assessment

**Narrow** — brand and franchise economics are real but shallow vs. broader QSR competition.

**Sources**:
- **Brand recognition**: Wingstop is synonymous with wings in the U.S. — strong unprompted awareness in the wings QSR category; ~20 years of consistent brand-building
- **Franchisee switching costs**: Franchisees have $390K–$887K invested per unit, brand training, and digital ecosystem integration. Switching to a competing brand requires writing off that investment.
- **Digital flywheel**: ~80% digital order mix (vs. 20–30% for most QSR chains) creates a proprietary customer data asset for personalization, retention, and media targeting
- **Operational simplicity**: Single-protein focus = lower labor costs = higher franchisee margins (~17% net margin, ~30–40% cash-on-cash) = sustained franchisee demand for new units

**Vulnerabilities**:
- No consumer switching costs — a hungry customer can pivot to BWW, Raising Cane's, Zaxby's, or McDonald's value menu
- No proprietary recipe protection; chicken wing prices are volatile commodity inputs
- McDonald's / BK value campaigns directly compete for budget-conscious QSR consumers — visible in FY2025's –3.3% SSS print
- International execution unproven at scale; AUVs in newer markets are below domestic
- Multiple compression history: prior 40–60× EV/EBITDA premium was justified by 17.5%/5.6% SSS in 2023/2024 — that premium evaporated rapidly when SSS turned negative

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (1994)**: Chicken wings, ordered while watching sports, are a culturally resonant American comfort food. Wingstop's insight was operational simplicity: sell ONLY wings and tenders, prep-cooked and sauced to order, minimal labor. The result: a restaurant with unusually strong unit economics for an uncommonly simple menu. The digital-first pivot (2015–2020) transformed it from a neighborhood wing shop into a delivery-first, data-rich franchise platform.

**Today**: 3,056 units in 47 U.S. states and 15 countries, 98% franchised. Wingstop earns royalties (6%) and advertising fund contributions (5.5%) on every transaction. The strategic vision is to become a top-10 global restaurant brand by unit count; targets 7,000+ global units long-term.

**Pivotal question**: Is the FY2025 –3.3% domestic SSS print transitory (consumer value-seeking + chicken price inflation that will normalize) or structural (saturation + permanent QSR value competition)? If the former, $189 is undervalued; if the latter, the franchise is still a growth story but no longer deserves a premium multiple.

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Consumer discretionary / QSR value sensitivity**: Wingstop's $25–30 average check sits in the mid-range of QSR — vulnerable to consumers trading to McDonald's $5 value meals during periods of consumer stress. The 2025 SSS decline coincides with elevated housing + food cost stress on lower-income consumers.

**Chicken wing commodity prices**: Wing prices are driven by avian flu cycles and demand spikes (Super Bowl, sports events). When wing prices spike, franchisees face COGS pressure; menu price increases drive volume loss in value-sensitive environments — this is exactly the 2025 dynamic.

**Delivery ecosystem**: DoorDash/Uber Eats commissions (~25–30%) are a structural COGS headwind for franchisees; any reduction improves unit economics. WING's digital-first model is both a risk (DoorDash dependency) and an opportunity (own-channel at lower commission).

**TAM**: Global chicken QSR is underpenetrated relative to burger/pizza. The 7,000-unit long-term target is ~2.3× current footprint, with most growth international.

*Limits the signal*: If SSS is structurally negative, the unit-growth math doesn't fully offset AUV compression — and at 18× FY2026E EBITDA the multiple still embeds growth-stock pricing.

The SSS decline is the single most-covered topic in QSR sell-side. There is little informational asymmetry here — the data is fully public. The one residual edge is detailed franchisee-level economics (AUV, cash-on-cash), which suggest the system is healthier than the headline SSS implies — but this is a marginal informational advantage, not a primary one.
