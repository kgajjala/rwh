## Business Description

Wingstop Inc. is a high-growth, asset-light franchise restaurant company focused
exclusively on cooked-to-order chicken wings and thighs. Founded in 1994 in Garland, TX;
IPO'd in 2015. As of end-2025: **3,056 locations** in 47 U.S. states and 15 countries —
98% franchised.

**Business model**: Wingstop does NOT operate restaurants at scale. Revenue streams:
1. **Royalties** — 6% of franchisee gross sales (core income)
2. **Advertising fund contributions** — 5.5% of gross sales (raised in 2025)
3. **Franchise fees** — one-time fees for new unit openings (~$20K/unit)
4. **Company-owned stores** — ~60 units for testing/data purposes

Reported revenue ($697M in FY2025) is the royalty/fee line, not system-wide sales
($5.3B). The royalty stream is pure-margin, capital-light cash flow that scales with
unit count × SSS.

The brand is laser-focused: wings, fries, simple menu. ~80% digital/delivery mix. The
critical concern entering 2026: **first domestic SSS decline in 22 years** (–3.3% in
FY2025).

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **Is the 2025 SSS decline (–3.3%) a temporary reset from value-seeking consumer
> behavior, or the first signal of brand/concept saturation that caps the long-term
> unit and earnings compounding story?**

Bull: –3.3% SSS is transitory noise vs. a 19% unit growth flywheel; domestic AUV of
~$2.0M and franchisee margins remain healthy; 2026 guidance is flat to low-single digit
SSS recovery; international (Australia, Saudi, Netherlands) is a multi-decade TAM.
Bear: SSS is structural (McDonald's value competition, consumer fatigue with $15 wings);
AUV compression at 3,000+ units signals saturation; at 21x EBITDA, any SSS miss is
severely punished; the stock is 61% off its peak.

## Moat Rating

- **Rating**: Narrow
- **Sources**:
  - Brand recognition in chicken-centric QSR niche; top-3 consumer awareness in wings
  - Franchisee switching costs: proven unit economics, brand investment, training system
  - Digital flywheel: ~80% digital order mix drives customer data and repeat purchase
  - Simple menu = operational simplicity = lower labor costs vs. full-service QSR
- **Pressure**:
  - Low absolute consumer switching costs (QSR is highly competitive)
  - No proprietary recipe protection; chicken wing prices are commodity (COGS volatility)
  - International execution unproven at scale
