## Business Overview

Zillow Group (NASDAQ: ZG / Z) operates the largest U.S. real estate online marketplace, connecting buyers, sellers, renters, agents, and mortgage providers. Its core assets are **Zillow.com** (~221M monthly visitors in 2024 — the dominant U.S. real estate portal), **Trulia**, **StreetEasy** (NYC), **HotPads** (rentals), **Zillow Home Loans** (mortgage origination), and the agent-focused **Premier Agent** advertising program. FY2025 revenue was **$2.6B (+16%)**, with Q4 2025 at **$654M (+18%)** and Q4 Adjusted EBITDA of **$149M (23% margin, +260bps YoY)**. The company guided **Q1 2026 revenue to $700–710M (+18% YoY)** with EBITDA of $160–175M (24% margin) ([Zillow Q4 2025 press release](https://investors.zillowgroup.com/investors/news-and-events/news/), 2026-02-10).

The company has restructured around three segments — **For Sale**, **Mortgages**, **Rentals** — as it transitions from pure-play marketplace to a "super app" combining listings + tours + financing + agent matching. Mortgages grew 39% YoY in Q4 2025 (purchase loan origination volume +67% to $1.5B). Rentals grew 45% YoY (multifamily +63%).

## Moat Assessment

**Wide** (consumer side) / **Narrow** (agent side) — the Zillow.com traffic flywheel is dominant; the agent-monetization moat is contested.

**Sources**:
- **Consumer demand aggregation**: Zillow.com is the default U.S. real-estate search experience — 221M monthly visits, 4× nearest portal
- **Brand**: "Zillow" is the verb for property search
- **Listing comprehensiveness**: Multi-MLS data + agent-supplied + off-market + Zestimate

**Vulnerabilities**:

- Housing turnover at multi-decade lows; revenue is exposed to existing-home-sales volume
- Government / DOJ scrutiny over portal practices (Jacobin / housing-affordability narrative)

## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Founding insight (Rich Barton + Lloyd Frink, 2006)**: Real estate transactions were opaque, with consumers blocked from listings and pricing data. A consumer-first portal that aggregated MLS + public records + algorithmic estimates (Zestimate) would shift power toward the consumer and create the largest real-estate audience — which agents would *pay* to access. The 2006 launch of Zestimate broke the data-opacity moat overnight.

**Today**: Zillow.com is the dominant U.S. real estate portal. The company has expanded into mortgage origination, rentals, agent CRM, and tours/showings. Rich Barton (Glassdoor / Expedia / Zillow) returned as CEO in 2019, then transitioned to Executive Chairman in 2024; Jeremy Wacksman is CEO since Nov 2024.

**Pivotal question**: At $45 with a $10B market cap and FY26E adj. EBITDA likely in the $750M–$850M range, ZG trades at ~12× EV/EBITDA. Is this a deep-cycle entry into a marketplace compounder, or a structurally challenged portal facing Rocket-Redfin disintermediation?

## Section 9 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**U.S. residential real estate TAM**: ~$100B in transaction commissions + ancillary services. Online portals capture <5% — vast monetization runway if mortgage + ancillary attach scales.

**Housing turnover**: Existing-home sales at ~4M annualized — multi-decade lows due to lock-in (homeowners with sub-3% mortgages won't sell into 6.5%+ rates). Normalization to 5M+ would lift ZG revenue meaningfully without share gain.

**Rates**: Single biggest macro variable. 50bp cut would unlock turnover.

**Regulatory**: NAR commission settlement (March 2024) implemented in 2024; long-term effects on Premier Agent unit economics still resolving.

**Competitive**: Rocket-Redfin is the most credible vertically-integrated competitor in ZG's history. Marketing spend asymmetry is real.
