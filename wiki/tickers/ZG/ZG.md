## Business Overview

Zillow Group operates the largest U.S. residential-real-estate consumer marketplace, anchored by **Zillow.com** (~221M monthly visitors — 4× the nearest portal) plus **Trulia**, **StreetEasy** (NYC), and **HotPads**. The business is moving from pure-play listings portal → integrated **housing super-app** combining (a) listings search, (b) **Showcase** premium listing tier (**4.3% of new listings Q1 2026**, up from 1.7% in early 2024), (c) agent matching via **Premier Agent** (~30,000+ paying agents), (d) tour scheduling via the **ShowingTime** stack, (e) **Zillow Home Loans** mortgage origination (purchase volume $1.5B in Q1 2026, +96% YoY), and (f) **Zillow Rentals** with **2.7M average monthly active listings** — highest in category — and 76,000 multifamily properties (+38% YoY).

FY2025 results crystallized the super-app thesis: **Total revenue $2.58B (+16%)**, **Adj EBITDA $622M (24% margin, +200bps)**, **FCF $420M (+36%)**, GAAP NI **$23M** (first full-year profit). Segment FY25: **Residential $1.70B (+7%)**, **Mortgages $199M (+37%)**, **Rentals $630M (+39%)**, **Other $50M (+14%)**. Q1 2026 extended the streak: $708M (+18%), Adj EBITDA $182M (26% margin — beat), GAAP NI $46M. Q1 2026 buyback: **13.5M shares for $626M** — the most aggressive single-quarter repurchase in company history, reducing cash to **$788M** ([Q4 2025 PR](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/zillow-group-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025-financial-results-302684089.html), [Q1 2026 PR](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/zillow-group-reports-first-quarter-2026-financial-results-302764351.html), [Q1 2026 Shareholder Letter](https://s24.q4cdn.com/723050407/files/doc_earnings/2026/q1/presentation/Zillow-1Q26-Shareholders-Letter.pdf)).

CEO **Jeremy Wacksman** (since Nov 2024, internal promotion from COO); founder **Rich Barton** is Co-Executive Chairman; CFO **Jeremy Hofmann**.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> Does Zillow's super-app monetization (Mortgages + Rentals + Showcase) durably compound through a multi-year housing-cycle trough — and is the Rocket-Redfin combination + the now-proceeding FTC trial over the $100M rental-syndication deal correctly priced into the **–56% drawdown from $93** highs?

The pivot: Q1 2026 printed $708M (+18%), 26% EBITDA margin, GAAP NI $46M, and Showcase at 4.3% penetration — all ahead of the guide — while management explicitly said it is "not planning for the housing market to improve" in 2026. If the super-app continues compounding independent of housing-cycle help, the operating leverage when turnover normalizes (5M+) is the uncalled option. The FTC trial (accelerated timeline, H1 legal headwind) is the near-term overhang that will define whether the Rentals moat is durable.

## Section 3 — Competitive Moat & Landscape

**Verdict: Wide (consumer side) / Narrow (agent side)** — the consumer traffic flywheel is uncontested at scale; the agent monetization moat is contested by Rocket-Redfin and CoStar/Homes.com.

### Sources

| Source | Strength | Evidence |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Consumer traffic dominance | **Strong** | 221M monthly visitors; **4× nearest portal**; Zestimate is the default U.S. home-value reference |
| Brand ("Zillow" as a verb) | **Strong** | Top-of-funnel default in U.S. consumer real-estate search |
| Listing comprehensiveness | **Strong** | Multi-MLS + agent-supplied + off-market + new construction; Showcase deepens broker direct relationships |
| Integrated super-app stack | **Moderate-Growing** | Search + tour (ShowingTime) + finance (ZHL) + agent matching (Premier Agent + Followup Boss CRM); end-to-end stack no peer replicates |
| Premier Agent network | **Moderate** | 30,000+ paying agents; lock-in via lead conversion analytics; pressured by NAR settlement |
| Rentals share | **Moderate-Growing** | **63% share of rental listings in 2025** (up from 54%) following $100M Redfin syndication deal |

### Vulnerabilities

- **Rocket-Redfin** combination (closed 2025) — vertically integrated finance-plus-portal alternative. Mitigant: ZG executed a **$100M rental syndication deal with Redfin** that shifted Redfin from full competitor to *partner* in rentals (FTC is challenging this — see §6).
- **CoStar/Homes.com $1B+ marketing assault** — sustained spend to dislodge Zillow's #1 share. Visibility growing; share-shift evidence not yet decisive.
- **NAR commission settlement** aftermath — long-term Premier Agent ARPU pressure; ZG's read so far is that it drives consumers *deeper into the funnel* (more reliance on portal-mediated agent matching), not toward disintermediation.
- **DOJ / FTC scrutiny** — multiple active fronts (FTC rental-syndication suit, state AG antitrust suits) — see §6.

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Market position / moat | Threat vector to ZG |
|------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Realtor.com (NWS)** | #2 U.S. residential portal; MLS-funded promotion | Steady #2 share; less aggressive marketing escalation than CSGP |
| **Homes.com (CSGP)** | CoStar-backed challenger; #3 U.S. consumer real-estate portal | **$1B+ marketing budget** to attack Zillow's traffic share; well-capitalized; sustained-investment risk *not yet priced in* |
| **Redfin (now part of RKT)** | Brokerage + portal; combined with Rocket Mortgage post-2025 close | Hybrid threat: stronger consumer-finance bundle; *partial mitigant* via $100M Zillow rentals syndication deal — Redfin pulled out of rental-listings competition |
| **Apartments.com (CSGP)** | Multifamily rental category leader (commercial/landlord side) | Direct rentals competitor; CSGP's deeper landlord relationships |
| **Rocket Mortgage (RKT)** | #1 U.S. mortgage lender by volume; ~15,000 LOs vs. ZG ~330 | Direct mortgage-attach competitor; bigger origination scale but ZG owns the consumer top-of-funnel |
| **Better.com / UWMC / GHC / banks** | Mortgage origination peers | Standard mortgage-rate competition |
| **Angi (ANGI) / Thumbtack / Porch (PRCH)** | Adjacent home-services marketplaces | Adjacent — not direct head-to-head; potential expansion-path overlap |

**How ZG's moat differs from peers**:
- Only player with a *combined* top-of-funnel consumer brand + integrated tours + integrated finance + Premier Agent network. RKT has finance scale but lacks consumer search. CSGP has B2B real-estate-data scale but lacks Zillow's consumer brand. Realtor.com has #2 portal but no super-app.
- **Evidence the moat is durable**: (a) traffic share holding 4× nearest portal *despite* CSGP's marketing escalation, (b) Mortgages purchase volume +67% Q4 — cross-sell is *converting*, not just funneling, (c) Rentals share rose to 63% — share-grab in motion.

**Tail-risk read**: CSGP is the well-capitalized, currently-losing challenger that could escalate further. The Rocket-Redfin partnership was *neutralized* by the rental-syndication deal — the FTC suit is the live wildcard there.

## Section 7 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

- **U.S. residential transactions TAM**: ~$100B in commissions + ancillary services. Online portals capture <5% of the value pool — multi-decade monetization runway if mortgage / rental / Showcase attach scales.

- **Rentals macro**: Multifamily new-supply digestion underway; ZG's listing-share gains have offset broader category softness.
- **Competitive intensity**: Rocket-Redfin (now via $100M syndication deal — mostly partner, partial competitor) + CSGP/Homes.com is the most credible competitive setup ZG has faced in its history.
